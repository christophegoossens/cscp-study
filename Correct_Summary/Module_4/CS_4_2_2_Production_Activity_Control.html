Overview

Production Activity Control (PAC) = detailed scheduling and control of shop floor resources after MRP generates planned orders.

The ASCM Supply Chain Dictionary defines a parent item as “the item produced from one or more components,” while a component is “the raw material, part, or subassembly that goes into a higher-level assembly, compound, or other item. This term may also include packaging materials for finished items.”
  • PAC links MRP plan to execution: sequencing, dispatching, monitoring, and exception resolution
  • System nervousness: excessive MRP changes; may signal underlying quality or shop floor issues — find root causes
Lot-for-lot scheduling depends upon the following essential information:
  • Lot-for-lot inputs needed: gross delivery required, projected on-hand, scheduled receipts, net requirements, lead time
  • Kanban: visual pull signal (card/bin/light/empty space) triggering material delivery in JIT environment
  • Good communication key in JIT: schedules shared widely along supply chain to improve supplier responsiveness
  • Process map: flowchart of all process steps including duration, resources, and responsible parties
Pareto analysis, which shows the frequency of items in a data set, is based on Pareto’s law, defined in the ASCM Supply Chain Dictionary as “a concept developed by Vilfredo Pareto, an Italian economist, that states that a small percentage of a group accounts for the largest fraction of its impact or value.” Closely associated with Pareto’s law is 80-20, a principle that “suggests that most effects come from relatively few causes; that is, 80 percent of the effects (or sales or costs) come from 20 percent of the possible causes (or items)” (ASCM Supply Chain Dictionary).
  • Pareto / 80-20: 80% of effects come from 20% of causes; focus improvement on the vital few
  • Matrix data analysis chart: plots product lines against two variables to compare and differentiate features
Setup time reduction has a major impact on production costs and product variety. This practice aims to reduce waste (time and material) in the setup process. It encompasses improved processes as well as having employees being responsible for their own setups. The ASCM Supply Chain Dictionary defines setup time as
  • Setup time reduction: strategic goal first (time, cost, quality, or resources); 5-step process
  • Not all suppliers want JIT partnerships — may resist long-term contracts, frequent changes, small shipments
  • TOC: speeding before constraint builds WIP, not throughput; speeding after = idle wait time
  • Max throughput of system = rate of the constraint

Question 1
Production Activity Control (PAC) is responsible for:
A.Setting 12-month production plans
B.Executing and controlling production orders on the shop floor in the short term
C.Purchasing raw materials
D.Selecting suppliers
PAC translates planned production orders into work centre assignments, monitors progress, and addresses deviations in real time.
Question 2
A dispatch list in shop floor control is:
A.A list of trucks available
B.A prioritized list of jobs to be processed at a work centre
C.A supplier delivery schedule
D.A quality inspection checklist
A dispatch list ranks open production orders at each work centre by priority, guiding operators on which job to process next.
Question 3
Critical Ratio (CR) = (Due Date - Today) / (Remaining Lead Time). CR < 1 means:
A.Ahead of schedule
B.Behind schedule — highest priority
C.On schedule — no action needed
D.Cancelled order
CR < 1 means the job is behind schedule (more work remaining than time available). Lower CR = higher priority in dispatch.
Question 4
Shortest Processing Time (SPT) scheduling minimizes:
A.Total defects
B.Average flow time and makespan
C.Total cost
D.Number of setups
SPT sequences jobs to minimize average time spent in the system — a common production scheduling heuristic.
