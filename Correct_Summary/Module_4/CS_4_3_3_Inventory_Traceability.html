Overview

Inventory accuracy and traceability are essential for effective supply chain operations. Without accurate inventory records, MRP and other planning systems produce unreliable results. Traceability supports quality control, regulatory compliance, and recall management.

ASCM Definitions

📖 ASCM Definition — Lot Control:

A set of procedures for tracking and controlling inventory in specific lots or batches, typically from receipt through final disposition. Lot numbers link specific units to their manufacturing or receipt history for quality and recall purposes.

📖 ASCM Definition — Cycle Counting:

An inventory accuracy audit technique where inventory is counted on a cyclic schedule rather than once a year. A cycle count program counts a subset of inventory regularly throughout the year, with high-value or high-movement items counted more frequently.

📖 ASCM Definition — First In, First Out (FIFO):

A method of inventory valuation and rotation where the oldest items (first received) are consumed or sold first. FIFO prevents perishables from expiring and maintains freshness.

Inventory Record Accuracy

Inventory record accuracy (IRA) measures how closely inventory records match actual physical inventory. High IRA (typically ≥95%) is required for MRP to function effectively. Errors stem from:

  • Transactions not recorded (receipts, issues, scrap, returns)
  • Wrong quantity or part number recorded
  • Unauthorized withdrawals
  • Damage or theft not reported

Cycle Counting vs. Physical Inventory

💡 Key Point:

Cycle counting counts a subset of items daily/weekly throughout the year — less disruption to operations, catches errors sooner, allows root cause analysis. Physical inventory counts ALL items at once (typically annually) — requires shutting down operations and is labor-intensive. Modern best practice favors cycle counting supplemented by exception-based investigation.

Lot Traceability and Serialization

  • Lot/batch traceability: Tracking all units sharing the same production batch. Enables targeted product recalls (only affected lot, not all production).
  • Serial number traceability: Tracking individual units by unique serial number. Required for high-value, safety-critical, or warranty-tracked items.
  • Forward traceability: From raw material/lot → to end customer (who received what)
  • Backward traceability: From problem/recall → back to supplier/component source

Inventory Disposition

Disposition determines what happens to excess, obsolete, or defective inventory:

  • Return to vendor: Defective or surplus sent back to supplier
  • Salvage/liquidation: Sold at reduced value
  • Donation: Charitable contribution
  • Rework/remanufacture: Process to restore to usable condition
  • Scrap/disposal: Discarded (last resort due to cost)

DEFINITION
What is lot control in inventory management?
ANSWER
A set of procedures for tracking inventory in specific lots or batches from receipt through disposition. Lot numbers link specific units to their manufacturing history, enabling targeted quality control and product recalls.
DEFINITION
What is cycle counting?
ANSWER
An inventory accuracy audit technique where a subset of inventory is counted on a cyclic schedule throughout the year. High-value (A) items are counted most frequently. Avoids annual full-shutdown physical inventories.
DEFINITION
What is FIFO (First In, First Out)?
ANSWER
A method of inventory management where the oldest items (first received) are consumed or issued first. Prevents perishables from expiring and maintains freshness. Standard rotation method for food, pharma, and dated goods.
DEFINITION
What is LIFO (Last In, First Out)?
ANSWER
An inventory accounting method where the most recently received items are consumed first. Primarily a financial accounting method (not a physical handling method) — less common in practice, banned under IFRS.
CONCEPT
What is Inventory Record Accuracy (IRA) and why is it important?
ANSWER
IRA measures how closely inventory records match physical stock, expressed as a percentage. High IRA (≥95%) is critical for MRP to produce valid plans. Record errors lead to planning failures, excess inventory, and stockouts.
CONCEPT
What are the main causes of inventory record inaccuracy?
ANSWER
Unrecorded transactions (receipts, issues, returns, scrap); wrong quantities or part numbers; unauthorized withdrawals; theft or damage not reported; counting errors during receiving.
KEY DISTINCTION
What is the difference between cycle counting and physical inventory?
ANSWER
Cycle counting: counts subset of items continuously, less disruptive, catches errors faster, enables root cause analysis. Physical inventory: counts all items at once (annually), requires operational shutdown, labor-intensive. Cycle counting is modern best practice.
CONCEPT
How is ABC analysis applied to cycle counting frequency?
ANSWER
A items (highest value/activity) are counted most frequently (e.g., monthly or quarterly). B items are counted semi-annually. C items are counted annually. This ensures most critical inventory accuracy is maintained with least counting effort.
CONCEPT
What is forward vs. backward traceability?
ANSWER
Forward traceability: tracks from raw material/component lot → to end customer (who received what). Backward traceability: tracks from a defective product or recall → back to the specific supplier lot or component source.
CONCEPT
What is the difference between lot traceability and serial number traceability?
ANSWER
Lot traceability tracks groups of items sharing the same batch/production run. Serial number traceability tracks individual unique items. Serial tracking provides more precision but requires more data management overhead.
CONCEPT
What are the options for inventory disposition?
ANSWER
1) Return to vendor; 2) Rework/remanufacture; 3) Salvage/liquidation; 4) Donation; 5) Scrap/disposal. Disposition decisions balance recovery value against cost of handling each option.
CONCEPT
What is a product recall and how does lot traceability support it?
ANSWER
A product recall withdraws defective or unsafe products from the market. Lot traceability enables targeted recalls — only the affected lot is pulled, not the entire product line — minimizing recall scope and cost.
CONCEPT
What is a blind count in cycle counting?
ANSWER
A counting method where the counter is NOT shown the system quantity before counting. This prevents the counter from simply confirming the record rather than counting independently. Best practice for accuracy.
CONCEPT
What is inventory shrinkage?
ANSWER
The difference between recorded inventory and actual physical inventory due to theft, administrative error, damage, or other losses. Measured as a percentage of inventory value and monitored as a performance metric.
CONCEPT
What is a quarantine area in inventory management?
ANSWER
A designated storage location for goods that cannot be used until they are inspected, tested, or cleared. Prevents non-conforming material from entering the production or shipping process accidentally.
CONCEPT
What is the role of barcodes and RFID in inventory traceability?
ANSWER
Barcodes and RFID automate data capture during receiving, storage, picking, and shipping — reducing manual errors and increasing transaction speed. RFID enables bulk counting without line-of-sight and real-time location tracking.
CONCEPT
What is dead stock (dead inventory)?
ANSWER
Items that have not moved for an extended period and are unlikely to sell. Dead stock represents tied-up capital and storage space. Disposition actions (liquidation, donation, scrap) are needed to free resources.
CONCEPT
What is a location code in warehouse inventory management?
ANSWER
A unique identifier for a specific storage location (aisle, bay, level, bin). Location codes enable precise tracking of where each item is stored, supporting efficient picking and accurate cycle counts.
CONCEPT
What is the ABC-XYZ inventory matrix?
ANSWER
Combines ABC (by value) with XYZ (by demand variability): X = stable demand, Y = variable demand, Z = highly erratic demand. AX items get tightest controls; CZ items may use simple rules or be made-to-order.
CONCEPT
What is the purpose of a wall-to-wall physical inventory count?
ANSWER
A complete count of all inventory at a point in time — typically at year-end or fiscal year-end — to reconcile records with actual stock, identify shrinkage, and satisfy financial reporting requirements.
