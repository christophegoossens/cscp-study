Overview

This section covers operational controls including scatter chart analysis and the relationship between changeovers, lead time, and lot sizing.

  • Scatter chart (scatter diagram / cross plot): shows relationship between two variables
  • X axis = independent variable; Y axis = dependent variable (changes in relation to x)
  • Direction and closeness of plotted points indicate correlation type and strength
  • Positive correlation: points trend upper-right; negative: upper-left; no correlation: scattered randomly
  • Changeover time reduction directly enables smaller lot sizes
  • Smaller lot sizes reduce WIP, shorten queues, and enable cellular manufacturing layouts
  • Traceability: ability to track inventory by lot, batch, or serial number through the supply chain

Question 1
FIFO (First In, First Out) assumes:
A.Most recently purchased inventory is sold first
B.Oldest inventory is sold first
C.All inventory has the same cost
D.Inventory is valued at market price
FIFO assumes the oldest stock is consumed first — important for perishable goods and accurate balance sheet valuation.
Question 2
Lot traceability means:
A.Tracking the financial cost of each batch
B.The ability to identify the origin, movement, and disposition of a specific production lot
C.Counting inventory by lot number
D.Scheduling production by lot size
Lot traceability records the history of each production batch from raw material through manufacturing to customer.
Question 3
Serial number tracking differs from lot tracking in that:
A.Serial tracking is cheaper
B.Serial numbers identify individual units; lot numbers identify batches
C.Lot tracking is only for food
D.Serial tracking requires blockchain
Serial number tracking identifies each individual unit; lot/batch tracking identifies groups of units produced under the same conditions.
Question 4
EU Regulation EC 178/2002 requires:
A.All companies to use ISO 9001
B.One-step-back one-step-forward traceability in food supply chains
C.Carbon footprint reporting
D.Financial audits of all food suppliers
EC 178/2002 legally requires traceability in EU food supply chains — enabling effective product recalls.
