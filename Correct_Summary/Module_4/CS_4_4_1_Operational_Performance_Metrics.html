Overview

Operational performance metrics link internal operations and inventory management to overall supply chain and financial performance. They enable managers to identify problems, benchmark performance, and drive continuous improvement.

Key Supply Chain Operational Metrics

Inventory Performance

📖 ASCM Definition — Inventory Turns (Inventory Turnover):

Annual Cost of Goods Sold / Average Inventory Value. A measure of how efficiently inventory is used. Higher turns indicate faster movement and better capital utilization.

📖 ASCM Definition — Days of Supply (Days on Hand):

Average Inventory / Average Daily Demand (or Daily COGS). Measures how many days of demand are covered by current inventory. Complements inventory turns.

📖 ASCM Definition — Fill Rate:

The fraction of orders (or demand) satisfied completely from available stock without backorders or lost sales. A high fill rate indicates strong inventory availability.

📖 ASCM Definition — Cash-to-Cash Cycle Time:

The time from when cash is invested in raw materials to when cash is received from customers. Cash-to-Cash = Days Inventory Outstanding + Days Sales Outstanding — Days Payable Outstanding. Shorter is better for working capital.

Delivery and Service Performance

  • On-Time Delivery (OTD): % of orders delivered by the promised/requested date
  • On-Time In-Full (OTIF): % of orders delivered on time AND in the full quantity ordered — stricter than OTD alone
  • Order Cycle Time: Time from customer order receipt to delivery
  • Perfect Order Fulfillment: % of orders delivered on time, in full, damage-free, with correct documentation

Production and Quality Metrics

  • Throughput: Rate of output per unit of time (units/hour, units/day)
  • Yield: % of output meeting quality standards without rework
  • First-pass yield: % of units passing quality inspection without any rework
  • Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE): Availability × Performance × Quality — comprehensive equipment productivity measure
  • Scrap rate: % of production discarded as defective

Financial Performance Metrics

  • Total Supply Chain Cost: All costs to plan, source, make, and deliver
  • Cost of Goods Sold (COGS): Direct material, labor, and overhead in produced items
  • Gross Margin: (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue
  • Return on Assets (ROA): Net Income / Total Assets — impacted by inventory levels
  • Working Capital: Current Assets - Current Liabilities — inventory and receivables are major components

💡 Key Point:

The SCOR (Supply Chain Operations Reference) model provides a comprehensive, standardized framework for measuring supply chain performance across five attributes: Reliability, Responsiveness, Agility, Cost, and Asset Management. Each attribute has defined metrics at Level 1 (strategic) and deeper levels.

DEFINITION
What is the formula for inventory turns?
ANSWER
Inventory Turns = Annual COGS / Average Inventory Value. Higher turns = faster-moving inventory = better capital efficiency. Low turns may indicate excess or slow-moving stock.
DEFINITION
What is Days of Supply (DOS)?
ANSWER
Days of Supply = Average Inventory / Average Daily Demand (or Daily COGS). Tells how many days of demand are currently covered. DOS = 365 / Inventory Turns.
DEFINITION
What is fill rate?
ANSWER
The fraction of demand satisfied completely from available stock. High fill rate = strong inventory availability. Can be measured by order, line, or unit. A key customer service metric.
DEFINITION
What is Cash-to-Cash (C2C) cycle time?
ANSWER
C2C = Days Inventory Outstanding + Days Sales Outstanding - Days Payable Outstanding. Measures working capital efficiency. Shorter C2C means less cash tied up in the supply chain.
DEFINITION
What is On-Time In-Full (OTIF)?
ANSWER
The percentage of orders delivered both on time AND in the complete quantity ordered. OTIF is stricter than on-time delivery alone and is a key retailer metric (e.g., Walmart fines for OTIF failures).
DEFINITION
What is Perfect Order Fulfillment?
ANSWER
The percentage of orders delivered on time, in full, damage-free, and with accurate documentation. A composite metric used in SCOR to measure reliability across all order fulfillment dimensions.
CONCEPT
What are the five SCOR performance attributes?
ANSWER
1) Reliability (perfect order); 2) Responsiveness (order fulfillment cycle time); 3) Agility (supply chain flexibility); 4) Cost (total SC cost, COGS); 5) Asset Management (inventory turns, C2C, ROA).
DEFINITION
What is Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE)?
ANSWER
OEE = Availability × Performance × Quality Rate. Measures how effectively equipment is used compared to its full potential. World-class OEE is generally considered ≥85%.
CONCEPT
What is Order Cycle Time?
ANSWER
The elapsed time from customer order receipt to product delivery at the customer's location. Also called Order-to-Delivery time or customer lead time. A key responsiveness metric.
CONCEPT
How does reducing inventory turns affect financial performance?
ANSWER
Lower inventory turns mean more cash tied up in inventory (higher working capital needs, higher holding costs). Higher turns free cash, reduce storage costs, and lower obsolescence risk — improving ROA and cash flow.
CONCEPT
What is Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)?
ANSWER
DSO = Accounts Receivable / (Annual Revenue / 365). Measures how long it takes to collect payment after a sale. Shorter DSO improves C2C cycle time and cash flow.
CONCEPT
What is Days Payable Outstanding (DPO)?
ANSWER
DPO = Accounts Payable / (Annual COGS / 365). Measures how long the company takes to pay suppliers. Longer DPO improves C2C by using supplier credit as working capital — but must be managed to preserve relationships.
CONCEPT
What is throughput in operations management?
ANSWER
The rate at which a system generates output (revenue or units) per unit of time. Theory of Constraints focuses on maximizing throughput at the bottleneck. Throughput = Revenue - Variable Costs.
CONCEPT
What is first-pass yield (FPY)?
ANSWER
The percentage of units that complete a production process without requiring any rework, repair, or rejection. FPY = Units meeting spec first time / Total units started. High FPY reduces hidden rework costs.
CONCEPT
What is the relationship between supply chain performance and financial performance?
ANSWER
Supply chain efficiency directly impacts financial KPIs: lower inventory → less working capital; faster cycle times → better revenue recognition; higher fill rates → less lost sales; lower COGS → better margins.
CONCEPT
What is total supply chain cost?
ANSWER
The sum of all costs to plan, source, make, deliver, and return products — including material costs, production, warehousing, transportation, inventory holding, and returns processing. A comprehensive SCOR Level 1 metric.
CONCEPT
What is Return on Assets (ROA) and how is it linked to inventory?
ANSWER
ROA = Net Income / Total Assets. High inventory levels inflate total assets without proportional income — reducing ROA. Lean inventory management directly improves ROA by freeing capital.
CONCEPT
What is a balanced scorecard approach to supply chain metrics?
ANSWER
Using metrics across multiple dimensions — financial, customer, internal process, and learning/growth — to get a balanced view of performance. Prevents over-optimizing one dimension (e.g., cost) at the expense of others (e.g., service).
CONCEPT
What is supply chain cost as a percentage of revenue?
ANSWER
A benchmark metric comparing total supply chain costs to annual revenue. Industry averages vary widely (1–5% for consumer goods to 10–20%+ for complex manufacturing). Used for competitive benchmarking.
CONCEPT
What is the difference between efficiency and effectiveness metrics?
ANSWER
Efficiency metrics measure how well resources are used (cost per unit, OEE, inventory turns). Effectiveness metrics measure whether goals are achieved (fill rate, OTIF, order cycle time). Best practice tracks both dimensions.

Question 1
OEE of 72% means:
A.28% of production is defective
B.Equipment is available 72% of the time
C.72% of scheduled time produces good product at the right speed
D.72% of jobs are on time
OEE = Availability x Performance x Quality. 72% OEE means only 72% of scheduled time produces good-quality output at target speed.
Question 2
Fill rate measures:
A.On-time delivery only
B.The proportion of customer orders fulfilled completely from available stock without backorder
C.Total inventory cost
D.Production yield
Fill rate = % of demand satisfied immediately from available inventory. High fill rate minimizes stockouts and backorders.
Question 3
On-Time Delivery (OTD) measures:
A.% of orders shipped on the promised/requested date
B.Average lead time
C.Inventory accuracy
D.Order fill rate
OTD = % of orders shipped or delivered on the promised date — a primary customer service metric.
Question 4
Throughput in the Theory of Constraints is:
A.The rate at which the system generates money through sales
B.Total cost of production
C.Inventory turns per year
D.Number of workers on the line
In TOC, throughput = rate at which the system generates revenue through sales.
