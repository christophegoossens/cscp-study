Overview

Operational performance management integrates planning (S&OP → MPS → MRP) with performance measurement and continuous improvement across four capacity-check levels.

image161.png
  • S&OP: aggregate supply/demand balance for product families; monthly production volumes
  • MPS: disaggregates S&OP into specific end-item quantities by week; drives MRP
  • Capacity checks: resource planning (S&OP level) → RCCP (MPS) → CRP (MRP) → capacity control (execution)

Increase velocity. Velocity refers to the time it takes to provide value to the customer. Velocity in supply chain management is defined in the ASCM Supply Chain Dictionary as “the relative speed of all transactions, collectively, within a supply chain community.” The definition goes on to state that “a maximum velocity is most desirable because it indicates higher asset turnover for stockholders and faster order-to-delivery response for customers.” Through the use of JIT and lean techniques, velocity produces products and services only as fast as customers want them.

Buffer (ASCM Supply Chain Dictionary): 1) A quantity of materials awaiting further processing. It can refer to raw materials, semifinished stores or hold points, or a work backlog that is purposely maintained behind a work center. 2) In the theory of constraints, buffers can be time or material and support throughput and/or due date performance.

Question 1
OEE of 72% means:
A.28% of production is defective
B.Equipment is available 72% of the time
C.72% of scheduled time produces good product at the right speed
D.72% of jobs are on time
OEE = Availability x Performance x Quality. 72% OEE means only 72% of scheduled time produces good-quality output at target speed.
Question 2
Fill rate measures:
A.On-time delivery only
B.The proportion of customer orders fulfilled completely from available stock without backorder
C.Total inventory cost
D.Production yield
Fill rate = % of demand satisfied immediately from available inventory. High fill rate minimizes stockouts and backorders.
Question 3
On-Time Delivery (OTD) measures:
A.% of orders shipped on the promised/requested date
B.Average lead time
C.Inventory accuracy
D.Order fill rate
OTD = % of orders shipped or delivered on the promised date — a primary customer service metric.
Question 4
Throughput in the Theory of Constraints is:
A.The rate at which the system generates money through sales
B.Total cost of production
C.Inventory turns per year
D.Number of workers on the line
In TOC, throughput = rate at which the system generates revenue through sales.
