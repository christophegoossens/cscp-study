Overview

Supply chain connectivity links trading partners through shared data and processes. Visibility provides real-time awareness of supply chain status — enabling proactive management and faster response to disruptions.

ASCM Definitions

📖 Definition:
Supply chain visibility: The ability to track the components of a product from the supplier's supplier to the customer's customer in real time. Includes upstream (supply) visibility and downstream (demand) visibility.
📖 Definition:
Real-time data: Data that is captured and made available for use immediately or nearly immediately after the event occurs. Enables live tracking of orders, shipments, inventory, and exceptions.
📖 Definition:
Control tower: A supply chain visibility platform that aggregates data from multiple systems (ERP, WMS, TMS, suppliers, carriers) and provides a unified view of supply chain status with exception alerting.

Connectivity Methods

💡 Key Point:
The key connectivity technologies in order of sophistication: 1) Manual data exchange (email, spreadsheets), 2) Electronic Data Interchange (EDI), 3) Application Programming Interfaces (APIs), 4) Cloud collaboration platforms, 5) IoT sensor networks.
  • EDI: structured document exchange (purchase orders, invoices, ship notices) — widely adopted but batch-oriented
  • APIs: real-time, event-driven data exchange between systems — the modern integration standard
  • Cloud platforms: shared supply chain platforms where all partners access the same data in real time
  • IoT sensors: direct data from physical assets (vehicles, containers, products) without human intervention
  • RFID: automated identification of tagged items at dock doors, distribution points, and store shelves

Visibility Benefits and Challenges

💡 Key Point:
End-to-end visibility enables: proactive exception management (before problems become crises), accurate available-to-promise, faster response to disruptions, better supplier accountability, and reduced safety stock needs.
  • Challenge: data standards vary across partners — EDI, APIs, and file formats differ by industry and company
  • Challenge: Tier 2/3 supplier visibility is difficult — most companies have limited visibility beyond Tier 1
  • Challenge: data latency — information may be delayed, batch-updated rather than real-time
  • Challenge: data quality — inaccurate or incomplete data undermines visibility value
📖 Definition:
Tier 1 supplier: A supplier that directly supplies components or materials to the manufacturer. A Tier 2 supplier supplies the Tier 1 supplier. Most companies have good visibility to Tier 1 but poor visibility to Tier 2 and beyond.

DEFINITION
What is supply chain visibility?
ANSWER
The ability to track the components of a product from supplier's supplier to customer's customer in real time — including upstream supply status and downstream demand and delivery status.
DEFINITION
What is a supply chain control tower?
ANSWER
A visibility platform aggregating data from multiple systems (ERP, WMS, TMS, carrier feeds, supplier portals) to provide a unified real-time view of supply chain status with exception alerting and root-cause analysis.
DEFINITION
What is real-time data and why is it important in supply chains?
ANSWER
Data captured and made available immediately or nearly immediately after the event occurs. Enables live tracking of shipments, inventory, orders, and exceptions — allowing proactive response rather than reactive firefighting.
CONCEPT
What are the five levels of supply chain connectivity in order of sophistication?
ANSWER
1) Manual (email/spreadsheets), 2) EDI (batch document exchange), 3) APIs (real-time integration), 4) Cloud collaboration platforms (shared real-time data), 5) IoT sensor networks (automated physical asset tracking).
CONCEPT
What is EDI and what are its strengths and limitations?
ANSWER
Electronic Data Interchange — structured electronic document exchange between trading partners. Strength: widely adopted standard for POs, invoices, ship notices. Limitation: batch-oriented (not real-time), requires mapping setup, rigid message formats.
DEFINITION
What is an API (Application Programming Interface) in supply chain integration?
ANSWER
A real-time, event-driven interface for system-to-system data exchange. More flexible and faster than EDI — enables live order status updates, inventory queries, and exception alerts between supply chain systems.
CONCEPT
What are the four key benefits of end-to-end supply chain visibility?
ANSWER
1) Proactive exception management, 2) Accurate available-to-promise to customers, 3) Faster disruption response, 4) Better supplier accountability and performance management.
CONCEPT
Why is Tier 2/3 supplier visibility challenging?
ANSWER
Most companies have contractual and data relationships only with Tier 1 suppliers. Tier 2/3 suppliers may not share data and may not have the IT infrastructure for digital connectivity. This creates 'blind spots' in supply chain risk management.
KEY DISTINCTION
What is the difference between Tier 1 and Tier 2 suppliers?
ANSWER
Tier 1: directly supplies the manufacturer (primary supplier relationship). Tier 2: supplies the Tier 1 supplier. Disruptions at Tier 2 affect Tier 1 supply, but most manufacturers lack visibility into Tier 2 operations.
CONCEPT
What are the main challenges to supply chain visibility implementation?
ANSWER
1) Different data standards across partners, 2) Limited Tier 2/3 visibility, 3) Data latency (batch vs. real-time), 4) Data quality issues, 5) Partner willingness to share proprietary data.
CONCEPT
What is IoT (Internet of Things) connectivity in the supply chain?
ANSWER
Physical sensors on vehicles, containers, products, and equipment that automatically transmit location, condition, and status data to supply chain systems without human intervention — enabling real-time track-and-trace.
DEFINITION
What is track-and-trace capability?
ANSWER
The ability to track the current location and status of shipments, orders, or products (tracking) and to trace the history of their movements and handling (tracing). Essential for customer service and regulatory compliance.
CONCEPT
What is an exception alert in a supply chain control tower?
ANSWER
An automated notification when a supply chain event deviates from the plan — e.g., a shipment is late, inventory falls below safety stock, or a supplier sends an early delivery. Allows planners to focus on problems rather than routine status.
KEY DISTINCTION
What is the difference between upstream and downstream visibility?
ANSWER
Upstream visibility: tracking materials and components from Tier 1/2/3 suppliers toward the manufacturer. Downstream visibility: tracking finished goods from manufacturer through DCs and retailers to the end customer.
DEFINITION
What is a cloud collaboration platform in supply chain?
ANSWER
A shared technology platform (SaaS) where multiple supply chain partners (buyers, suppliers, logistics providers) access and update the same data in real time — eliminating data sync delays and enabling true end-to-end visibility.
