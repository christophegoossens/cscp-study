Cost Accounting & Standard Costing
📖 ASCM Definition — Standard Costs: the target costs of an operation, process, or product, including direct material, direct labor, and overhead charges.
📖 ASCM Definition — Standard Cost Accounting System: a cost accounting system that uses cost units determined before production for estimating the cost of an order or product.
  • Cost variance: actual cost vs. standard. Composed of rate variance (price paid vs. standard) and volume/usage variance (quantity used vs. standard).
  • Cost driver: a measurable activity that drives overhead cost (e.g., direct labor hours). Used to allocate overhead to products.
  • Efficiency = standard hours produced ÷ actual hours worked. >100% = more efficient than standard.
  • Variances are the primary control mechanism in standard costing — signal when corrective action is needed.
Financial Statements
📖 ASCM Definition — International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS): a common global language for business affairs so that company accounts are understandable and comparable across international boundaries.
  • Balance Sheet: Assets = Liabilities + Equity. Snapshot at a point in time.
  • Income Statement: Revenue − COGS = Gross Profit; Gross Profit − Expenses = Net Income. Period-based.
  • Statement of Cash Flows: tracks actual cash in/out from operating, investing, and financing activities.
  • IFRS is principles-based (used in 144+ countries). US GAAP is rules-based. Both require the same four statements.

SC Total Cost & Strategic Profit Model
  • SC Total Cost includes: transportation, warehousing, inventory carrying cost, order processing, and SC management overhead.
  • Z-score (Altman): a financial distress prediction model using financial ratios. Used in SC to assess supplier financial health — high risk of bankruptcy = supply chain risk.
  • Strategic Profit Model (DuPont): links net profit margin × asset turnover = ROA (Return on Assets). Then ROA × financial leverage = ROE. Shows how SC improvements (lower cost, lower inventory) improve ROA and ROE.

TERM / CONCEPT
What is a cost variance?
ANSWER
The difference between actual cost and standard cost for an activity. Composed of: rate variance (price per unit vs. standard) and volume/usage variance (quantity used vs. standard).
TERM / CONCEPT
What is a cost driver?
ANSWER
A measurable activity that drives overhead cost (e.g., direct labor hours, machine hours). Used to allocate overhead to products in standard costing.
TERM / CONCEPT
How is efficiency calculated in standard costing?
ANSWER
Efficiency = Standard hours produced ÷ Actual hours worked × 100%. >100% = more productive than standard. <100% = less productive.
TERM / CONCEPT
ASCM def: IFRS
ANSWER
A common global language for business affairs so that company accounts are understandable and comparable across international boundaries. Used in 144+ countries. Principles-based.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the accounting equation (balance sheet)?
ANSWER
Assets = Liabilities + Equity. A balance sheet is a snapshot at a specific point in time showing what the company owns (assets), owes (liabilities), and the owners’ residual claim (equity).
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the income statement equation?
ANSWER
Revenue − COGS = Gross Profit; Gross Profit − Operating Expenses = EBIT; EBIT − Interest − Taxes = Net Income. Covers a period (month, quarter, year).
TERM / CONCEPT
What does the statement of cash flows show?
ANSWER
Actual cash inflows and outflows from (1) operating activities, (2) investing activities, and (3) financing activities. Different from net income — a company can be profitable but cash-negative.
TERM / CONCEPT
IFRS vs. US GAAP: key difference?
ANSWER
IFRS is principles-based (general rules, more judgment). GAAP is rules-based (detailed specific rules). Both require the same 4 financial statements. IFRS is used internationally; GAAP primarily in the US.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)?
ANSWER
The direct costs to produce goods sold: direct materials + direct labor + manufacturing overhead. SC cost reductions directly lower COGS and improve gross margin.
TERM / CONCEPT
What does the Strategic Profit Model show?
ANSWER
How SC decisions affect financial performance: Net Profit Margin × Asset Turnover = ROA; ROA × Financial Leverage = ROE. Lower inventory = higher asset turnover = higher ROA.
TERM / CONCEPT
How does reducing inventory improve ROA?
ANSWER
Lower inventory reduces total assets. ROA = Net Income ÷ Total Assets. With the same net income but fewer assets, ROA increases. This is the SC-finance linkage the Strategic Profit Model demonstrates.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the Altman Z-score used for in SC?
ANSWER
A financial distress model that predicts bankruptcy probability. SC managers use it to assess supplier financial health — a low Z-score means higher bankruptcy risk = supply chain risk.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is Supply Chain Total Cost?
ANSWER
All costs associated with running the SC: transportation + warehousing + inventory carrying cost + order processing + SC management overhead. Reducing any component improves total cost performance.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is inventory carrying cost?
ANSWER
The cost of holding inventory: storage/warehousing, capital cost (opportunity cost of cash tied up), obsolescence, insurance, shrinkage. Typically 20-30% of inventory value per year.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is rate variance in standard costing?
ANSWER
The difference between the actual price paid for a resource and the standard price, multiplied by actual quantity. Example: paid $21/hr vs. $20 standard × 1,300 hrs = $1,300 unfavorable rate variance.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is usage variance in standard costing?
ANSWER
The difference between actual quantity used and standard quantity, multiplied by the standard rate. Example: used 1,300 hours vs. 1,500 standard hours × $20 = $4,000 favorable usage variance.
TERM / CONCEPT
Why does SC care about financial statement analysis?
ANSWER
SC decisions (inventory levels, sourcing costs, logistics spend) directly impact balance sheet (assets), income statement (COGS, gross profit), and cash flow (working capital). SC managers must understand these linkages.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is management accounting?
ANSWER
Accounting focused on internal decision support — cost analysis, budgeting, variance reporting — as opposed to financial accounting which reports to external parties. Standard costing is a management accounting tool.
