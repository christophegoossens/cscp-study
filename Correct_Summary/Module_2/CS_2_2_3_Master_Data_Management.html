Master Data & MDM

Master data are static reference files (customer, supplier, product/SKU, BOM, inventory) that support all transactions. Master Data Management (MDM) is the discipline that coordinates the consistent use of master data across all systems and partners.

  • Types of SC master data: customer/supplier files, product/SKU files, BOM and standard costs, PO data, order history, inventory data, logistics/shipment data, engineering data.
  • Master data life cycle: Plan → Create & Process → Analyze/Use/Transfer → Maintain & Store → Archive → Destroy or Repurpose.
  • Effectivity date: the date on which a new engineering specification replaces an old one. Critical for BOM accuracy.

Data Capture Methods & Challenges
📖 ASCM Definition — Automatic Identification System (AIS): a system that can encode product data and enter it automatically into a computer system. Used for tracking shipments, inventory, and WIP.
  • Capture at source: best practice — avoids transcription errors from manual re-entry.
  • Passive/automatic capture (RFID, barcode scanners) is more productive than manual entry.
  • Real-time vs. batch: real-time is 10x+ more expensive; use batch where real-time isn’t required.
  • Big data risk: too much data slows systems and reduces focus — collect what adds value.
  • Challenges: fast-paced environments (best solution: passive/automated capture), hostile environments (special scanning equipment), language/literacy barriers (picture-based interfaces).

Barcode, RFID & Decision Support
  • 1D barcode (UPC): encodes product data in parallel lines. Requires line-of-sight scan. UPC-A is the standard 12-digit US barcode.
  • 2D barcode (QR code): stores more data than 1D. Can encode URLs, text, serial numbers. Scanned by camera.
  • RFID (Radio Frequency Identification): uses radio waves to identify tagged items. Does NOT require line of sight — can scan multiple items simultaneously. More expensive than barcodes but enables bulk scanning and tracking in motion.
  • Decision Support System (DSS): uses master data and analytics to help managers make decisions (e.g., inventory reorder, network optimization, demand sensing).

TERM / CONCEPT
What is Master Data Management (MDM)?
ANSWER
A discipline that works alongside IT to coordinate the consistent definition, governance, and use of master data across all systems, departments, and supply chain partners.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the 6 stages of the master data life cycle?
ANSWER
1. Plan. 2. Create & Process. 3. Analyze / Use / Transfer. 4. Maintain & Store. 5. Archive. 6. Destroy or Repurpose.
TERM / CONCEPT
What types of master data are important in SC management?
ANSWER
Customer/supplier files, product/SKU files, BOMs and standard costs, PO data for spend analysis, order history for demand analysis, inventory data, logistics/shipment data, engineering data.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is an effectivity date?
ANSWER
The date on which a new engineering specification (e.g., revised BOM or component) replaces the old one. Critical for ensuring the correct version of a BOM is used in production.
TERM / CONCEPT
ASCM def: Automatic Identification System (AIS)
ANSWER
A system that can encode product data and enter it automatically into a computer system. Used for tracking shipments, inventory, and work-in-process.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the two key features of AIDC devices?
ANSWER
1. Automatic classification (categorizing the item). 2. Automatic identification (identifying the specific item/batch). Together they eliminate manual scanning and data entry errors.
TERM / CONCEPT
Why is capturing data at the source best practice?
ANSWER
It avoids transcription errors from manual re-entry. Data captured at origin is more accurate and timely than data entered later from paper records or memory.
TERM / CONCEPT
Real-time vs. batch data capture: key trade-off?
ANSWER
Real-time is many times more expensive to engineer. Use real-time only when the business truly needs it (e.g., live stock visibility). Batch is cheaper and sufficient for many non-time-critical processes.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the big data risk?
ANSWER
Organizations can be overwhelmed by too much data — more data = slower systems, harder analysis, and less focus. Collect only data that adds value to decisions.
TERM / CONCEPT
How to handle fast-paced data capture environments?
ANSWER
Use passive/automatic capture (barcodes, RFID) rather than manual entry. Management is often resistant to steps that slow the process — automated capture adds speed AND accuracy.
TERM / CONCEPT
How to handle language/literacy barriers in data capture?
ANSWER
Use picture-based interfaces, color coding, or voice-guided systems. Reduces dependence on literacy or language proficiency for data input.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is a 1D barcode (UPC)?
ANSWER
A barcode that encodes data in parallel lines of varying widths. Requires direct line-of-sight scanning. UPC-A is the standard 12-digit US consumer product barcode.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is a 2D barcode (QR code)?
ANSWER
A barcode that encodes data in a grid pattern. Can store much more data than 1D (URLs, text, serial numbers). Scanned by camera — no line-of-sight laser required.
TERM / CONCEPT
RFID vs. barcode: key differences?
ANSWER
RFID: uses radio waves, no line-of-sight needed, multiple items scanned simultaneously, items can move at speed, higher cost. Barcode: line-of-sight required, one item at a time, cheaper.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the global identification requirement for AIDC?
ANSWER
Identifiers for objects must be unique globally so the internet will yield only one result. This is the basis for GS1 standards (GTIN, EPC) used in global trade.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is a Decision Support System (DSS)?
ANSWER
An information system that uses master data and analytics to help managers make decisions. Provides analysis, modeling, and scenario tools — not just data reporting.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the maintain and store phase of the master data life cycle?
ANSWER
Ensuring data security in transit and at rest, cleansing data (removing duplicates, correcting errors), and keeping master data current and accurate for ongoing transactions.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the archive phase of the master data life cycle?
ANSWER
Moving data that is no longer needed for current transactions to long-term storage (archive). Retained for legal, audit, or historical analysis purposes.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the destroy/repurpose phase of master data life cycle?
ANSWER
Deleting data no longer needed (for legal or system performance reasons) or repurposing it for new uses. Must comply with data privacy laws (e.g., GDPR).
TERM / CONCEPT
Why do barcodes and RFID reduce stockouts?
ANSWER
Automated, accurate real-time inventory tracking eliminates the errors (missed scans, wrong counts) that lead to phantom inventory and stockouts — especially critical for perishables and high-velocity items.
