IT Benefit-Cost Analysis

IT investments must be justified by a credible benefit-cost analysis. Past success lends credibility to new proposals.

  • Tangible benefits: measurable cost savings (logistics, procurement, sourcing) and revenue increases.
  • Intangible benefits: customer satisfaction, employee satisfaction, collaboration, reduced bullwhip effect, workforce redeployment.
  • Tangible costs: capital expenditures, one-time project expenses, ongoing operational costs.
  • Common cost pitfalls: overlooking support costs, optimistic estimates, underestimating to get approval, skipping change management/training.
  • Also consider the cost of NOT implementing — sometimes inaction is more expensive than the investment.

SC Technology Optimization Stages

Supply chain technology maturity follows an evolutionary path — not linear, but progressive.

  • Stage 1 — Multiple Dysfunction: disconnected legacy MRP systems, no SC leadership, no web capability. Fix: standardize processes, appoint SC leader, become internet-ready.
  • Stage 2 — Semifunctional Enterprise: MRP II/ERP in place; documented processes; KPIs aligned to goals. Gap: still mostly internal focus. Fix: appoint external-facing SC leader, start partner integration.
  • Stage 3 — Integrated Enterprise: point-to-point planning integration with key partners. Culture and technical resistance must be overcome. Requires a visionary business unit leader.
  • Stage 4 — Extended Enterprise: supply network with e-commerce exchanges; public/private internet exchanges for buying/selling. Requires sustained executive leadership and investment in human/technology capabilities.
  • Stage 5 — Networked Community: dynamic, self-adjusting supply networks with community-level optimization.

TERM / CONCEPT
What is the key to credibility in IT cost justification?
ANSWER
Past success. If prior IT investments delivered promised benefits, it lends strong credibility to a new proposal. Use actual data from similar past projects when available.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are tangible IT benefits?
ANSWER
Measurable, quantifiable cost savings (logistics, procurement, inventory) or revenue increases. Can be directly reflected in financial statements.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are intangible IT benefits?
ANSWER
Benefits that are real but hard to quantify in dollars: customer satisfaction, employee satisfaction, reduced bullwhip effect, workforce redeployment, improved collaboration and visibility.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the 3 categories of IT costs?
ANSWER
1. Capital expenditures (hardware, software licenses). 2. One-time project expenses (implementation, training, data migration). 3. Ongoing operational/support costs (maintenance, upgrades, staff).
TERM / CONCEPT
What are common IT cost estimation mistakes?
ANSWER
Overlooking operational support costs, using best-case assumptions, underestimating to get approval, and failing to invest in change management and training.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the benefit-cost ratio?
ANSWER
Total benefits over project life ÷ total costs over project life. A ratio > 1.0 means the investment returns more than it costs. Example: ratio of 1.37 = $1.37 back for every $1 invested.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is ROI in IT investment context?
ANSWER
Return on Investment = (Benefits − Costs) ÷ Costs × 100%. Measures net gain as a percentage of the investment made.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the cost of NOT implementing an IT project?
ANSWER
Sometimes inaction is more expensive than the investment — e.g., failing to keep up with competitors, losing customers due to poor service, or maintaining expensive manual processes.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is Stage 1 (Multiple Dysfunction) in SC technology evolution?
ANSWER
Multiple disconnected legacy MRP systems, no SC leadership, minimal web capability, system bottlenecks. Fix: standardize internal processes, become internet-ready, appoint SC leader.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is Stage 2 (Semifunctional Enterprise)?
ANSWER
MRP II/ERP implemented; documented processes; KPIs aligned to goals. Still mostly internal focus. Fix: appoint externally-focused SC leader to begin partner integration.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is Stage 3 (Integrated Enterprise)?
ANSWER
Point-to-point planning integration with key partners. Overcoming cultural and technical resistance is required. Needs a visionary business unit leader to champion integration.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is Stage 4 (Extended Enterprise)?
ANSWER
Supply network using e-commerce, public/private internet exchanges to buy and sell. Requires sustained executive leadership and investment in human and technology capabilities.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is Stage 5 (Networked Community)?
ANSWER
Dynamic, self-adjusting supply networks with community-level optimization. The highest level of SC technology maturity — all partners share data and optimize collectively.
TERM / CONCEPT
What characterizes each stage of SC technology evolution?
ANSWER
Stage 1: disconnected. Stage 2: integrated internally (ERP). Stage 3: integrated with key partners. Stage 4: networked supply community. Stage 5: dynamic self-optimizing network.
TERM / CONCEPT
Why is change management critical in IT projects?
ANSWER
Poor change management leads to user resistance, low adoption, and high rework costs. Failing to invest in change management often causes IT projects to fail or deliver below expected benefits.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is a sunk cost?
ANSWER
A cost that has already been incurred and cannot be recovered. Salaried employee time is a sunk cost once incurred. Sunk costs should be excluded from future benefit-cost decisions.
TERM / CONCEPT
What should IT investment decisions prioritize?
ANSWER
Credibility of assumptions, realistic cost estimates (including support costs), and a clear line between the investment and measurable benefits — either tangible (financial) or intangible (strategic).
TERM / CONCEPT
How is technology optimization mapped to SC maturity?
ANSWER
Each stage of SC evolution requires a corresponding level of technology capability. Moving to a higher stage requires the technology, processes, culture, and leadership of the current stage to be solidly in place.
TERM / CONCEPT
What blocks companies from moving between stages?
ANSWER
Culture and technical resistance. Stage 2→3 requires overcoming internal resistance to external integration. Stage 3→4 requires sustained executive leadership and investment in network-level capabilities.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the relationship between IT investment and SC competitiveness?
ANSWER
Well-managed IT investments can generate cost savings across logistics, procurement, and sourcing — directly improving competitiveness. Poorly chosen or managed IT is a competitive liability.
