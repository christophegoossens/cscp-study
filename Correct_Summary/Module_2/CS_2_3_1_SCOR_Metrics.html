Measurement System Basics

Good metrics drive the right behavior. The basic objectives of any measurement system are:

  • Monitoring: track actual performance vs. business objectives.
  • Controlling: compare data to standards; determine when the system needs modification.
  • Directing: understand human motivation; incentivize people to work toward goals.

SC metrics should cut across functions and organizations — not just internal KPIs — to promote collaboration. Key metrics include: % on-time delivery, forecast accuracy, order fulfillment lead time, total SC cost, cash-to-cash cycle time, inventory days of supply.

SCOR DS Framework

SCOR DS (Supply Chain Operations Reference — Digital Standard) is ASCM’s cross-industry SC management framework. It provides a hierarchical process model, standard metrics, and best practices.

  • 6 SCOR DS processes: Plan, Source, Make, Deliver, Return, Enable.
  • SCOR DS is a series of never-ending processes — it drives continuous improvement.
  • Hierarchical levels: Level 1 = process types (Plan/Source/Make/Deliver/Return/Enable); Level 2 = process categories; Level 3 = process elements; Level 4 = implementation steps (company-specific).
  • SCORmark: benchmarking tool that compares your SC performance against industry peers to identify gaps.

Key SCOR DS Metrics
  • Perfect Customer Order Fulfillment: % of orders delivered on time, complete, undamaged, with correct documentation. The highest-level customer-facing metric.
  • Order Fulfillment Cycle Time: time from customer order to delivery.
  • Total SC Management Cost: all costs to run the SC (plan, source, make, deliver, return).
  • Cash-to-Cash Cycle Time: DIO + DSO − DPO (days inventory outstanding + days sales outstanding − days payable outstanding). Measures working capital efficiency.
  • Inventory Days of Supply: inventory on hand ÷ average daily demand. How many days of supply are held.
  • Return on SC Fixed Assets (ROSCA) and Return on Working Capital (ROWC): financial returns from SC investment.

TERM / CONCEPT
What are the 3 objectives of any measurement system?
ANSWER
1. Monitoring: track actual performance vs. business objectives. 2. Controlling: compare to standards, determine when to modify. 3. Directing: understand human motivation and incentivize desired behavior.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the 6 SCOR DS processes?
ANSWER
Plan, Source, Make, Deliver, Return, Enable. These cover the entire supply chain from planning through reverse logistics and enabling processes.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is SCOR DS?
ANSWER
Supply Chain Operations Reference — Digital Standard. ASCM’s cross-industry framework providing a hierarchical process model (Levels 1-4), standard metrics, and best practices for SC management.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the 4 levels of the SCOR DS hierarchy?
ANSWER
Level 1: Process types (Plan/Source/Make/Deliver/Return/Enable). Level 2: Process categories. Level 3: Process elements. Level 4: Implementation steps (company-specific, not defined by SCOR).
TERM / CONCEPT
What is SCORmark?
ANSWER
A benchmarking tool that compares an organization’s SC performance against industry peers to identify performance gaps and prioritize improvement efforts.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is Perfect Customer Order Fulfillment?
ANSWER
The percentage of orders delivered on time, complete, undamaged, with the correct documentation (invoice, packing list, etc.). The highest-level customer-facing SCOR DS metric.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is Order Fulfillment Cycle Time?
ANSWER
The time elapsed from when a customer places an order to when they receive it. A key responsiveness metric.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is Cash-to-Cash Cycle Time?
ANSWER
DIO + DSO − DPO. Days Inventory Outstanding + Days Sales Outstanding − Days Payable Outstanding. Measures how efficiently working capital flows through the SC.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is Inventory Days of Supply?
ANSWER
Inventory on hand ÷ average daily demand. How many days the current inventory would last at the current demand rate.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)?
ANSWER
The average number of days it takes to collect payment after a sale. High DSO = slow cash collection = more working capital tied up in receivables.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is Days Payable Outstanding (DPO)?
ANSWER
The average number of days it takes to pay suppliers. Higher DPO = supplier financing being used = less working capital needed.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is Total SC Management Cost?
ANSWER
All costs to plan, source, make, deliver, and return goods across the supply chain. Includes direct costs and overhead allocated to SC activities.
TERM / CONCEPT
Why must SC metrics cut across functions?
ANSWER
Internal metrics can optimize a single function at the expense of the whole chain (e.g., purchasing lowers price but increases supply risk). Cross-functional metrics align incentives to overall SC performance.
TERM / CONCEPT
What KPIs are tracked per supplier in SCOR DS scorecards?
ANSWER
On-time delivery rate, quality (defect rate), lead time performance, responsiveness to issues. Scorecards include both raw material suppliers and freight forwarders.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is ROSCA?
ANSWER
Return on Supply Chain Fixed Assets — measures the financial return generated from SC fixed asset investments (warehouses, equipment, IT systems).
TERM / CONCEPT
What is ROWC?
ANSWER
Return on Working Capital — measures the financial return from working capital employed in SC operations (inventory, receivables minus payables).
TERM / CONCEPT
What is EBIT and why is it relevant to SC?
ANSWER
Earnings Before Interest and Taxes — measures the organization’s earning power from ongoing operations. SC cost reductions directly improve EBIT.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is COGS and why matters in SC?
ANSWER
Cost of Goods Sold — the direct costs to produce goods sold. SC efficiency improvements (lower sourcing cost, less waste, better logistics) reduce COGS and improve gross margin.
TERM / CONCEPT
What visual tools are used in SC scorecards?
ANSWER
Traffic lights (red/yellow/green), trend arrows, bar charts, and gauge indicators. Visual dashboards allow executives to quickly identify issues without reading detailed data tables.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the Enable process in SCOR DS?
ANSWER
The enabling processes that support all other SCOR DS processes — includes SC risk management, performance management, data management, regulatory compliance, and technology management.
