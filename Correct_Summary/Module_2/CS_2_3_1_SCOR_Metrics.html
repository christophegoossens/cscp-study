Overview

The Supply Chain Operations Reference (SCOR) model provides a cross-industry framework for describing, measuring, and improving supply chain performance. SCOR metrics enable benchmarking and performance improvement across the supply chain.

ASCM Definitions

📖 Definition:
SCOR model: A reference model maintained by ASCM that provides a cross-industry standard for evaluating and improving supply chain operations through Process, Metrics, Best Practices, and Technology. The processes are: Plan, Source, Make, Deliver, Return, and Enable.
📖 Definition:
Perfect Order Fulfillment (POF): The percentage of orders that are delivered on time, in full, damage-free, with correct documentation, to the right location. The primary SCOR customer-facing reliability metric.
📖 Definition:
Order Fulfillment Cycle Time: The average time from customer order receipt to customer receipt of delivery. A SCOR responsiveness metric.
📖 Definition:
Upside Supply Chain Flexibility: The number of days required to achieve an unplanned 20% increase in production volume. A SCOR agility metric.
📖 Definition:
Upside Supply Chain Adaptability: The maximum unplanned percentage increase in production volume that can be sustained for 30 days. A SCOR agility metric.
📖 Definition:
Total Supply Chain Management Cost: The total costs to plan, source, make, and deliver products — including order management, materials, labor, inventory carrying, and return processing costs. A SCOR cost metric.

SCOR Metric Categories

💡 Key Point:
SCOR measures supply chain performance in five categories: 1) Reliability (Perfect Order Fulfillment), 2) Responsiveness (Order Fulfillment Cycle Time), 3) Agility (Flexibility and Adaptability), 4) Cost (Total SCM Cost, COGS), 5) Asset Management (Cash-to-Cash Cycle Time, Inventory Days of Supply).
  • Customer-facing attributes: Reliability, Responsiveness, Agility
  • Internal-facing attributes: Cost, Asset Management
  • SCOR metrics are nested: Level 1 (strategic), Level 2 (operational), Level 3 (diagnostic)
  • Benchmarking: SCOR metrics enable comparison to industry peers and identification of gaps
📖 Definition:
Cash-to-cash cycle time: Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) + Inventory Days of Supply (IDS) − Days Payable Outstanding (DPO). Measures how many days it takes for cash invested in the supply chain to return as cash from customers.

DEFINITION
What is the SCOR model?
ANSWER
The Supply Chain Operations Reference model — a cross-industry standard for evaluating and improving supply chain operations. Provides Process, Metrics, Best Practices, and Technology frameworks. Processes: Plan, Source, Make, Deliver, Return, Enable.
CONCEPT
What are the six SCOR processes?
ANSWER
Plan, Source, Make, Deliver, Return, and Enable. Plan is the overarching coordination process. Enable supports all other processes with data, governance, and infrastructure.
DEFINITION
What is Perfect Order Fulfillment (POF)?
ANSWER
The percentage of orders delivered on time, in full, damage-free, with correct documentation, to the right location. The primary SCOR reliability metric — measures the 'perfect' end-to-end order experience.
CONCEPT
What are the five SCOR performance attribute categories?
ANSWER
1) Reliability (customer-facing), 2) Responsiveness (customer-facing), 3) Agility (customer-facing), 4) Cost (internal), 5) Asset Management (internal).
DEFINITION
What is Order Fulfillment Cycle Time?
ANSWER
The average elapsed time from customer order receipt to delivery — a SCOR responsiveness metric. Shorter cycle time = more responsive supply chain. Includes all delays in order processing, production, and delivery.
DEFINITION
What is Upside Supply Chain Flexibility (SCOR agility metric)?
ANSWER
The number of days required to achieve an unplanned 20% increase in production volume. Measures how quickly a supply chain can scale up production in response to a demand surge.
FORMULA
What is Cash-to-Cash Cycle Time and its formula?
ANSWER
Formula: Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) + Inventory Days of Supply (IDS) − Days Payable Outstanding (DPO). Measures how many days cash is tied up in the supply chain. Lower is better for working capital.
DEFINITION
What is Total Supply Chain Management Cost (SCOR cost metric)?
ANSWER
The total cost to plan, source, make, and deliver products — including order management, material acquisition, production, inventory carrying, and returns costs expressed as a percentage of revenue.
KEY DISTINCTION
What is the difference between customer-facing and internal-facing SCOR attributes?
ANSWER
Customer-facing: Reliability, Responsiveness, Agility — measured from the customer's perspective. Internal-facing: Cost and Asset Management — measured from the company's financial perspective. Both matter for supply chain excellence.
DEFINITION
What is Inventory Days of Supply (IDS)?
ANSWER
A SCOR asset management metric: IDS = (Inventory Value / Daily Cost of Goods Sold). Measures how many days of sales the current inventory level can support. Lower IDS = more efficient inventory use.
DEFINITION
What is Days Payable Outstanding (DPO) and why does it affect cash-to-cash?
ANSWER
DPO = (Accounts Payable / Daily COGS). Measures how many days a company takes to pay its suppliers. Higher DPO reduces the cash-to-cash cycle — the company uses supplier credit to delay cash outflow.
DEFINITION
What is Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)?
ANSWER
DSO = (Accounts Receivable / Daily Revenue). Measures how many days it takes to collect cash from customers after a sale. Higher DSO = cash tied up longer in receivables, increasing the cash-to-cash cycle.
CONCEPT
How are SCOR metrics organized in levels?
ANSWER
Level 1 (strategic): high-level performance attributes. Level 2 (operational): diagnostic metrics supporting Level 1. Level 3 (detailed): specific process-level measures. Lower levels diagnose why Level 1 metrics are under/over performing.
CONCEPT
What is benchmarking and why is the SCOR model useful for it?
ANSWER
Benchmarking compares your performance metrics against industry peers or best-in-class companies. SCOR provides standardized metric definitions enabling apples-to-apples comparison — identifying gaps and improvement opportunities.
DEFINITION
What is Upside Supply Chain Adaptability?
ANSWER
The maximum unplanned percentage increase in production volume that can be sustained for 30 days — a SCOR agility metric. Measures the upper limit of surge capacity.
