Overview

Supply chain technology applications span planning, execution, visibility, and collaboration. Understanding which technology serves which supply chain function is essential for effective SC management.

Planning Systems

📖 Definition:
Advanced Planning and Scheduling (APS): Software that uses optimization algorithms to create feasible production and supply plans, considering materials, capacity, and other constraints simultaneously. Goes beyond basic MRP.
📖 Definition:
Demand Planning (DP) system: Software that supports statistical forecasting, collaborative forecasting, and demand management. Provides a platform for creating, sharing, and improving demand plans.
📖 Definition:
Supply Chain Planning (SCP): Software that plans the movement and storage of goods across the entire supply chain — from raw material sourcing through production and delivery to end customers.

Execution Systems

📖 Definition:
Warehouse Management System (WMS): Controls all activities within a warehouse — receiving, putaway, directed picking, packing, shipping, cycle counting, and labor management.
📖 Definition:
Transportation Management System (TMS): Plans and optimizes shipment routing, carrier selection, load consolidation, and freight cost. Provides shipment visibility from origin to destination.
📖 Definition:
Order Management System (OMS): Manages the lifecycle of customer orders from capture through fulfillment — including available-to-promise, allocation, pick/pack/ship, and returns.

Visibility and Collaboration

📖 Definition:
Supply chain visibility (SCV): The ability to track goods, materials, and information in real time from origin to destination across the entire supply chain — including Tier 2 and Tier 3 suppliers.
📖 Definition:
Vendor-Managed Inventory (VMI): An arrangement where the supplier (vendor) is responsible for managing and replenishing inventory at the customer's location — using the customer's inventory and sales data.
💡 Key Point:
Technology enables three types of supply chain collaboration: 1) Data sharing (EDI, APIs), 2) Process integration (CPFR, VMI), 3) Joint decision making (S&OP with customers/suppliers).
  • Internet of Things (IoT): sensors on products, vehicles, and equipment provide real-time location and condition data
  • Blockchain: provides immutable, shared transaction records for supply chain traceability and authenticity
  • RFID (Radio Frequency Identification): enables automated, non-line-of-sight inventory tracking in warehouses and transit
  • Barcode scanning: the foundational data capture technology for most supply chain execution systems

DEFINITION
What is Advanced Planning and Scheduling (APS)?
ANSWER
Software using optimization algorithms to create feasible production and supply plans considering materials, capacity, and constraints simultaneously. Goes beyond basic MRP by optimizing rather than just calculating requirements.
DEFINITION
What is a Warehouse Management System (WMS)?
ANSWER
Software controlling all warehouse activities — receiving, directed putaway, pick/pack/ship, cycle counting, and labor management. Provides real-time inventory location visibility within the warehouse.
DEFINITION
What is a Transportation Management System (TMS)?
ANSWER
Software for planning, executing, and optimizing freight movement — carrier selection, load planning, rate management, shipment tracking, and freight bill audit/payment.
DEFINITION
What is an Order Management System (OMS)?
ANSWER
Software managing the full lifecycle of customer orders from capture through fulfillment — including ATP checks, allocation, pick/pack/ship direction, status tracking, and returns processing.
DEFINITION
What is Supply Chain Visibility (SCV)?
ANSWER
The ability to track goods, materials, and information in real time from origin to destination across the entire supply chain — including Tier 2 and Tier 3 suppliers. Enables proactive exception management.
DEFINITION
What is Vendor-Managed Inventory (VMI)?
ANSWER
An arrangement where the supplier manages and replenishes inventory at the customer's location using the customer's inventory and POS data. Reduces ordering burden for the customer and gives the supplier better demand visibility.
CONCEPT
What is the Internet of Things (IoT) and how is it used in supply chains?
ANSWER
A network of physical sensors on products, vehicles, and equipment providing real-time location, condition (temperature, humidity), and status data. Enables track-and-trace, cold chain monitoring, predictive maintenance.
CONCEPT
What is blockchain in supply chain context?
ANSWER
A distributed, immutable ledger shared among supply chain partners that records transactions in tamper-evident blocks. Used for product traceability, authenticity verification, smart contracts, and reducing fraud.
DEFINITION
What is RFID (Radio Frequency Identification)?
ANSWER
Technology using electromagnetic fields to automatically identify and track tagged items — works without line-of-sight, enabling bulk scanning. Used for warehouse receiving, pallet tracking, anti-theft, and inventory accuracy.
DEFINITION
What is Collaborative Planning, Forecasting and Replenishment (CPFR)?
ANSWER
A business process where trading partners jointly develop forecasts, share promotion plans, and align replenishment schedules. Requires both technology (shared data platform) and process (joint review and exception management).
DEFINITION
What is demand planning software?
ANSWER
Software supporting statistical forecasting, collaborative forecasting, promotion planning, and demand management. Provides algorithms, workflows, and interfaces for the full demand planning process.
CONCEPT
What are the three types of supply chain technology collaboration?
ANSWER
1) Data sharing (EDI, APIs — automated document exchange), 2) Process integration (CPFR, VMI — shared business processes), 3) Joint decision making (supplier integration into S&OP, collaborative design).
DEFINITION
What is Electronic Data Interchange (EDI)?
ANSWER
A standard for electronic exchange of business documents (POs, invoices, ASNs, ship notices) between companies in a structured format. Automates B2B transactions, eliminates manual data entry, and reduces transaction errors.
DEFINITION
What is an Advance Ship Notice (ASN) in supply chain systems?
ANSWER
An electronic notification from a supplier to a customer about a shipment — including items, quantities, carrier, and expected arrival time. Enables the receiving DC to plan labor and space before the truck arrives.
DEFINITION
What is Supply Chain Planning (SCP) software?
ANSWER
Software that plans material and goods movement across the entire supply chain from raw materials to delivery — integrating demand planning, supply planning, inventory optimization, and distribution planning into a coordinated plan.
