Overview

Purchase orders (POs) are the primary transactional documents that execute procurement commitments. Understanding the PO process and its variations is essential for effective supply chain execution.

ASCM Definitions

📖 Definition:
Purchase order (PO): A commercial document issued by a buyer to a seller indicating types, quantities, and agreed prices for products or services. Upon acceptance, it becomes a legally binding contract.
📖 Definition:
Three-way match: The verification process that compares the Purchase Order (PO), the Goods Receipt (packing slip or receiving document), and the Supplier Invoice to ensure all three are consistent before authorizing payment.
📖 Definition:
Open purchase order: A PO that has been issued but not yet fully received or invoiced. Represents a committed but unfulfilled purchasing obligation.
📖 Definition:
Expediting: The process of urgently following up on a purchase order to accelerate delivery due to a supply shortage, unexpected demand, or supply disruption.

PO Process

💡 Key Point:
Standard PO lifecycle: 1) Requisition (need identified), 2) PO creation and approval, 3) PO issued to supplier, 4) Supplier acknowledgment, 5) Goods/services delivery, 6) Receiving and inspection, 7) Three-way match and invoice approval, 8) Payment.
  • Purchase requisition: an internal document requesting authorization to purchase — must be approved before a PO is issued
  • PO acknowledgment (POA): supplier confirmation that the PO is received and the terms are accepted
  • Advance Ship Notice (ASN): supplier notification that goods have shipped — enables DC receiving preparation
  • Goods Receipt (GR): formal recording of goods received against the open PO in the ERP system
  • Invoice matching: comparing supplier invoice to PO and GR — discrepancies go to disputes before payment

Special PO Types

📖 Definition:
Blanket purchase order (BPO): A long-term PO for a specified quantity at an agreed price, against which individual releases are called off as needed. Reduces per-transaction overhead.
📖 Definition:
Scheduled agreement: A long-term purchasing agreement specifying delivery quantities and dates in advance, enabling supplier production planning. Used in automotive and other high-volume manufacturing.
💡 Key Point:
P-cards (procurement cards): corporate credit cards that employees can use for small-value, low-risk purchases without going through the full PO process. Reduces transaction cost for tail spend.

DEFINITION
What is a purchase order (PO)?
ANSWER
A commercial document issued by a buyer to a seller specifying item type, quantity, and agreed price. Upon supplier acceptance, it becomes a legally binding contract for that transaction.
DEFINITION
What is the three-way match in accounts payable?
ANSWER
Comparing the Purchase Order (PO) + Goods Receipt (receiving document) + Supplier Invoice to verify all three are consistent before authorizing payment. Catches pricing errors, quantity discrepancies, and duplicate invoices.
CONCEPT
What is the standard PO lifecycle?
ANSWER
1) Requisition (need identified), 2) PO creation and approval, 3) PO issued to supplier, 4) Supplier acknowledgment, 5) Delivery, 6) Receiving and inspection, 7) Three-way match and invoice approval, 8) Payment.
DEFINITION
What is a PO acknowledgment (POA)?
ANSWER
A formal confirmation from the supplier that the PO has been received and the terms (price, quantity, delivery date) are accepted. If the supplier cannot meet the requested delivery date, they should propose an alternate date at this stage.
DEFINITION
What is an Advance Ship Notice (ASN)?
ANSWER
An electronic notification from the supplier that goods have shipped — specifying carrier, tracking number, items, and expected arrival time. Enables the receiving DC to plan labor, dock space, and put-away before the truck arrives.
DEFINITION
What is a Goods Receipt (GR) and why is it important?
ANSWER
The formal recording in ERP that goods ordered on a PO have been physically received. The GR is the trigger for accounts payable to match the supplier invoice and authorize payment. Without a GR, invoices should not be paid.
DEFINITION
What is expediting?
ANSWER
The urgent follow-up on an open purchase order to accelerate delivery when there is a supply shortage, unexpected demand spike, or supply disruption. May involve premium freight, supplier priority requests, or emergency sourcing.
DEFINITION
What is a blanket purchase order (BPO)?
ANSWER
A long-term PO for a specified quantity at an agreed unit price, against which individual delivery releases are called off over time. Reduces per-transaction ordering overhead while maintaining pricing commitment.
DEFINITION
What is a purchase requisition?
ANSWER
An internal request to purchase — submitted by the user department, requiring approval from purchasing authority before a PO is issued. Creates an audit trail and ensures purchases are authorized before commitment to a supplier.
DEFINITION
What is a procurement card (P-card)?
ANSWER
A corporate credit card used for small-value, low-risk purchases without the full PO process. Reduces transaction cost for 'tail spend' (many small purchases). Typically used with spending limits and approved vendor lists.
DEFINITION
What is tail spend?
ANSWER
The large number of small-value purchases that collectively represent a significant transaction cost burden — often 80% of transactions but only 20% of spend. Managed efficiently via P-cards, catalogs, or supplier-managed replenishment.
DEFINITION
What is a scheduled agreement in purchasing?
ANSWER
A long-term agreement specifying delivery quantities and dates in advance — enabling the supplier to plan production and materials. Common in automotive manufacturing (JIT delivery schedules). Reduces ordering lead time to near-zero.
DEFINITION
What is an open purchase order?
ANSWER
A PO that has been issued to a supplier but not yet fully received or invoiced. Represents a committed but unfulfilled purchasing obligation — must be tracked and managed until closed.
CONCEPT
What is an invoice discrepancy and how is it resolved?
ANSWER
When the supplier invoice does not match the PO or GR — price difference, quantity error, or items not received. Goes to a 'dispute' queue where purchasing/AP resolves with the supplier before payment is authorized.
CONCEPT
What is EDI in purchase order processing?
ANSWER
Electronic Data Interchange enables POs, POAs, ASNs, and invoices to be exchanged electronically between buyer and supplier systems in standardized formats — eliminating manual data entry, reducing errors, and accelerating the transaction cycle.
