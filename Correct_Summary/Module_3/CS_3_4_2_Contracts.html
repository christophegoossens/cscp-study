If a relationship is to be successful, both parties must know and understand their responsibilities. Normal responsibilities must be defined in the form of terms and conditions. The ASCM Supply Chain Dictionary defines terms and conditions as “all the provisions and agreements of a contract.”
Organizations need to specify payment terms in contracts with suppliers or customers. The ASCM Supply Chain Dictionary defines payment terms as “conditions surrounding payment for a sale, providing a time frame in which a customer can pay without late penalties or additional fees.” These terms specify when payment is due, whether shipment will occur before or after payment is made, and the method of payment that is required.

Question 1
A fixed-price contract is most appropriate when:
A.Requirements are uncertain
B.Scope and specifications are well-defined and stable
C.The supplier faces high cost volatility
D.Speed is the priority
Fixed-price contracts work best when scope is clear and stable, transferring cost risk to the supplier.
Question 2
A cost-plus contract shifts cost risk to:
A.The supplier
B.The buyer
C.Both parties equally
D.A third-party insurer
In cost-plus contracts, the buyer reimburses all allowable costs plus a fee — the buyer bears cost risk.
Question 3
An option to purchase in a contract gives:
A.The supplier the right to refuse orders
B.The buyer the right to purchase additional quantities at a predetermined price
C.Both parties price renegotiation rights
D.Automatic contract renewal
An option to purchase gives the buyer the right (not obligation) to buy additional volume at a negotiated price.
Question 4
A take-or-pay contract requires:
A.The buyer to pay only if goods are taken
B.The buyer to pay a minimum amount whether or not the volume is purchased
C.The supplier to guarantee delivery or pay penalties
D.Annual price renegotiation
Take-or-pay contracts commit the buyer to purchase a minimum volume or pay for it regardless — protecting the supplier's investment in capacity.
