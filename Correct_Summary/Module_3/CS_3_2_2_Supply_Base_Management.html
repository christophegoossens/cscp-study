Spend Analysis

A spend analysis is a retroactive review of all purchases during a period, used to identify opportunities for cost savings through supply base optimization.

Steps:

  • Consolidate supplier databases (across business units/systems)
  • Sort by category; rank from highest to lowest total spend
  • Identify total spend and number of suppliers per category
  • Run 3 Pareto analyses:
  1. Categories ranked by total spend (highest to lowest) — identify where most money goes.
  2. Categories ranked by number of suppliers (most to fewest) — identify fragmented supply base.
  3. Average spend per supplier by category (lowest to highest) — identify where consolidation would generate quantity discounts.

Supply Base Right-Sizing (Rationalization)

Supply base right-sizing (rationalization) = reviewing suppliers in a category to determine the ideal number. Reducing supplier count in a category can:

  • Improve quality of remaining suppliers
  • Reduce costs of quality (fewer defects, fewer process changes)
  • Generate quantity discounts (higher volume per supplier)
  • Lower administrative and procurement costs

Approaches:

  • Pareto analysis to identify which suppliers to keep (top 20% providing 80% of value)
  • SMART goal for reduction (e.g., reduce from 12 to 5 suppliers in category X within 6 months)
  • Consider product life cycle stage: easier to reduce during growth/maturity (more options) than introduction/decline
  • Redesign products/services to require fewer or different materials — reduces supply base organically

Right-sizing works well alongside outsourcing/offshoring initiatives. It is a continuous endeavor — new categories emerge, supplier performance changes, and market conditions shift.

Supply Base Market Research
  • Starts with internal analysis: historical demand, forecast demand, supplier performance gaps
  • External market analysis: what other suppliers exist, validate market price predictions
  • Corroborate predictions from multiple sources (experts, consultants, networking, government publications)
  • Present only the most decision-relevant information to senior management

Key principle: the reduction in supplier count should not be arbitrary — it should be based on an assessment of the ideal number for the category, balancing cost, quality, risk, and flexibility.

TERM / CONCEPT
What is a spend analysis?
ANSWER
A retroactive review of all purchases the organization makes during a period — sorted by category and supplier — to identify optimization opportunities for the supply base.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the 3 Pareto analyses in a spend analysis?
ANSWER
1. Categories by total spend (high to low): where most money goes. 2. Categories by # of suppliers (most to fewest): fragmented supply base. 3. Average spend per supplier by category (lowest to highest): where consolidation yields discounts.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is supply base right-sizing (rationalization)?
ANSWER
Reviewing suppliers in a category to determine the ideal number. Reducing supplier count improves quality, reduces costs of quality, generates quantity discounts, and lowers administrative costs.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is a SMART goal in supply base right-sizing?
ANSWER
Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, Time-bound. Example: reduce suppliers in category X from 12 to 5 within 6 months. Goals should be based on the ideal number, not arbitrary targets.
TERM / CONCEPT
Why is supply base right-sizing a continuous endeavor?
ANSWER
New categories emerge over time, supplier performance changes, market conditions shift, and product redesign changes material needs. Supply base optimization is never a one-time event.
TERM / CONCEPT
How does product redesign relate to supply base right-sizing?
ANSWER
Redesigning products to require fewer or different materials organically reduces the supply base — eliminating categories entirely or enabling standardization across categories.
TERM / CONCEPT
When is supply base right-sizing hardest?
ANSWER
During introduction (limited alternative suppliers) and decline phases (suppliers exit market). Easiest during growth and maturity when more supplier options exist.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is supply base market research?
ANSWER
Gathering internal (historical demand, forecasts, current performance) and external (alternative suppliers, market prices) information to inform supply base decisions. Corroborate predictions from multiple sources.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the 80/20 rule in supply base analysis?
ANSWER
From a Pareto analysis, typically ~20% of suppliers provide ~80% of value (spend). These should be retained; the remaining 80% are candidates for consolidation or elimination.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the benefits of supplier consolidation?
ANSWER
Fewer suppliers → better relationships, quantity discounts, lower quality costs (fewer defects), simpler administration, more leverage in negotiation.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the risks of too few suppliers?
ANSWER
Supply disruption risk increases. Single or sole-source dependency is dangerous if the supplier has quality issues, financial problems, or capacity constraints.
TERM / CONCEPT
What does supply base market research require externally?
ANSWER
Validating what other suppliers exist, checking market price predictions, and understanding supply market dynamics. Sources: expert suppliers, consultants, networking, government publications.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the key data preparation step for a spend analysis?
ANSWER
Consolidating supplier databases across different business units and IT systems — ensuring the same supplier isn’t counted multiple times under different names.
TERM / CONCEPT
What additional organizational goals can supply base right-sizing serve?
ANSWER
Cost reduction is the primary goal, but right-sizing can also advance: CSR/diversity supplier goals, sustainability, risk reduction (geographic diversification), and innovation (bringing in best-in-class specialists).
TERM / CONCEPT
How does right-sizing relate to outsourcing?
ANSWER
A supply base right-sizing initiative can work well with outsourcing/offshoring — after outsourcing to a best-in-class provider, the number of suppliers in that category may naturally decrease.
