Overview

Supply base analysis helps determine which categories of purchasing would provide the greatest cost savings because there is significant room for improvement. This information helps with supply base right-sizing, such as consolidating to fewer suppliers.

Question 1
Supply base rationalization primarily aims to:
A.Increase supplier competition
B.Reduce procurement complexity and build deeper strategic relationships
C.Eliminate all single-source risks
D.Comply with regulations
Rationalization reduces the number of suppliers to a manageable set, enabling deeper collaboration, better pricing through volume consolidation.
Question 2
Kraljic Strategic quadrant items (high supply risk, high profit impact) require:
A.Competitive bidding
B.Long-term partnerships, joint development, and supply assurance strategies
C.Minimal management attention
D.Spot purchasing
Strategic items require long-term partnerships and supply assurance strategies due to their criticality and limited supplier base.
Question 3
Preferred supplier status means:
A.The supplier is the sole source
B.The supplier has met qualification criteria and receives preferential volume allocation
C.The supplier has the lowest price
D.The supplier is geographically closest
Preferred supplier status indicates qualification and is given priority for new business — they are trusted partners.
Question 4
Supplier development programmes:
A.Reduce the number of suppliers
B.Improve a supplier's capabilities (quality, delivery, cost) to better meet buyer requirements
C.Switch to lower cost suppliers
D.Eliminate supplier audits
Supplier development involves joint improvement activities to build supplier capability where gaps exist.
