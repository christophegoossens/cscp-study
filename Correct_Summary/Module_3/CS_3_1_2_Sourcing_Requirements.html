Overview

Sourcing requirements define what the supply chain needs from its supply base — in terms of quality, quantity, cost, delivery, sustainability, and service. Well-defined sourcing requirements are the foundation of effective procurement.

ASCM Definitions

📖 Definition:
Sourcing: The process of identifying, evaluating, and engaging suppliers for goods and services. Part of the procurement function — involves strategy, supplier selection, contracting, and ongoing management.
📖 Definition:
Procurement: The act of buying goods and services. In supply chain context, procurement encompasses the entire process from need identification through supplier selection, ordering, receiving, and payment.
📖 Definition:
Specification: A detailed description of the requirements for a product, service, material, or process — including dimensions, quality standards, performance requirements, and compliance criteria.

Types of Requirements

💡 Key Point:
Requirements for sourcing span five dimensions — the 'QCDSR' framework: Quality, Cost, Delivery, Service, and Regulatory/compliance requirements. All five must be defined before supplier selection begins.
  • Quality requirements: product specifications, tolerance ranges, quality standards (ISO, Six Sigma), inspection criteria
  • Cost requirements: target cost, total cost budget, pricing structure expectations (fixed, variable, indexed)
  • Delivery requirements: lead time, delivery frequency, minimum order quantities, packaging requirements
  • Service requirements: technical support, returns processing, EDI capability, VMI willingness
  • Regulatory/compliance: environmental standards, labor practices, material safety, country-of-origin
📖 Definition:
Statement of Work (SOW): A formal document defining the scope, deliverables, timeline, and performance standards for a service engagement or project. The service equivalent of a product specification.
📖 Definition:
Request for Proposal (RFP): A formal document issued to potential suppliers inviting them to submit a proposal demonstrating how they would meet stated requirements. More comprehensive than a Request for Quote (RFQ) — includes technical evaluation.
📖 Definition:
Request for Quote (RFQ): A document soliciting price quotations from suppliers for a specified product or service. More transactional than an RFP — focused primarily on pricing for well-defined requirements.

DEFINITION
What is sourcing?
ANSWER
The process of identifying, evaluating, and engaging suppliers — encompassing strategy, supplier selection, contracting, and ongoing management. Part of the broader procurement function.
DEFINITION
What is procurement?
ANSWER
The act of buying goods and services — encompassing the full process from need identification through supplier selection, purchase ordering, receiving, and payment. Includes both tactical purchasing and strategic sourcing.
DEFINITION
What is a specification in sourcing context?
ANSWER
A detailed description of the requirements for a product, service, material, or process — including dimensions, quality standards, performance parameters, and compliance criteria. The foundational document for supplier evaluation.
CONCEPT
What are the five dimensions of the 'QCDSR' sourcing framework?
ANSWER
Quality (specifications, standards), Cost (target cost, pricing structure), Delivery (lead time, frequency, MOQ), Service (support, EDI, VMI), and Regulatory/Compliance (environmental, labor, safety standards).
DEFINITION
What is a Statement of Work (SOW)?
ANSWER
A formal document defining the scope, deliverables, timeline, and performance standards for a service engagement. The service equivalent of a product specification — used for consulting, logistics, and outsourced service contracts.
DEFINITION
What is a Request for Proposal (RFP)?
ANSWER
A formal document inviting potential suppliers to submit proposals addressing stated requirements — including technical approach, qualifications, implementation plan, and pricing. More comprehensive than an RFQ; used for complex or strategic sourcing.
DEFINITION
What is a Request for Quote (RFQ)?
ANSWER
A document soliciting price quotations from suppliers for a specified product or service. More transactional than an RFP — focused primarily on pricing for well-defined requirements. Used for commodity or repeat purchases.
KEY DISTINCTION
What is the difference between an RFP and an RFQ?
ANSWER
RFP: used when requirements need further definition or when technical approach matters — suppliers propose how they would meet needs. RFQ: used when requirements are fully defined and price is the primary evaluation criterion.
CONCEPT
What are quality requirements in sourcing?
ANSWER
Product specifications, tolerance ranges, quality certification standards (ISO 9001, Six Sigma levels), inspection acceptance criteria, defect rate targets, and required quality management system documentation.
CONCEPT
What is Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) in a sourcing context?
ANSWER
The full cost of acquiring and using a sourced item — purchase price + transportation + customs + receiving + quality inspection + supplier management + risk cost + switching cost. TCO enables true apples-to-apples comparison across suppliers.
CONCEPT
What are delivery requirements in sourcing?
ANSWER
Lead time (time from PO to delivery), delivery frequency (daily, weekly, monthly), minimum order quantities (MOQ), delivery window accuracy, packaging and labeling requirements, and Advance Ship Notice (ASN) requirements.
DEFINITION
What is a minimum order quantity (MOQ)?
ANSWER
The smallest quantity a supplier will accept per order. Driven by production setup costs or minimum economic run quantities. High MOQs force buyers to hold more inventory; low MOQs provide flexibility but may increase unit cost.
CONCEPT
What are regulatory/compliance requirements in sourcing?
ANSWER
Environmental standards (RoHS, REACH), labor practice certifications (no child labor, fair wages), material safety data (SDS), country-of-origin requirements for customs/duty purposes, and industry-specific regulations (food safety, pharma GMP).
CONCEPT
What is a supplier qualification process?
ANSWER
A systematic evaluation of potential suppliers against defined requirements before approval to supply — typically covering quality system audit, financial stability assessment, technical capability review, and compliance verification.
KEY DISTINCTION
What is the difference between strategic sourcing and tactical purchasing?
ANSWER
Strategic sourcing: longer-term, analytical process of selecting and developing suppliers for categories that significantly affect cost or risk. Tactical purchasing: routine execution of purchase orders for already-qualified suppliers on established contracts.
