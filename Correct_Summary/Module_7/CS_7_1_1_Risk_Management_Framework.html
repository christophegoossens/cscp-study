Overview

Risk management starts with a strategy and plan. Key frameworks: COSO ERM, GRC, and ISO 31000.

  • Risk = uncertainty that could positively OR negatively affect business objectives
  • Positive uncertainty = opportunity; negative uncertainty = threat
  • SCRM process: identify → assess/classify → develop response plan → execute → update
  • Resilience levers: redundancy, visibility, responsiveness, adaptability
  • Higher risk maturity = 50% lower stock price volatility (AON/Wharton); low maturity = 17–30% losses
  • Known risks: plan proactively; unknown risks: use a reserve fund
  • Risk appetite = overall attitude (risk-seeking vs. risk-averse)
  • Risk tolerance = readiness to accept a specific threat; differs by business constraint (cost, time, quality)
  • Risk threshold = cutoff point below which risk is accepted; above = proactive response required
  • Risk management plan: specifies tools, categories, tolerances, thresholds, budget, owners, and reporting
  • COSO ERM: 8 components — internal environment, objective setting, event ID, assessment, response, controls, communication, monitoring
  • GRC = 3-legged stool: Governance + Risk Management + Compliance — all three required
  • ISO 31000: principles + iterative framework + process; NOT for certification
  • ISO 31010: supporting standard for risk assessment techniques (FMEA, Monte Carlo, brainstorming, etc.)
  • ISO Guide 73: common risk management vocabulary for consistent terminology across partners

B.The ability to anticipate, resist, absorb, and recover from supply chain disruptions
C.Holding maximum inventory
D.Using only domestic suppliers
