Overview

Supply chain maturity measures integration and coordination relative to competing supply chains. Organizations progress through five stages as technology and culture advance.

  • Stage 1 – Multiple Dysfunction: reactive, ad hoc, perceived as cost center, no clear plan
  • Stage 2 – Semifunctional Enterprise: functional areas defined, efficiency focus, reactive-efficient
  • Stage 3 – Integrated Enterprise: company-wide process focus, ERP/MRP, cross-functional; proactive-efficient
  • Stage 4 – Extended Enterprise: processes extend beyond corporate walls, early collaboration; strategic driver
  • Stage 5 – Orchestrated Supply Chain: digital transformation, AI/ML, full network visibility; consistent and systematic
  • Maturity is relative to competitors—not an absolute level
  • Organizations may show behaviors from two adjacent stages simultaneously
  • Higher stages: greater connectivity, visibility, resilience, and responsiveness
  • Specialized supply chains: humanitarian, retail, service—each differs from manufacturing model
  • Service supply chains include utilities, transport, and other intangible providers

Question 1
Which model provides a standard supply chain process framework using Plan, Source, Make, Deliver, Return?
A.CPFR
B.SCOR
C.DMAIC
D.Lean canvas
The SCOR model from ASCM provides a cross-industry standard for supply chain improvement using Plan, Source, Make, Deliver, Return, and Enable processes.
Question 2
What does supply chain maturity measure?
A.Age of physical infrastructure
B.Degree to which supply chain processes are defined, managed, and continuously improved
C.Number of supplier tiers
D.Geographic spread of the network
Maturity models measure how well processes are documented, standardized, integrated, and optimized across the organization and its partners.
Question 3
A company with siloed departments and no cross-functional collaboration is at which maturity stage?
A.Optimized
B.Integrated
C.Defined
D.Ad hoc and reactive
In the lowest maturity stage (ad hoc/reactive), there is little process definition or collaboration — each function operates independently.
Question 4
Which SCOR metric category covers delivering the right product, to the right place, at the right time, in the right condition?
A.Responsiveness
B.Agility
C.Reliability
D.Cost
Reliability in SCOR covers Perfect Order Fulfillment — delivering the correct product to the correct location at the right time in perfect condition.
Question 5
SCOR Agility metrics measure:
A.On-time delivery percentage
B.The ability to respond to unplanned changes in demand or supply
C.Total supply chain cost
D.Asset utilization
SCOR Agility metrics (upside/downside flexibility, overall value at risk) measure responsiveness to unplanned changes such as demand spikes or supply disruptions.
