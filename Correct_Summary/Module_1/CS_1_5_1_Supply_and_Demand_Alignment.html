Overview

Operations planning and control aligns supply with demand through a hierarchy from strategic plans down to production activity control. Master planning is the core process.

  • Planning hierarchy: Strategic plan (5-10 yr) → Business plan (1-3 yr) → Master plan (S&OP) → MPS → MRP → PAC
  • Master planning = demand management + production/resource planning + master scheduling
  • S&OP output: production plan (product family level, monthly rate)
  • MPS (Master Production Schedule) = weekly item-level plan derived from production plan
  • MRP uses BOM + inventory data + MPS to calculate component and material requirements
  • Resource planning (RRP): long-range capacity planning, typically 15-18 month horizon
  • Business plan: stated in dollars by product family; too-optimistic marketing projections are reconciled in S&OP
  • Strategic plan: 5-10 years; defines value proposition, competitive positioning, long-term resource strategy
  • Demand history → cleanse → statistical forecast → demand review → consensus plan → supply constraint → executive S&OP
  • Distribution requirements planning (DRP): plans inventory replenishment across the distribution network

Question 1
S&OP is primarily designed to:
A.Replace the MRP system
B.Align supply plans with demand plans at the executive level
C.Set individual production schedules
D.Negotiate supplier contracts
S&OP is a monthly cross-functional process that balances volume-level demand and supply plans, aligning operations with business strategy and financials.
Question 2
Which is NOT a typical step in the S&OP process?
A.Data gathering and demand review
B.Supply review
C.Individual SKU scheduling at machine level
D.Pre-S&OP reconciliation meeting
Individual SKU scheduling is a tactical activity handled by MPS/MRP. S&OP works at aggregate product family and volume level.
Question 3
S&OP outputs are expressed in:
A.Individual part numbers
B.Aggregate product families or units of capacity
C.Detailed shop floor schedules
D.Customer order quantities
S&OP balances supply and demand at the volume/family level, producing constrained production plans that feed down to MPS.
Question 4
When demand exceeds available supply capacity in an S&OP review, the appropriate action is:
A.Ignore the gap and run MRP
B.Accept all orders and expedite later
C.Identify options such as overtime or outsourcing and escalate trade-offs to management
D.Cancel low-priority orders without notice
S&OP identifies supply-demand imbalances and presents trade-off options for executive decision-making.
