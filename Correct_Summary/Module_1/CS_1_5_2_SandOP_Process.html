Overview

Sales and Operations Planning (S&OP) is the monthly cross-functional process that aligns demand plans, supply plans, and financial plans at an aggregate level. It produces a consensus operational plan for the business.

ASCM Definitions

📖 Definition:
Sales and Operations Planning (S&OP): A process to develop tactical plans that provide management the ability to strategically direct its businesses to achieve competitive advantage on a continuous basis by integrating customer-focused marketing plans for new and existing products with the management of the supply chain.
📖 Definition:
Integrated Business Planning (IBP): An advanced S&OP process that integrates financial planning, strategic planning, and operational planning into a single cross-functional process. IBP connects S&OP to the financial plan explicitly.

The Five-Step S&OP Process

💡 Key Point:
Standard S&OP follows five steps: 1) Data gathering and review, 2) Demand planning review, 3) Supply planning review, 4) Pre-S&OP meeting (reconciliation), 5) Executive S&OP meeting (approval and decisions).
  • Step 1: Gather and validate demand and supply data; refresh statistical forecasts
  • Step 2: Demand review — commercial team reviews forecast, incorporates market intelligence, agrees on demand plan
  • Step 3: Supply review — operations reviews capacity, identifies constraints, develops supply plan options
  • Step 4: Pre-S&OP — cross-functional team reconciles demand and supply; identifies gaps and options for executive decision
  • Step 5: Executive S&OP — leadership makes final trade-off decisions, approves plan, aligns with financial targets

S&OP Key Concepts

💡 Key Point:
S&OP operates at an aggregate level (product families, regions) over a rolling 18–24 month horizon. It drives the disaggregation into Master Production Schedule (MPS) and procurement plans at the detailed level.
  • S&OP is a monthly cadence with weekly execution reviews — not a one-time annual event
  • The S&OP output is the 'one number' company plan — one demand number, one supply number, agreed by all functions
  • Financial reconciliation: the S&OP plan must tie to the annual financial plan and budget
  • Demand-supply gaps at executive S&OP require explicit decisions — not just acceptance of misalignment
  • IBP (Integrated Business Planning) extends S&OP to explicitly link operational plans to P&L (profit and loss) impact
📖 Definition:
Rough-Cut Capacity Planning (RCCP): A high-level capacity check that validates whether the Master Production Schedule (MPS) is feasible given key resource constraints (bottlenecks, critical work centers). Used in S&OP to screen the supply plan before detailed planning.

DEFINITION
What is Sales and Operations Planning (S&OP)?
ANSWER
A monthly cross-functional process that integrates demand plans, supply plans, and financial plans at an aggregate level to produce a consensus operational plan. Involves sales, marketing, operations, finance, and executive leadership.
CONCEPT
What are the five steps of the S&OP process?
ANSWER
1) Data gathering/review, 2) Demand planning review, 3) Supply planning review, 4) Pre-S&OP meeting (reconciliation), 5) Executive S&OP meeting (approval and decisions).
CONCEPT
What happens in the demand review step of S&OP?
ANSWER
The commercial team reviews the statistical forecast, incorporates market intelligence (promotions, new listings, customer feedback), and agrees on a consensus demand plan for the planning horizon.
CONCEPT
What happens in the supply review step of S&OP?
ANSWER
Operations reviews current capacity, identifies bottlenecks and constraints, develops supply plan options to meet the demand plan, and identifies gaps with cost implications.
CONCEPT
What happens in the Pre-S&OP meeting?
ANSWER
A cross-functional team reconciles the demand plan against the supply plan, identifies remaining gaps and trade-off options, prepares issues and recommendations for the executive team to decide.
CONCEPT
What happens in the Executive S&OP meeting?
ANSWER
Senior leadership reviews unresolved issues, makes final trade-off decisions (e.g., invest in overtime vs. accept stockout risk), approves the consensus plan, and aligns with financial targets.
DEFINITION
What is Integrated Business Planning (IBP)?
ANSWER
An advanced evolution of S&OP that explicitly integrates financial planning, strategic planning, and operational planning into a single cross-functional process — connecting S&OP outputs directly to P&L impact.
CONCEPT
What is the planning horizon for S&OP?
ANSWER
Typically 18–24 months on a rolling basis. Near months are more detailed; outer months are more aggregate. S&OP operates at product family/region level — not individual SKU.
DEFINITION
What is Rough-Cut Capacity Planning (RCCP)?
ANSWER
A high-level capacity feasibility check that validates whether the MPS is achievable given key resource constraints (critical work centers, bottleneck equipment). Used in S&OP to screen the supply plan before detailed MRP.
CONCEPT
What is the 'one number' concept in S&OP?
ANSWER
The idea that S&OP produces a single agreed demand number and a single agreed supply number — one plan that all functions commit to. Eliminates the problem of sales, finance, and operations working from different numbers.
CONCEPT
How does S&OP connect to the Master Production Schedule (MPS)?
ANSWER
S&OP produces an aggregate plan at the product family level. The MPS disaggregates this plan to individual products and time buckets (weeks) and serves as the input to Material Requirements Planning (MRP).
KEY DISTINCTION
What is the difference between S&OP and IBP?
ANSWER
S&OP aligns demand and supply operationally. IBP explicitly integrates financial planning — connecting operational decisions to P&L impact, cash flow, and strategic targets. IBP makes the financial consequences of trade-offs visible.
CONCEPT
Why is S&OP described as a 'monthly cadence with weekly execution reviews'?
ANSWER
The formal S&OP review cycle is monthly (strategic adjustments). Weekly execution reviews track near-term performance against the approved plan and make tactical adjustments within the bounds set by S&OP.
CONCEPT
What does 'rolling horizon' mean in S&OP?
ANSWER
Each month, the S&OP plan extends one period further into the future — always maintaining an 18-24 month forward view. The plan is continuously updated as new information arrives, not reset from scratch annually.
CONCEPT
What is the primary output of the S&OP process?
ANSWER
A consensus operational plan that aligns demand, supply, and financial targets — authorizing production volumes, procurement commitments, and inventory plans for the planning horizon. Approved by executive leadership.
