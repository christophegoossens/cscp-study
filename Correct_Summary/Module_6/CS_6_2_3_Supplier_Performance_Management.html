Overview

Supplier performance management (SPM) is the ongoing measurement, monitoring, and development of supplier capabilities — ensuring suppliers consistently meet quality, delivery, cost, and service requirements.

ASCM Definitions

📖 Definition:
Supplier performance management (SPM): A systematic process for measuring, analyzing, and improving supplier performance — including scorecarding, regular reviews, corrective action processes, and development programs.
📖 Definition:
Supplier scorecard: A performance report card for a supplier, measuring quality, delivery, cost competitiveness, and service against agreed targets over a defined period.
📖 Definition:
Corrective Action Request (CAR): A formal request issued to a supplier to investigate and permanently fix the root cause of a performance failure — quality defect, delivery miss, or compliance issue.

Supplier Performance Metrics

💡 Key Point:
Core supplier performance metrics: 1) Quality (defect rate, PPM — Parts Per Million defects, DPMO), 2) Delivery (OTIF — On Time In Full, lead time adherence), 3) Cost (price competitiveness, cost reduction year-over-year), 4) Service (responsiveness, EDI capability, flexibility), 5) Compliance (certifications, regulatory adherence, sustainability).
  • PPM (Parts Per Million): defects per million parts received — common quality metric in automotive and electronics
  • OTIF (On-Time In-Full): percentage of orders delivered on the correct date in the correct quantity
  • Year-over-year cost reduction: contractual commitment to reduce cost annually (often 2-5%) — reflects productivity improvements
  • Corrective Action 8D report: structured 8-step problem-solving report required from suppliers after quality escapes
  • Supplier development: investing in supplier capabilities to close performance gaps

Performance Review Cadence

💡 Key Point:
Supplier review cadence should match strategic importance: strategic suppliers → monthly executive reviews; preferred suppliers → quarterly reviews; approved suppliers → annual review; conditional suppliers → monthly corrective action tracking until improved.
📖 Definition:
Business Review (QBR — Quarterly Business Review): A periodic meeting between the buying company and supplier to review performance scorecard, discuss trends, align on improvement priorities, and share future plans.

DEFINITION
What is supplier performance management (SPM)?
ANSWER
A systematic process for measuring, analyzing, and improving supplier performance — including scorecarding, regular reviews, corrective action processes, and supplier development investments.
DEFINITION
What is a supplier scorecard?
ANSWER
A performance report card measuring a supplier on agreed metrics — quality (defect rate), delivery (OTIF), cost competitiveness, service, and compliance — against targets over a defined period. Shared with suppliers in regular reviews.
DEFINITION
What is a Corrective Action Request (CAR)?
ANSWER
A formal request issued to a supplier to investigate and permanently fix the root cause of a performance failure. The supplier must provide a documented root cause analysis and corrective action plan within a defined timeframe.
CONCEPT
What are the five core supplier performance metrics?
ANSWER
1) Quality (defect rate, PPM), 2) Delivery (OTIF, lead time adherence), 3) Cost (price competitiveness, YoY reduction), 4) Service (responsiveness, flexibility, EDI capability), 5) Compliance (certifications, regulations, sustainability standards).
DEFINITION
What is PPM (Parts Per Million) in supplier quality?
ANSWER
Parts Per Million defects received from a supplier. Formula: (Defective Units / Total Units Received) × 1,000,000. Common quality metric in automotive and electronics. World-class targets: 50 PPM or less. Supplier quality issues are tracked and trended over time.
DEFINITION
What is OTIF (On-Time In-Full)?
ANSWER
On Time In Full — the percentage of purchase orders delivered on the promised delivery date in the exact quantity ordered. The primary delivery performance metric for suppliers. OTIF failures cause production stoppages and customer service failures.
DEFINITION
What is an 8D corrective action report?
ANSWER
An 8-step structured problem-solving report required from suppliers after quality escapes: 1) Use team approach, 2) Describe problem, 3) Implement containment, 4) Find root cause, 5) Develop permanent corrective action, 6) Implement, 7) Prevent recurrence, 8) Congratulate team.
DEFINITION
What is a Quarterly Business Review (QBR) with a supplier?
ANSWER
A periodic meeting between buyer and supplier to review performance scorecard, discuss trends and variances, align on improvement priorities, share future demand plans, and discuss strategic topics. Cadence should match supplier's strategic importance.
CONCEPT
What is year-over-year cost reduction in supplier contracts?
ANSWER
A contractual requirement for suppliers to reduce their prices by a set percentage annually (typically 2-5%) — reflecting expected productivity improvements and manufacturing efficiencies. Common in automotive and electronics industry long-term agreements.
DEFINITION
What is a supplier development program?
ANSWER
A structured investment in improving a supplier's capabilities — through training, technical assistance, process improvement support, or co-investment in equipment. Used when a strategic supplier has performance gaps that need to be closed.
CONCEPT
What is a conditional supplier status?
ANSWER
A supplier designation indicating performance below acceptable levels — requiring an active corrective action plan and increased monitoring frequency. The supplier must demonstrate improvement within a defined period or risk being removed from the Approved Supplier List.
CONCEPT
What does supplier compliance encompass in SPM?
ANSWER
Regulatory and standards compliance: quality certifications (ISO 9001), environmental certifications (ISO 14001), industry-specific standards (IATF 16949 for automotive), conflict minerals reporting, labor practice standards, and cybersecurity requirements.
DEFINITION
What is a supplier audit?
ANSWER
A formal assessment of a supplier's systems, processes, and capabilities — conducted at the supplier's facility or remotely. Types: quality system audit, financial audit, compliance audit, capacity audit. Results feed into the supplier scorecard and development plan.
CONCEPT
How should supplier performance management differ by supplier segment?
ANSWER
Strategic: monthly executive-level reviews, joint KPI ownership, development investment. Preferred: quarterly reviews, standard scorecard, positive recognition. Conditional: monthly corrective action tracking. Tactical: annual review, standard metrics only.
CONCEPT
What is supplier risk monitoring in SPM?
ANSWER
Ongoing monitoring of supplier financial health, geopolitical risk, natural disaster exposure, and operational risk indicators — beyond quality and delivery performance. Early warning enables proactive mitigation before disruption occurs.
