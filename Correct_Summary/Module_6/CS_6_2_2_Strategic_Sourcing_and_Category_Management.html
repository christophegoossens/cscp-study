Overview

Strategic sourcing is a systematic approach to supply market analysis, supplier selection, and contracting that maximizes total value rather than just minimizing unit price. Category management organizes spend into coherent groups for consistent strategy development.

ASCM Definitions

📖 Definition:
Strategic sourcing: A comprehensive approach to supply management that maximizes value for the organization by analyzing spend, understanding supply markets, evaluating suppliers, and developing optimal sourcing strategies — beyond price alone.
📖 Definition:
Category management: The strategic management of a product or service category as a business unit — developing a sourcing strategy, managing suppliers, and optimizing total cost for a defined spend category.
📖 Definition:
Spend analysis: The practice of collecting, classifying, and analyzing an organization's spending data to identify patterns, opportunities, and risks — the foundation of strategic sourcing.

Strategic Sourcing Process

💡 Key Point:
The seven-step strategic sourcing process: 1) Profile the category (spend, suppliers, volume), 2) Analyze the supply market (supply risks, alternatives, market structure), 3) Develop sourcing strategy (make/buy, number of suppliers, relationship type), 4) Screen and select suppliers (RFI/RFP/RFQ), 5) Negotiate and contract, 6) Transition and implement, 7) Track performance and continuous improvement.

Category Management

  • Category: a group of related products/services purchased from similar suppliers using similar procurement processes
  • Category strategy: defines how a specific category will be sourced — number of suppliers, relationship type, contract length, make/buy decision
  • Spend cube: analyzing spend by supplier, category, and business unit simultaneously to identify consolidation opportunities
  • Tail spend: the large number of small purchases that represent many transactions but limited spend — managed for efficiency rather than strategy
  • Should-cost modeling: calculating what a product or service should cost based on materials, labor, overhead, and margin — a negotiation anchor
📖 Definition:
Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) analysis: A comprehensive cost analysis including: purchase price + transportation + customs + receiving + quality + inventory carrying + supplier management + switching costs. TCO enables true comparison of sourcing options.

DEFINITION
What is strategic sourcing?
ANSWER
A comprehensive approach to supply management that maximizes total value by analyzing spend, understanding supply markets, evaluating suppliers, and developing optimal sourcing strategies — going well beyond price comparison.
DEFINITION
What is category management?
ANSWER
Strategic management of a spend category (group of related products/services) as a business unit — developing category-specific sourcing strategies, managing suppliers, and optimizing total cost across the entire category.
DEFINITION
What is spend analysis?
ANSWER
Collecting, classifying, and analyzing all organizational spending data by supplier, category, and business unit. The foundation of strategic sourcing — identifies savings opportunities, supply risks, and consolidation possibilities.
CONCEPT
What are the seven steps of strategic sourcing?
ANSWER
1) Profile the category, 2) Analyze supply market, 3) Develop sourcing strategy, 4) Screen and select suppliers (RFI/RFP), 5) Negotiate and contract, 6) Transition and implement, 7) Track performance and continuously improve.
DEFINITION
What is a should-cost model?
ANSWER
A bottom-up cost estimate calculating what a product should cost based on materials, labor, overhead, and reasonable profit margin — built independently by the buyer. Used as an objective negotiation anchor and to challenge supplier pricing.
CONCEPT
What is a spend cube analysis?
ANSWER
Analyzing spend simultaneously across three dimensions: by supplier, by category, and by business unit. Reveals consolidation opportunities (multiple BUs buying from the same supplier separately), maverick spend, and category strategy gaps.
DEFINITION
What is tail spend?
ANSWER
The large number of small-value purchases that collectively represent many transactions but a small percentage of total spend (typically 80% of transactions, 20% of spend). Managed for efficiency using P-cards, catalogs, or supplier-managed replenishment rather than strategic sourcing.
CONCEPT
What is supply market analysis in strategic sourcing?
ANSWER
Studying the external supply market for a category — number and concentration of suppliers, supply risk factors, commodity price trends, technology trends, and alternative sources. Informs the category strategy and negotiation approach.
KEY DISTINCTION
What is the difference between strategic sourcing and tactical purchasing?
ANSWER
Strategic sourcing: periodic (annual or multi-year) analysis and selection of suppliers and contracts for a category. Tactical purchasing: day-to-day execution of purchase orders against established contracts. Strategic sourcing creates the rules; purchasing executes them.
CONCEPT
What is Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) in strategic sourcing?
ANSWER
Full cost of a sourced item: purchase price + transportation + customs + receiving + quality inspection + inventory carrying + supplier management overhead + switching cost. TCO analysis prevents selecting a 'cheap' supplier who is actually expensive when all costs are included.
CONCEPT
What is competitive bidding in strategic sourcing?
ANSWER
Issuing a formal RFP/RFQ to multiple suppliers simultaneously and selecting based on best overall value. Creates price competition and transparency. Best for leverage items where switching costs are low and multiple qualified suppliers exist.
DEFINITION
What is a reverse auction in procurement?
ANSWER
A competitive bidding event where suppliers bid against each other in real time (unlike traditional bidding where bids are sealed) — driving prices down. Best for commodity items where price is the primary differentiator.
CONCEPT
What is category strategy and what does it define?
ANSWER
A documented plan for how a spend category will be managed: number of suppliers, relationship type (transactional vs. strategic), contract structure, make/buy decision, sustainability requirements, and performance targets. Updated annually through the strategic sourcing process.
DEFINITION
What is maverick spending?
ANSWER
Purchases made outside of approved contracts or preferred suppliers — bypassing negotiated rates and volume commitments. Increases total cost (no volume discount), creates compliance risk, and undermines supplier relationships. Controlled through P2P system controls and policy enforcement.
CONCEPT
What is the benefit of consolidating spend across business units for a category?
ANSWER
Consolidating volume from multiple BUs into a single category contract creates leverage for pricing — the combined volume justifies better rates than each BU could negotiate separately. Also reduces supplier management complexity.
