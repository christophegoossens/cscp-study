6.2.1 Overview: SRM and Supplier Segmentation
Supplier relationship management (SRM): a comprehensive approach to managing an enterprise’s interactions with the organizations that supply the goods and services it uses. The goal of SRM is to streamline and make more effective the processes between an enterprise and its suppliers.

SRM methodology structures and supports relationships with suppliers to:

  • Reduce procurement and inventory costs
  • Support a customer-centric business delivering customization and quality on time
  • Continuously improve supply processes
Exhibit 6-9: Supply Chain Community
Supplier Segmentation

Supplier segmentation creates categories of suppliers to manage them appropriately — just as you segment customers, you segment suppliers. It is a key tool for a responsive supply chain.

Six forms of supplier segmentation:

  • Product or service type: general category of goods/services supplied
  • Ideal relationship type: what partnership would this supplier be suited for?
  • Supplier capabilities: for niche/differentiation strategies — focus on unique capabilities
  • Customization vs. standardization: responsive/custom vs. efficient/standard
  • Level of innovation: suppliers who contribute creative input to product design
  • Lead times: grouping by lead time for scheduling and performance tracking
SRM applies most to higher-level buyer-supplier relationships. It does not necessarily replace arm’s-length market purchasing for commodity items.
Processes Enabled by SRM
  • Collaborative design
  • Collaborative planning, forecasting, and replenishment (CPFR)
  • Lean and Just-in-Time
  • 3PL/4PL partnerships
  • Supplier- and vendor-managed inventory (VMI)
  • Quick response programs
  • Distributor integration
  • Collaborative transportation management
  • Supplier co-location
Supplier co-location: locating a supplier or multiple suppliers within the buyer’s facility. Benefit to buyer: highly integrated operations. Benefit to supplier: larger and more stable volumes. Co-location may require licensing agreements (patents, software, revenue-sharing).
SRM Technologies

SRM software streamlines connections between purchasers and suppliers, increases efficiency, and enables better supplier selection and performance monitoring.

SRM system components:

  • Strategic sourcing and RFQ/ITT submission and analysis
  • Procurement via internet trade exchanges or auctions
  • Collaborative product design and planning
  • Purchasing/supplier scheduling via EDI or portals
  • Catalog management, category management, supplier segmentation
  • Supplier databases and rating systems

Web-enabled SRM (Exhibit 6-10) has three interfaces: Internal (ERP, procurement history, open orders, accounting, purchasing planning, supplier performance), External supplier interfaces (EDI/internet links), and SRM Analytics (visibility/DSS, strategic sourcing analysis, spend analysis, value analysis, performance analysis).

Exhibit 6-10: Web-Enabled SRM

SRM Analytics types:

  • Spend analysis: who are we buying from, what, when, and at what price?
  • Value analysis: identifies value-added vs. non-value-added procurement activities
  • Performance analysis: measures cost savings, quality, delivery, price, compliance

TERM / CONCEPT
What is spend analysis in SRM?
ANSWER
A strategic application that identifies who the organization is buying from, what they’re purchasing from each supplier, when and how much — enabling better negotiation and consolidation decisions.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is value analysis in SRM?
ANSWER
Finding the purchasing, financing, and delivery processes that are value-added and eliminating non-value-added activities — to reduce total cost of procurement.
TERM / CONCEPT
Exhibit 6-9: Supply Chain Community — what does it show?
ANSWER
If the supply chain is viewed as a series of value-adding actions, SRM positions the buying organization within a community of suppliers — emphasizing the relational and collaborative nature of supply chain membership.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is SRM procurement?
ANSWER
The goal is to streamline procurement of goods/services necessary to produce products and run the organization — using internet trade exchanges, auctions, EDI, and catalog management tools.
TERM / CONCEPT
Why does supplier segmentation increase responsiveness without significantly increasing costs?
ANSWER
It helps organizations with complex supplier networks focus partnership-level attention only on suppliers where it creates the most strategic value, while managing commodity suppliers with less intensive approaches.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is SRM services?
ANSWER
SRM services allow strategic sourcing searches beyond arm’s-length transactions — including supplier development, collaborative design, collaborative planning, member portals, security, and workflow tools.
