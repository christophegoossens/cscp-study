Overview

CRM and SRM are two sides of the same coin — managing sell-side and buy-side relationships through collaboration, communication, and continuous organizational learning.

  • CRM and SRM integrate to create seamless sell-side and buy-side relationship management
  • CRM collaboration: customer focus groups, VOC, product co-development with customers
  • SRM collaboration: supplier reps in product design, shared information, aligned processes
  • Virtual organization: short-term alliances acting as single entity; uses POS data to enable pull model
  • Effective partnerships require: shared risks, resources, rewards, vision, and values
  • Collaboration levels: transactional with info sharing → shared processes/partnership → strategic alliance
  • Barrier to collaboration: suboptimization — partners optimize locally at expense of supply chain whole
  • Communication process: sender → encoded message → medium → receiver decodes → feedback loop
  • RACI chart: clarifies Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed for each task
  • Communication management plan: identify audience, channels, craft message, gather feedback, close loop
  • Culture = shared system of values, beliefs, and attitudes; influences business behavior globally
  • Hofstede's cultural dimensions: model from IBM research across 64 countries; essential for global supply chain
  • Evolving customer relationship: price focus → value recognition → dependency on provider
  • Customer-centric business creation: 5-step process from redefining organization to measuring success

Question 1
CPFR involves:
A.A buyer creating forecasts without supplier input
B.Buyers and suppliers jointly developing forecasts and replenishment plans to improve synchronization
C.Government regulation of data sharing
D.Third-party forecasting only
CPFR is a collaborative business process where trading partners jointly create demand forecasts and synchronize replenishment plans.
Question 2
In VMI, replenishment is initiated by:
A.The buyer issuing purchase orders
B.The supplier monitoring buyer inventory and replenishing within agreed parameters
C.The 3PL
D.The government
In VMI, the supplier monitors buyer stock and replenishes to agreed min/max targets without the buyer issuing purchase orders.
Question 3
The primary benefit of supply chain collaboration is:
A.Eliminating all contracts
B.Reducing information asymmetry and bullwhip effect, improving forecast accuracy and inventory efficiency
C.Increasing the number of suppliers
D.Reducing quality standards
Collaboration reduces information gaps that cause bullwhip amplification — shared demand signals lead to less safety stock and fewer stockouts.
Question 4
Demand-driven VMI with POS data sharing enables:
A.Traditional purchase order processing
B.Supplier replenishment aligned with actual consumption rather than lagged order signals
C.Manual inventory counting
D.One-time purchases only
POS-driven VMI aligns supplier production and delivery with actual consumption rather than lagged order signals.
