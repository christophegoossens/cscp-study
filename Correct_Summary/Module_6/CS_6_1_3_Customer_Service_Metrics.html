6.1.3 Overview: Customer Service Metrics

Customer service metrics measure what matters to customers — identified through interviews, questionnaires, and benchmarking. Metrics exist at two levels:

  • Strategic metrics: long-term, aggregated assessments (e.g., perfect order fulfillment, customer satisfaction)
  • Operational metrics: detailed measurements driving day-to-day performance
Perfect order fulfillment (SCOR metric): measures every step of a customer’s experience from order placement to delivery — delivered on time, in full, undamaged, with correct documentation.
Key Availability Metrics
Customer service ratio (fill rate): 1) A measure of delivery performance of finished goods, usually expressed as a percentage. In a make-to-stock company, the percentage of orders filled from stock. 2) The ratio of the total orders that can be filled from inventory.
On-time in full (OTIF): a delivery scoring system in which a target delivery goal (usually expressed as a percentage) is set — the deliverer must deliver on the promised date AND in the full quantity ordered.

Fill rate variations:

  • Unit fill rate: % of items delivered vs. ordered
  • Line item fill rate: % of line items delivered vs. ordered
  • Monetary value fill rate: % of monetary value delivered vs. ordered

Stockout frequency measures: % items in stockout; monetary value in stockout; average age of stockout; average time to recover.

Lead Time Monitoring
Cycle time: 1) In industrial engineering, the time between completion of two discrete units of production. 2) In materials management, the length of time from when material is ordered until it is received and ready for use.

Lead time monitoring covers four dimensions:

  • Speed of performance: elapsed time from order placement to delivery and ready for use
  • Consistency (delivery consistency): number of times cycles meet planned time; variance in lead times; % delivered in quoted lead time
  • Flexibility: ability to accommodate unexpected or unusual customer requests
  • Malfunction recovery: contingency plans in place; ability to source out-of-stock items from alternate facilities
Supply chain cycle time: time it would take to fill a customer order if inventory levels were zero — the sum of the longest lead times for each stage in the supply chain.

CRM Performance Management

CRM effectiveness must be measured across four areas:

  • Customer service: response to inquiries (speed + accuracy), order processing (cycle time, % on time, % accurate), level of service (right product/quantity/time/place), product/service quality (defect rate, warranty claims)
  • Customer satisfaction: expectation setting + fulfillment; meeting or exceeding expectations = satisfaction; falling short = dissatisfaction
Customer satisfaction measurement methods: VOC interviews/focus groups, transaction questionnaires, monthly/quarterly questionnaires, performance reviews with key customers, monitoring/responding to online negative feedback.
Communication management plan: a document that describes the communications needs and expectations for the project, how and in what format information will be communicated, when and where each communication will be made, and who is responsible.
Stakeholder relationship management: addressing and managing the competing priorities, needs, and concerns of internal and external stakeholders in a proactive and sustained manner, resulting in successful project delivery.

TERM / CONCEPT
What is perfect order fulfillment?
ANSWER
A SCOR metric measuring every step of the customer experience: delivered on time, in full, undamaged, correctly documented — all conditions must be met simultaneously.
TERM / CONCEPT
Strategic vs. operational customer service metrics — distinction?
ANSWER
Strategic: long-term, aggregated assessments for executive reporting (e.g., perfect order %, customer satisfaction score). Operational: detailed measurements driving day-to-day execution (e.g., fill rate, order cycle time, response time).
TERM / CONCEPT
What is partner relationship management (PRM)?
ANSWER
A system where an organization better controls how a customer is treated throughout the customer experience by leveraging partner data and coordination — improving service quality across all delivery channels.

OTIF (On Time In Full) measures:
A.On-time production starts
B.Percentage of orders delivered on the promised date AND in the full quantity ordered

OTIF = % orders where BOTH conditions are met: delivered on time AND in full quantity.
Question 2
A 95% case fill rate means:
A.5% of orders are cancelled
B.95% of ordered quantity was available for immediate shipment from stock
C.95% of orders were on time
D.5% of products were defective
Case fill rate = % of cases (units) ordered that were shipped without backorder.
Question 3
Order cycle time measures:
A.Time from supplier payment to cash collection
B.Average time between customer order placement and delivery
C.Cash-to-cash cycle
D.Order fill rate
Order cycle time = time from order receipt to delivery at the customer's location.
Question 4
Customer complaint rate helps:
A.Increase complaints
B.Identify quality and service failure trends requiring corrective action
C.Replace OTIF measurement
D.Measure warehouse productivity
Complaint rate tracks quality/service failures experienced by customers — high rates signal systemic issues.
