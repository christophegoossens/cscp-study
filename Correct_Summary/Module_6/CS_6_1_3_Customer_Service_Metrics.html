Overview

Customer service metrics measure how well the supply chain meets customer expectations for delivery, quality, responsiveness, and communication. They are the external-facing outcome measures of internal supply chain performance.

ASCM Definitions

📖 Definition:
Customer service: The ability to consistently satisfy or exceed customer expectations for delivery, quality, and responsiveness. From a supply chain perspective: the right product, right place, right time, right condition, right price.
📖 Definition:
Perfect Order Fulfillment (POF): The percentage of orders delivered: on time, in full, damage-free, with accurate documentation, to the correct location. The primary customer service SCOR metric.
📖 Definition:
Fill rate: The fraction of customer demand immediately fulfilled from available inventory. Order fill rate = complete orders fulfilled / total orders. Line fill rate = order lines fulfilled / total lines.
📖 Definition:
On-Time Delivery (OTD): The percentage of customer orders delivered on or before the committed delivery date.

Key Customer Service Metrics

💡 Key Point:
Customer service measurement framework: 1) Order Entry/Processing metrics (order cycle time, order accuracy), 2) Inventory metrics (fill rate, availability), 3) Transportation metrics (on-time delivery, damage rate), 4) Overall metrics (Perfect Order Fulfillment, customer satisfaction score).
  • Order fulfillment cycle time: elapsed time from order receipt to delivery at customer location
  • Back-order rate: percentage of orders that cannot be fulfilled immediately due to stockouts
  • Returns rate: percentage of delivered orders returned by customers — a quality and accuracy indicator
  • Invoice accuracy: percentage of invoices issued without errors — errors delay payment and damage relationships
  • Damage-free delivery rate: percentage of deliveries arriving without damage
💡 Key Point:
Service Level Agreement (SLA): a formal commitment defining measurable customer service standards — delivery lead time, fill rate, response time. SLA breaches trigger penalties; SLA compliance is tracked and reported regularly.
  • Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT): direct survey measure of satisfaction with a specific transaction or overall experience
  • Net Promoter Score (NPS): likelihood to recommend — leading indicator of retention and growth
  • Customer complaint rate: number of complaints per 1000 orders — tracks quality and service failures

CONCEPT
What is the 'seven Rs' definition of customer service?
ANSWER
The right product, in the right quantity, in the right condition, at the right place, at the right time, for the right customer, at the right cost. A comprehensive framework for defining complete customer service.
DEFINITION
What is Perfect Order Fulfillment (POF)?
ANSWER
The percentage of orders delivered: on time + in full + damage-free + with accurate documentation + to the correct location. The primary SCOR customer service reliability metric. All five conditions must be met for an order to count as 'perfect.'
DEFINITION
What is fill rate and why does it matter?
ANSWER
The fraction of customer demand filled immediately from available inventory. Order fill rate = complete orders filled ÷ total orders. Line fill rate = order lines filled ÷ total lines. Low fill rate = stockouts harming customer service.
DEFINITION
What is On-Time Delivery (OTD)?
ANSWER
The percentage of customer orders delivered on or before the committed delivery date. A primary customer satisfaction metric. Measured against customer-requested date or MABD (Must Arrive By Date).
DEFINITION
What is a Service Level Agreement (SLA)?
ANSWER
A formal commitment defining measurable customer service standards — delivery lead time, fill rate, response time. SLA breaches may trigger financial penalties. SLA compliance is tracked and reported regularly.
DEFINITION
What is order fulfillment cycle time?
ANSWER
The elapsed time from order receipt to delivery at the customer location. Includes: order entry, order processing, picking/packing, shipping, and transit time. Shorter cycle time = more responsive service.
DEFINITION
What is back-order rate?
ANSWER
The percentage of orders that cannot be fulfilled immediately from stock due to stockouts. High back-order rate indicates demand-supply misalignment. Must be communicated to customers with realistic recovery dates.
DEFINITION
What is a returns rate as a customer service metric?
ANSWER
The percentage of delivered orders returned by customers — indicating quality defects, wrong items shipped, or customer dissatisfaction. High returns rate is costly (reverse logistics, restocking) and signals service failure.
DEFINITION
What is customer satisfaction score (CSAT)?
ANSWER
A survey metric measuring customer satisfaction with a specific transaction or overall experience — usually a 1-5 or 1-10 scale. Collected after delivery, service interaction, or periodically. Leading indicator of retention and referrals.
DEFINITION
What is invoice accuracy in customer service?
ANSWER
The percentage of customer invoices issued without errors — correct prices, quantities, discount terms. Invoice errors delay payment collection, require dispute resolution, and damage the customer relationship.
KEY DISTINCTION
How does POF differ from OTIF?
ANSWER
OTIF (On Time In Full) measures only delivery timing and completeness. POF is more comprehensive: OTIF + damage-free + correct documentation + correct location. A single POF failure counts the order as imperfect, even if OTIF is met.
DEFINITION
What is a MABD (Must Arrive By Date)?
ANSWER
A customer-specified delivery deadline — the product must arrive at the customer DC by this date or penalties apply. Commonly used by large retailers (Walmart, Target, Amazon) who impose OTIF fines for late or short deliveries.
DEFINITION
What is the damage-free delivery rate?
ANSWER
The percentage of shipments arriving without damage to the product or packaging. Damage requires returns, replacements, and claims — increasing cost and degrading the customer experience. Often measured as a component of POF.
CONCEPT
What is a customer service scorecard?
ANSWER
A dashboard reporting key customer service metrics (OTD, fill rate, POF, returns rate, NPS) for a customer or segment. Shared with the customer in key account reviews to demonstrate performance and identify improvement opportunities.
CONCEPT
What is the relationship between supply chain performance and customer service metrics?
ANSWER
Supply chain performance drives customer service outcomes: forecast accuracy → fill rate and availability; supplier OTD → production continuity → on-time delivery; warehouse accuracy → order accuracy and damage-free delivery. Internal process metrics cascade to external customer metrics.
