6.1.1 Overview: CRM Philosophy and Segmentation

CRM is a philosophy that recognizes all companies are part service companies — quality of relationship with the customer is a key differentiator.

Customer Relationship Management (CRM): a marketing philosophy based on putting the customer first. Involves collection and analysis of information designed for sales and marketing decision support, and the execution of transactions and services to retain and expand the customer base.
  • CRM is broad (covers every customer interaction) and deep (focuses on long-term profitability of each relationship)
  • Customer-centric organizations: easy to do business with, add value, innovate, design from the customer’s perspective, and share customer insights throughout the supply chain
Customer Segmentation
Customer segmentation: the practice of dividing a customer base into groups of individuals that are similar in specific ways relevant to marketing. Traditional segmentation variables include geographic, demographic, psychographic, and behavioral data.
  • Intermediate customers: not at end of supply chain (e.g., manufacturer’s customers are retailers)
  • Ultimate customers: final recipients of the product or service
  • Segmentation by: customer value, customer needs, preferred contact channel
Customer driven: a company’s response to meet customer expectations and requirements.
Segmentation by customer value: Pareto principle — roughly 20% of customers generate 80% of profit. High-value customers warrant more investment to retain. Exhibit 6-1: Pareto Effect in Customer Segments

Sources of Customer Information & Voice of the Customer
  • Transaction records, sales representatives, service representatives, distribution points, purchased data
Voice of the Customer (VOC): actual customer descriptions in words for the functions and features customers desire for goods and services. In a broader context, a research and measurement tool to identify unmet needs that businesses can address.

VOC lets customers talk freely, reveals surprises, and allows organizations to anticipate needs.

CRM Strategies by Customer Segment & Relationship Type

Customer segment strategies: demographic/attitudinal, value-based, service-minded, retail, and B2B.

B2B customer priorities: complementary core competencies, knowledge of their business requirements, continuous improvement suggestions.

Four Customer Relationship Types:

  • Prospective customers: potential customers — focus on market research, pricing, promotions
  • Vulnerable customers: at risk of leaving; targeted retention programs
  • Win-back customers: ex-customers to be reacquired quickly
  • Loyal customers: least vulnerable; cross-sell and up-sell opportunities
Churn: the process of customers changing their buying preferences because they find better and/or cheaper products and services from another company. A high churn rate indicates poor retention.
CRM campaign impact study (PricewaterhouseCoopers): reducing churn in vulnerable customers = 15–20% profit increase; winning back lost customers = 10–20%; cross/up-selling loyal customers = 2–3%; increasing prospective customers = 3–4%.

What are the five characteristics of customer-centric organizations?
ANSWER
1) Easy to do business with (anytime, anywhere); 2) Add value to products/services; 3) Innovative in design, marketing, and delivery; 4) Design contact points from customer perspective; 5) Share customer insights across the supply chain.
TERM / CONCEPT
Difference between intermediate and ultimate customers?
ANSWER
Intermediate customers are not at the end of the supply chain (e.g., retailers for a manufacturer). Ultimate customers are the final recipients of the product or service.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the five key sources of customer information for CRM?
ANSWER
1) Transaction records; 2) Sales representatives; 3) Service representatives; 4) Distribution points; 5) Purchased data (surveys, database marketing companies).
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the four B2B customer priorities in CRM?
ANSWER
1) Complementary core competencies; 2) Knowledge of the customer’s business requirements; 3) Continuous improvement suggestions; 4) Extensive sales/service training focused on B2B needs.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is CRM’s philosophy regarding service?
ANSWER
All companies are at least part service companies — quality of customer relationship is a key competitive differentiator in an era where product quality is increasingly taken for granted.
TERM / CONCEPT
How do loyal customers reduce marketing costs? (CRM principle)
ANSWER
Acquiring new customers is front-loaded and expensive. Lifetime customers lower total marketing costs because retention costs less than acquisition, and they become more profitable over time through cross/up-selling.
