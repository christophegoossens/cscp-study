Overview

Customer Relationship Management (CRM) is the strategy, process, and technology for managing a company's interactions with current and potential customers — with the goal of improving customer satisfaction, loyalty, and profitability.

ASCM Definitions

📖 Definition:
Customer Relationship Management (CRM): A business strategy that uses technology and business processes to manage and analyze customer interactions and data throughout the customer lifecycle — with the goal of improving customer service, retention, and sales growth.
📖 Definition:
Customer segmentation: The division of a company's customer base into groups that share similar characteristics — buying behavior, profitability, geography, or industry. Enables differentiated service and management strategies.
📖 Definition:
Customer lifetime value (CLV): The total net profit a company can expect to earn from a customer over the entire duration of their relationship. High-CLV customers deserve more investment in relationship maintenance.

CRM Strategy Principles

💡 Key Point:
The Pareto principle applies to customers: approximately 20% of customers generate 80% of revenue (and often a larger share of profit). CRM enables identifying, serving, and retaining those high-value customers.
  • Transactional CRM: focuses on individual transactions — price, availability, and convenience. Lower switching cost for the customer
  • Collaborative CRM: shared data and processes between buyer and supplier — improving forecasting, product development, and joint planning
  • Analytical CRM: using data analytics to understand customer behavior, predict needs, and improve targeting
  • Operational CRM: automation of customer-facing processes — sales force automation, marketing automation, customer service

Customer Relationship Types

💡 Key Point:
Relationship types from least to most integrated: Transactional → Vendor → Preferred Supplier → Partner → Strategic Alliance. Each level requires more investment but provides more value and stability.
📖 Definition:
Key account management (KAM): A structured approach to managing relationships with the company's most important customers — assigning dedicated account teams, developing joint business plans, and creating mutual value beyond price negotiation.
  • Customer satisfaction: the degree to which customer expectations are met or exceeded
  • Customer loyalty: repeat purchasing and advocacy driven by satisfaction, switching cost, or emotional connection
  • Net Promoter Score (NPS): a measure of customer advocacy — likelihood to recommend on a 0-10 scale

DEFINITION
What is Customer Relationship Management (CRM)?
ANSWER
A business strategy and technology for managing customer interactions throughout the lifecycle — improving customer service, retention, and profitability. Encompasses strategy, process, and supporting software.
DEFINITION
What is customer segmentation?
ANSWER
Dividing the customer base into groups with similar characteristics — buying behavior, profitability, geography, or needs. Enables differentiated service levels, pricing, and management strategies for different segments.
DEFINITION
What is customer lifetime value (CLV)?
ANSWER
The total net profit expected from a customer over the entire duration of the relationship. High-CLV customers justify more investment in service, relationship management, and retention. Used to prioritize where to invest CRM resources.
CONCEPT
What is the CRM application of the Pareto principle?
ANSWER
Approximately 20% of customers generate 80% of revenue (and typically an even larger share of profit). CRM helps identify these high-value customers so they can receive prioritized service and relationship investment.
KEY DISTINCTION
What is the difference between transactional and collaborative CRM?
ANSWER
Transactional CRM: focuses on individual purchase transactions (price, availability). Collaborative CRM: integrates buyer-seller processes (shared data, joint forecasting, collaborative product development) — deeper relationship, higher switching cost.
DEFINITION
What is key account management (KAM)?
ANSWER
A structured approach to managing the most important customer relationships — dedicating teams, developing joint business plans, creating mutual value beyond transactions. Used for high-CLV, strategic customers.
CONCEPT
What are the five customer relationship types from least to most integrated?
ANSWER
Transactional → Vendor → Preferred Supplier → Partner → Strategic Alliance. Each step requires more investment (dedicated teams, shared systems, joint planning) but provides greater value, stability, and mutual dependency.
DEFINITION
What is Net Promoter Score (NPS)?
ANSWER
A customer advocacy metric — asks 'How likely are you to recommend us to a friend?' on a 0-10 scale. Promoters (9-10) minus Detractors (0-6) = NPS. Ranges from -100 to +100. Used as a leading indicator of retention and growth.
KEY DISTINCTION
What is customer satisfaction vs. customer loyalty?
ANSWER
Satisfaction: degree to which expectations are met or exceeded (a moment-in-time measure). Loyalty: repeat purchasing and advocacy driven by satisfaction, switching cost, or emotional connection (a behavioral outcome over time). Satisfaction drives loyalty, but doesn't guarantee it.
DEFINITION
What is analytical CRM?
ANSWER
Using data analytics to understand customer behavior, predict future needs, identify at-risk customers, and improve targeting of sales and marketing efforts. Turns customer transaction data into actionable insights.
DEFINITION
What is operational CRM?
ANSWER
Automation of customer-facing business processes — Sales Force Automation (SFA), marketing automation, customer service/helpdesk ticketing. Improves efficiency and consistency of customer interactions.
CONCEPT
What is the supply chain implication of CRM?
ANSWER
CRM provides demand signals that improve supply chain planning accuracy — customer purchase history, planned promotions, and collaborative forecasts. Better demand intelligence reduces forecast error, inventory, and stockouts.
CONCEPT
What is customer profitability analysis?
ANSWER
Calculating the actual profit generated by each customer segment or individual account — revenue minus cost to serve (order processing, transportation, returns, special requests). Some high-revenue customers are actually low-profit due to high service costs.
DEFINITION
What is Voice of the Customer (VoC)?
ANSWER
A structured process for capturing customers' stated and unstated needs, preferences, and expectations — through surveys, interviews, focus groups, and complaints analysis. Used to align product and service design with customer requirements.
CONCEPT
What is a customer scorecard in supply chain?
ANSWER
A performance dashboard showing service metrics for a specific customer: on-time delivery, fill rate, order accuracy, complaint rate, and responsiveness. Used to demonstrate performance and identify improvement areas in key account management.
