Overview

SC strategy must align with organizational strategy and clearly define the value proposition, core competencies, and capabilities required to support business goals.

  • SC goal: deliver goods/services where/when wanted at optimal quality and cost
  • Value chain ≠ supply chain: value chain includes broader activities (R&D, marketing) not always part of SC
  • Value stream: all processes from concept to customer delivery
  • Value stream mapping (VSM): lean tool visualizing as-is and to-be material/information flows
  • Three types of value: financial, customer, and social
  • Financial value: ROI, ROA; cost reductions must yield net system-wide gains — not just shift costs
  • Customer value dimensions: quality, delivery reliability, flexibility, innovation, cost
  • Social value: sustainable products, ethical practices, community impact
  • SC capabilities to assess: organizational design, processes, systems/technology, human resources, metrics
  • Effectiveness = doing the right things; efficiency = doing things right — both required
  • Efficiency = actual output ÷ standard output; capacity depends on efficiency
  • Core SC competencies: customer service excellence, systemwide resource efficiency, leveraging partner competencies
  • Customer service = availability + operational performance + reliability
  • SC metrics benchmarked vs.: desired performance, competitor, industry average, world-class
  • SC professionals must see the chain as one continuous entity and manage cross-functional relationships
  • Organizational design: structure enabling or hindering SC management; covers authority, communication, incentives

Although many would assume that a supply chain is, in fact, a value chain—at least if well managed—others may draw a distinction between the two. According to the ASCM Supply Chain Dictionary, the value chain is made up of “the functions within a company that add value to the goods or services that the organization sells to customers and for which it receives payment.” A supply chain then strings the value chains of each entity together to satisfy market demands for specific products or services.
