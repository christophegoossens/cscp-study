Overview

Supply chain strategy must align with and support the overall business strategy. Different competitive strategies demand different supply chain capabilities — what works for a cost leader does not work for a differentiator.

ASCM Definitions

📖 Definition:
Business strategy: The plan an organization develops to achieve its long-term goals and competitive objectives — defining how the organization will compete in its chosen markets.
📖 Definition:
Supply chain strategy: The long-term plan for how the supply chain will support the business strategy — defining the supply chain's structure, capabilities, and trade-offs to achieve competitive advantage.
📖 Definition:
Competitive advantage: A condition that enables a company to operate in a more efficient or higher-quality manner than others in the industry, resulting in superior business performance.

Strategic Alignment

💡 Key Point:
Michael Porter's three generic competitive strategies: 1) Cost Leadership (lowest cost producer — supply chain must be highly efficient), 2) Differentiation (unique products/service — supply chain must be responsive and flexible), 3) Focus (serving a narrow segment — supply chain is customized to that segment).
💡 Key Point:
Marshall Fisher's framework: Functional products (stable demand, long lifecycle) → use Efficient supply chains. Innovative products (uncertain demand, short lifecycle) → use Responsive supply chains.
  • Efficient supply chain: minimizes cost — lean, high utilization, long runs, minimal excess capacity
  • Responsive supply chain: maximizes speed and flexibility — buffer capacity, faster transport, higher inventory
  • Hybrid strategy: some products/segments may require both — manage portfolio approach

Strategic Trade-offs

💡 Key Point:
The fundamental supply chain strategic trade-off: Cost vs. Service. Maximizing service (high inventory, fast transport, excess capacity) costs more. Minimizing cost (lean inventory, slow transport, high utilization) reduces service capability. The strategy must define where on this spectrum to operate.
  • Long-term vs. short-term: operational efficiency (short-term) must not compromise supply chain resilience and adaptability (long-term)
  • Centralization vs. decentralization: centralized inventory reduces total stock but extends lead times; decentralized is opposite
  • Standardization vs. customization: standardized products/processes reduce cost; customization improves fit but increases complexity
  • Make vs. buy: control vs. flexibility trade-off in vertical integration decisions
📖 Definition:
Order winners vs. order qualifiers: Order qualifiers are the minimum criteria to be considered by customers (e.g., quality, basic delivery). Order winners are the criteria that actually win the business — differentiators. Supply chain must excel at order winners.

DEFINITION
What is supply chain strategy?
ANSWER
The long-term plan for how the supply chain will support the business strategy — defining structure, capabilities, and trade-offs to achieve competitive advantage. Must be explicitly aligned with the business strategy.
CONCEPT
What are Porter's three generic competitive strategies and their SC implications?
ANSWER
Cost Leadership: supply chain must be efficient and low-cost. Differentiation: supply chain must be responsive, flexible, and capable of supporting unique offerings. Focus: supply chain is tailored to the specific needs of the target segment.
CONCEPT
What is Fisher's framework for matching supply chain to product type?
ANSWER
Functional products (stable demand, long lifecycle) → Efficient supply chain (low cost, high utilization). Innovative products (uncertain demand, short lifecycle) → Responsive supply chain (speed, flexibility, buffer capacity).
DEFINITION
What is an efficient supply chain?
ANSWER
A supply chain designed to minimize cost — high asset utilization, lean inventory, long production runs, low-cost transportation. Best for functional products with stable, predictable demand. Examples: commodity food products, chemicals.
DEFINITION
What is a responsive supply chain?
ANSWER
A supply chain designed for speed and flexibility — buffer capacity, fast transport modes, higher inventory, shorter lead times. Best for innovative products with volatile, uncertain demand. Examples: fashion, consumer electronics.
CONCEPT
What is the fundamental supply chain strategic trade-off?
ANSWER
Cost vs. Service/Responsiveness. Maximizing service (high inventory, fast transport, excess capacity) costs more. Minimizing cost (lean inventory, slow transport, full utilization) reduces responsiveness. Strategy must explicitly position on this spectrum.
DEFINITION
What are order winners vs. order qualifiers?
ANSWER
Order qualifiers: minimum criteria to be considered by customers (basic quality, acceptable price, delivery availability). Order winners: differentiating criteria that actually win the business. Supply chain must excel at order winners for the target market.
DEFINITION
What is competitive advantage?
ANSWER
A condition enabling a company to operate more efficiently or at higher quality than competitors — resulting in superior business performance. Supply chain can be a source of competitive advantage through cost, speed, reliability, or flexibility.
CONCEPT
What is the make-vs.-buy strategic decision in supply chain strategy?
ANSWER
Deciding which activities to perform internally (make) vs. source from external partners (buy). The answer depends on core competency alignment, total cost, control requirements, and strategic risk. Both vertical integration and outsourcing create different supply chain capabilities.
CONCEPT
What is supply chain agility as a strategic capability?
ANSWER
The ability to respond quickly to unplanned changes in demand or supply — a strategic differentiator for industries with volatile demand. Requires spare capacity, flexible supplier relationships, and technology for rapid replanning.
KEY DISTINCTION
What is the difference between supply chain efficiency and effectiveness?
ANSWER
Efficiency: doing things at minimum cost (operational focus — lean, utilization). Effectiveness: doing the right things to achieve strategic goals (strategic focus — delivering what customers value). Strategy must balance both.
CONCEPT
How does business strategy cascade into supply chain strategy?
ANSWER
Business strategy defines competitive position (cost leader, differentiator). SC strategy defines how supply chain enables that position (efficient vs. responsive). SC structure, metrics, investments, and supplier strategies all cascade from the SC strategy.
CONCEPT
What is total supply chain cost leadership?
ANSWER
A strategy where the entire supply chain (including suppliers) is optimized for cost — leveraging shared efficiency, volume consolidation, and process standardization across the whole chain, not just within the company.
DEFINITION
What is supply chain strategic fit?
ANSWER
The degree of alignment between a company's competitive strategy and its supply chain strategy. A company pursuing cost leadership must have an efficient supply chain. A differentiator must have a responsive supply chain. Misfit between strategies leads to poor performance.
CONCEPT
What is the time-based competition strategy?
ANSWER
Competing on speed — fastest time-to-market for new products, fastest order fulfillment, shortest supply chain cycle time. Supply chain strategy: minimize all lead times, invest in visibility and agility, position inventory close to customers.
