Guidelines vs. Standards vs. Certification
  • Guidelines: Broad, principle-based best practices; lack authority of a standard; may not provide implementation detail
  • Standards: Best practices developed by consensus of numerous practitioners and experts
Accreditation (ASCM): Certification by a recognized body of the facilities, capability, objectivity, competence, and integrity of an agency, service, operational group, or individual to perform specified functions.
Certification (ASCM): Documentation of competency by a supplier or by an organization, such as ISO 9000 certification.

Key standards for CSCP: ISO 9000/9001, ISO 14000/14001, ISO 26000, SA8000, ANSI Z.10, plus international guidelines: UN Global Compact, OECD Guidelines, GRI.

International Guidelines

UN Global Compact

  • 10 key principles across 4 areas: Human Rights (2), Labour (2), Environment (3), Anti-Corruption (1)
  • Two complementary objectives: (1) Incorporate the 10 principles into global business activities; (2) Catalyze actions supporting broader UN goals
  • Recommends reporting sustainability efforts using GRI Sustainability Reporting Guidelines

OECD Guidelines for Multinational Enterprises

OECD Guidelines (ASCM): A set of recommendations about responsible business conduct addressed by governments to multinational enterprises (MNEs) operating in or from adhering countries. Covers employment/labor, environment, antibribery, consumer interests, information disclosure, and science/technology.

Threefold goal: (1) Align enterprises’ operations with government policies; (2) Reinforce trust between enterprises and societies; (3) Strengthen foreign investment climate and sustainable development.

Global Reporting Initiative (GRI)
GRI Reporting Framework (ASCM): The framework that sets out the principles and performance indicators organizations can use to measure and report their human rights, labor, environment, and anti-corruption activities.
Exhibit 8-14: GRI Sustainability Reporting Standards
  • Transitioned from guidelines to standards in 2018 (effective date)
  • Universally applicable: GRI 1 (Foundation — reporting principles), GRI 2 (General Disclosures), GRI 3 (Material Topics)
  • Topic-specific series: GRI 200 (Economic), GRI 300 (Environmental), GRI 400 (Social)

GRI Purposes: Build capacity, foster commitment to sustainability, improve performance and risk management, improve reliable sustainability information flow from supplier to buyer.

10 Reporting Principles (GRI 101): 5 for defining content (Stakeholder Inclusiveness, Sustainability Context, Materiality, Completeness, Accuracy) and 5 for quality (Balance, Comparability, Clarity, Reliability, Timeliness).

GRI 200 Economic topics: Economic performance, market presence, indirect impacts, procurement practices, anti-corruption, anti-competitive behavior, tax.

GRI 300 Environmental topics: Materials, energy, water/effluents, biodiversity, emissions, waste, environmental compliance, supplier assessment.

GRI 400 Social topics: Employment, labor/management, health & safety, training, diversity, non-discrimination, freedom of association, child labor, forced labor, security, indigenous peoples, local communities, supplier social assessment, public policy, customer health/safety, privacy.

ISO Sustainability Standards

ISO Key Characteristics

  • Voluntary: ISO is an NGO with no legal authority to enforce standards
  • Market-driven: Standards developed for which there is a market need, by international cross-section of experts
  • Consensus-based: Widespread applicability; generic management system standards (ISO 9000, ISO 14000) apply to any organization
  • Registration: Audit of implementation and conformance; ISO certification renewed every 3 years

ISO 9000 Series — Quality Standards

ISO 9000 Series Standards (ASCM): A set of international standards about quality management and quality assurance developed to help companies effectively document the quality system elements needed to maintain an efficient quality system.
  • ISO 9001: Certification standard; specifies QMS requirements; focuses on customer requirements, top management commitment, process approach, and continual improvement; renewed every 3 years

ISO 14000 Series — Environmental Standards

ISO 14000 Series Standards (ASCM): A series of generic environmental management standards developed by ISO that provide structure and guidance for a proactive approach to environmental management.
  • ISO 14001: Framework for strategic, holistic environmental policy, plans, and actions; can be certified
  • ISO 14004: Guidelines on elements of an environmental management system (EMS) and implementation

Social and Safety Standards

ISO 26000 (ASCM): An international standard developed by ISO to assist organizations in contributing to sustainable development. Provides guidance for social responsibility (not applicable for certification). Addresses 7 core areas: organizational governance, human rights, labor practices, environment, fair operating practices, consumer issues, community involvement.
SA8000 (ASCM): A widely recognized international standard for managing human rights in the workplace. Provides an auditable framework for social accountability covering child labor, forced labor, health/safety, freedom of association, discrimination, disciplinary practices, working hours, remuneration, and management systems.
ANSI Z.10 (ASCM): A voluntary consensus standard for occupational health and safety management systems. Uses recognized management system principles to be compatible with ISO 9001 and ISO 14001. Focuses on principles rather than detailed specifications.

What is the difference between a guideline and a standard?
ANSWER
Guideline: Broad, principle-based best practices; lacks authority of a standard; may not provide implementation detail. Standard: Best practices developed by consensus of numerous practitioners and experts; can support certification and audit.

TERM / CONCEPT
What does ISO 9001 certification require?
ANSWER
Organizations must demonstrate their ability to consistently provide conforming products and services. Requires internal audits; certification by accredited third-party organizations; renewed every 3 years. Focuses on customer requirements, top management commitment, process approach, and continual improvement.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the ASCM definition of ISO 14000 Series Standards?
ANSWER
A series of generic environmental management standards developed by ISO that provide structure and guidance for a proactive approach to environmental management. Help companies minimize harmful environmental effects and continually improve environmental performance.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is ISO 26000?
ANSWER
An ISO standard that assists organizations in contributing to sustainable development by providing guidance for social responsibility. Not a certification standard. Addresses 7 core areas: organizational governance, human rights, labor practices, environment, fair operating practices, consumer issues, and community involvement.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is SA8000?
ANSWER
A widely recognized international standard for managing human rights in the workplace; provides an auditable framework. Covers 9 areas: child labor, forced labor, health/safety, freedom of association, discrimination, disciplinary practices, working hours, remuneration, and management systems. Certification available per work site.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is ANSI Z.10?
ANSWER
A voluntary consensus standard for occupational health and safety management systems. Focuses on principles rather than detailed specifications, making it compatible with ISO 9001 and ISO 14001. Accredited by the ANSI-ASQ National Accreditation Board (ANAB).
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the 4 key characteristics of ISO?
ANSWER
1. Voluntary (no legal enforcement authority), 2. Market-driven (developed for market-identified needs), 3. Consensus-based (international cross-section of experts), 4. Registration (requires audit; ISO certification renewed every 3 years).
