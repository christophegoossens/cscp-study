Emerging Technology Overview

Key emerging technologies for supply chains: cloud computing, artificial intelligence, machine learning, data analytics, sensors, telematics, control towers, quantum computing, 3D printing, wearable technology, augmented reality, robotic process automation, automated guided vehicles, and drones.

Cloud Computing, AI, and Analytics
Cloud computing (ASCM): An emerging way of computing in which data is stored in massive data centers that can be accessed from any location over a network. Eliminates need for in-house hardware and software management.
  • SaaS (Software as a Service): Applications accessed via web browser; leased solutions
  • PaaS (Platform as a Service): Leased platform infrastructure
  • IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service): Leased hardware/network infrastructure
  • Downsides: ongoing lease cost, internet failure risk, security and data privacy concerns
Artificial intelligence (ASCM): Computer programs that can learn and reason in a manner similar to humans. Problem defined in terms of states and operators that can be solved by search or constraint satisfaction.
Machine learning (ASCM): Artificial intelligence software capable of analysis, self-training, and observation to improve its own performance. Often used in AI applications such as pattern detection and predictive analytics.
Expert system (ASCM): A type of AI computer system that mimics human experts by using rules and heuristics rather than deterministic algorithms.
Heuristics (ASCM): A form of problem-solving in which results or rules have been determined by experience or intuition instead of by optimization. Can be rule-based and provide quick near-optimal solutions.
Analytics (ASCM): The review of typically large sets of business data using mathematics, statistics, and computer software to identify meaningful patterns in the data that can be applied to improve business decisions.
  • Predictive analytics: Uses AI/machine learning to predict maintenance requirements, demand patterns, etc.
  • Big data: Very large datasets requiring significant processing power to extract useful information
Sensors, Control Towers & Other Technologies
Sensors (ASCM): Devices that can monitor differences in conditions to control equipment on a dynamic basis.
Supply chain control tower (ASCM): A centralized hub that provides an integrated, complete view of data across the end-to-end supply chain. Allows the supplier to see the requirements of the customer and the customer to see the supplier’s ability to deliver.
  • Telematics: Remote sensors that transmit data; enhance visibility and automation across industries
  • Transportation control towers: Monitor on-time orders, transport costs, track/trace, exception management
  • SC control towers: Monitor on-time in full (OTIF) orders, total cost to serve, 3PL performance
Quantum computing: An entirely different way to build a computer using quantum mechanical phenomena. Can explore many combinations simultaneously, enabling optimization of complex supply chain tradeoffs (routing, facility location, etc.).
3D printing (ASCM): The process of layering materials to make products and components using computer data. Enables on-demand production of 3D objects from digital models.
  • Key application: rapid prototyping (any 3D design can be printed and physically experienced)
Wearable (ASCM): A form of technology worn on the body that allows hands-free work by being voice and/or gesture activated. Used in warehousing, maintenance, and field operations.
Augmented reality (AR) (ASCM): Using holographic imagery alongside the physical environment to provide additional information or guidance about how to carry out a task.
Robotic process automation (RPA): The use of software robots (bots) to emulate human execution of repetitive, pre-defined business processes. Automates high-volume, rule-based tasks without human intervention.
Intelligent Supply Chains
Internet of Things (IoT) (ASCM): An environment in which objects, animals, or people are provided with unique identifiers and the ability to transfer data over a network without requiring human-to-human or human-to-computer interaction.
  • IoT networks non-computer devices (equipment, drones, ASRS, vehicles) that update systems on status, location, sensor readings, and maintenance needs
Blockchain (ASCM): A continuously growing list of records (blocks) linked and secured using cryptography. Each block contains a cryptographic hash of the previous block, a timestamp, and transaction data. Provides irrefutable evidence of transactions.
  • SC applications of blockchain: Chain of custody for goods, automated capture of IoT data (e.g., temperature records), preventing insertion of counterfeits by validating serial numbers
  • Famous as the technology behind cryptocurrencies; also used in TradeLens (IBM + Maersk) for water shipping
Intelligent Asset Management (IAM): Creates a digital version (“digital twin”) of an industrial asset, allowing monitoring, analysis, and optimization of physical assets through their digital counterpart.
Automated guided vehicle system (AGVS) (ASCM): A system that automatically routes one or more material-handling devices (carts, pallet trucks) and positions them at predetermined destinations without operator intervention.
  • Navigation uses markers, wires, vision sensors, or radio frequency; used in manufacturing and warehousing
  • Primary issue: cost (though falling); typical payback period of 2 years

What are 3 supply chain applications of blockchain?
ANSWER
1. Reliable chain of custody for goods (irrefutable evidence of transfer between parties). 2. Automated capture and distribution of reliable data from IoT devices (e.g., refrigerated truck temperature records). 3. Preventing insertion of counterfeits by validating serial numbers in the network.
