Overview
Customs refers to a country’s regulation of import and export trade at its international ports and borders. The purposes of customs are to ensure border security, to collect all required tariffs, and to enforce trade restrictions. A\xa0tariff,\xa0according to the\xa0ASCM Supply Chain Dictionary,\xa0is “an official schedule of taxes and fees imposed by a country on imports or exports.”
Organizations that engage in international trade use global trade management to ensure that interactions with customs proceed in a smooth and cost-effective manner. The\xa0ASCM Supply Chain Dictionary\xa0defines\xa0global trade management\xa0as
  • Tariff = official schedule of taxes/fees on imports or exports
  • Global trade management = optimize cross-border shipments; ensure compliance + efficiency
  • Organizations preclear freight to reduce customs delays

Key Documentation:

  • Commercial invoice: states value, quantity, description for customs
  • Packing list: itemizes contents of each package
  • Bill of lading (B/L): transport contract and receipt of goods
  • Certificate of origin: proves where goods were made (affects tariff rates)
  • Letter of credit: bank guarantee of payment; reduces payment risk
  • Export license: required for controlled goods/technology

Question 1
A commercial invoice in international trade is used by customs to:
A.Certify country of origin
B.Assess duties based on the transaction value
C.Book ocean freight
D.Insure the cargo
The commercial invoice documents the transaction value — the primary customs document for duty assessment.
Question 2
A negotiable Bill of Lading serves as:
A.Only a receipt for goods
B.A receipt, contract of carriage, AND a title document — the holder can claim the goods
C.Only a quality certificate
D.Only a customs declaration
A negotiable Bill of Lading simultaneously serves as a receipt, contract of carriage, and title document in international trade.
Question 3
A Letter of Credit (LC) protects:
A.Only the buyer
B.Only the seller
C.Both buyer and seller — payment guaranteed when compliant documents are presented
D.Only the bank
An LC guarantees payment to the seller when compliant documents are presented, while protecting the buyer from non-delivery.
Question 4
A Certificate of Origin is required to:
A.Book freight
B.Claim preferential tariff rates under free trade agreements
C.File a Bill of Lading
D.Set import duty rates
A Certificate of Origin certifies where goods were manufactured — required for preferential tariff treatment under FTAs.
