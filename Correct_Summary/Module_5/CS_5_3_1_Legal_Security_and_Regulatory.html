Overview

International supply chains operate within a complex web of legal requirements, security regulations, and trade compliance rules. Failure to comply can result in delays, fines, cargo seizure, or criminal liability.

ASCM Definitions

📖 Definition:
Trade compliance: The adherence to all applicable laws, regulations, and requirements governing the import and export of goods — including customs duties, import restrictions, export controls, and sanctions.
📖 Definition:
Customs: The government authority responsible for controlling the flow of goods in and out of a country — collecting duties, enforcing import/export regulations, and preventing prohibited goods from crossing borders.
📖 Definition:
Duty (tariff): A tax levied by a government on imported goods. Calculated as a percentage of the goods' value (ad valorem) or as a fixed amount per unit (specific duty).

Trade Security Programs

📖 Definition:
Customs-Trade Partnership Against Terrorism (C-TPAT): A US Customs and Border Protection (CBP) program where importers voluntarily adopt security standards in exchange for reduced inspections and faster processing.
📖 Definition:
Authorized Economic Operator (AEO): The EU equivalent of C-TPAT — a certification for businesses demonstrating high standards of customs compliance and supply chain security.
📖 Definition:
Importer of Record (IOR): The entity legally responsible for ensuring imported goods comply with all applicable regulations and for paying all applicable duties and taxes. Takes legal responsibility for the shipment.

Export Control

📖 Definition:
Export Administration Regulations (EAR): US regulations governing exports of commercial items that could have military applications. Administered by the Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS).
📖 Definition:
International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR): US regulations controlling the export of defense-related articles and services. Much stricter than EAR — requires licenses for exports of listed defense items.
💡 Key Point:
Denied parties screening: before any international transaction, companies must check that they are not doing business with individuals or entities on government denied/debarred/sanctioned lists (OFAC SDN list, BIS entity list).
  • OFAC (Office of Foreign Assets Control): administers US economic sanctions programs
  • HTS code (Harmonized Tariff Schedule): the universal product classification code used to determine applicable tariff rates
  • Country of Origin (COO): determines which duty rate applies and whether trade agreement benefits are available

DEFINITION
What is trade compliance?
ANSWER
Adherence to all applicable laws and regulations governing import and export of goods — including customs duties, import restrictions, export controls, sanctions, and documentation requirements.
DEFINITION
What is a customs duty (tariff)?
ANSWER
A tax levied by a government on imported goods. Calculated ad valorem (percentage of value) or as a specific duty (fixed amount per unit). Purpose: generate revenue and protect domestic industries.
DEFINITION
What is C-TPAT (Customs-Trade Partnership Against Terrorism)?
ANSWER
A US CBP program where importers voluntarily adopt supply chain security standards in exchange for reduced cargo inspections and faster customs processing. Demonstrates supply chain security compliance.
DEFINITION
What is an Authorized Economic Operator (AEO)?
ANSWER
The EU equivalent of C-TPAT — a certification for businesses that demonstrate high standards of customs compliance and supply chain security. Provides benefits including expedited processing and fewer physical inspections.
DEFINITION
What is an Importer of Record (IOR)?
ANSWER
The entity legally responsible for ensuring imported goods comply with all regulations and for paying applicable duties and taxes. The IOR assumes legal liability for the shipment's compliance.
DEFINITION
What are Export Administration Regulations (EAR)?
ANSWER
US regulations governing exports of commercial dual-use items (items with both commercial and potential military applications). Administered by the Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS). Require export licenses for controlled items.
DEFINITION
What are International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR)?
ANSWER
US regulations controlling exports of defense articles and services — weapons, military equipment, and defense technology. Much stricter than EAR; requires licenses for any export of ITAR-controlled items. Violations carry severe penalties.
CONCEPT
What is denied parties screening?
ANSWER
Before any international transaction, checking that counterparties are not on government blacklists — US OFAC SDN (Specially Designated Nationals) list, BIS entity list, EU consolidated list. Failure to screen can result in criminal liability.
DEFINITION
What is an HTS (Harmonized Tariff Schedule) code?
ANSWER
A standardized numerical code classifying products for customs purposes — determines the applicable duty rate, import restrictions, and whether trade agreement benefits apply. The same product classification system used by 200+ countries.
CONCEPT
What is Country of Origin (COO) and why does it matter?
ANSWER
The country where a product was manufactured or substantially transformed. Determines: applicable duty rate, whether trade agreement preferential rates apply, and whether import restrictions (quotas, embargoes) apply.
DEFINITION
What is OFAC and what does it regulate?
ANSWER
Office of Foreign Assets Control — administers US economic sanctions programs. Prohibits transactions with sanctioned countries, entities, and individuals. Supply chain companies must screen all parties (suppliers, customers, banks, carriers) against OFAC lists.
DEFINITION
What is a customs broker?
ANSWER
A licensed professional who files customs entry documents, pays duties, and manages the clearance process on behalf of importers. Required expertise in tariff classification, duty calculation, and import regulations.
CONCEPT
What is AML (Anti-Money Laundering) in supply chain context?
ANSWER
Regulations requiring businesses to detect and prevent the use of supply chain transactions to launder money. Supply chains can be used to transfer value (over-invoicing goods, trade-based money laundering) — financial controls and transaction monitoring are required.
CONCEPT
What is supply chain security and why is it important beyond compliance?
ANSWER
Securing the physical and data integrity of the supply chain against theft, tampering, terrorism, and fraud. Beyond regulatory compliance: a disruption due to a security incident can cause significant financial and reputational damage.
CONCEPT
What is a free trade agreement (FTA) and how does it reduce supply chain costs?
ANSWER
A treaty between countries that reduces or eliminates tariffs on goods exchanged between them. To benefit, goods must meet rules of origin requirements (sufficient local content). FTAs can significantly reduce duty costs on qualifying imports.
