Overview

Trade zones and trade agreements create preferential conditions for international commerce — reducing duties, simplifying customs procedures, and enabling efficient global supply chains.

ASCM Definitions

📖 Definition:
Free Trade Zone (FTZ): A designated geographical area where goods may be imported, stored, handled, manufactured, or reconfigured, and re-exported under specific customs regulatory exemptions. Duties are paid only when goods enter the domestic market.
📖 Definition:
Free Trade Agreement (FTA): A treaty between two or more countries that eliminates or reduces tariffs and trade barriers between the signatories. Examples: USMCA (US-Mexico-Canada), CPTPP, EU Single Market.
📖 Definition:
Rules of Origin: Criteria used to determine the national origin of a product for trade purposes — essential for determining whether goods qualify for preferential duty rates under a Free Trade Agreement.
📖 Definition:
Most Favored Nation (MFN) status: A trade treatment where a country extends the same trade conditions to another country as it extends to its 'most favored' trading partner. Standard baseline under WTO rules.

Types of Trade Zones

💡 Key Point:
Free Trade Zone (FTZ) — known in the US as Foreign Trade Zone: goods can be imported duty-free, processed, and re-exported without paying duty. Duty is paid only on the portion that enters US commerce. Key benefit: defer or eliminate duty payments.
  • Bonded warehouse: a government-approved facility where imported goods can be stored without paying duty until they are released into commerce or re-exported
  • Special Economic Zone (SEZ): a broader zone often including FTZ benefits plus tax incentives, simplified regulations, and infrastructure for attracting foreign investment
  • Enterprise zone: a designated area offering tax incentives to businesses to stimulate economic activity
  • Maquiladora: a Mexican manufacturing facility near the US border operating under a special duty program

Key Trade Agreements

  • USMCA (United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement): replaced NAFTA; governs trade between US, Mexico, and Canada
  • EU Single Market: enables free movement of goods, services, capital, and people among EU members
  • CPTPP (Comprehensive and Progressive Agreement for Trans-Pacific Partnership): 11-country Pacific trade bloc
  • RCEP (Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership): largest trade agreement by GDP — includes 15 Asia-Pacific countries

DEFINITION
What is a Free Trade Zone (FTZ)?
ANSWER
A designated area where goods can be imported, processed, and re-exported under customs exemptions. Duties are paid only when goods enter the domestic market. Used to defer duty payments, reduce import costs on re-exported goods, and enable international distribution.
DEFINITION
What is a Free Trade Agreement (FTA)?
ANSWER
A treaty between countries that eliminates or reduces tariffs and trade barriers between signatories. Examples: USMCA, EU Single Market, CPTPP. Qualifying goods must meet rules of origin requirements to receive preferential rates.
DEFINITION
What are rules of origin?
ANSWER
Criteria determining the national origin of a product for FTA preferential duty purposes. Must demonstrate sufficient local content or transformation. Without meeting rules of origin, goods cannot claim FTA duty preferences.
DEFINITION
What is Most Favored Nation (MFN) status?
ANSWER
A WTO principle requiring that trade concessions extended to any WTO member must be extended to all WTO members equally. The baseline tariff rate a country applies to imports from WTO members. FTAs provide rates better than MFN.
CONCEPT
What is the key supply chain benefit of an FTZ?
ANSWER
Goods can be imported, stored, assembled, or processed in the FTZ without paying duty — duties are paid only when goods leave the FTZ to enter the domestic market. If goods are re-exported, no duty is paid. This improves cash flow and reduces landed cost.
DEFINITION
What is a bonded warehouse?
ANSWER
A government-approved warehouse where imported goods can be stored without paying duty for a specified period (typically up to 5 years in the US). Duties are paid when goods are released into commerce or forgiven if goods are re-exported.
DEFINITION
What is a Special Economic Zone (SEZ)?
ANSWER
A broader zone offering FTZ benefits plus tax holidays, reduced regulations, and infrastructure investments to attract foreign manufacturing and trade. Common in China, India, and developing economies as tools for industrial development.
DEFINITION
What is USMCA and what did it replace?
ANSWER
The United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement — a trade agreement governing trade between the US, Mexico, and Canada. Replaced NAFTA in 2020. Includes updated rules on automotive content, digital trade, and labor standards.
DEFINITION
What is a maquiladora?
ANSWER
A Mexican manufacturing facility near the US border that imports materials duty-free, processes them into finished goods, and exports them back to the US under favorable duty treatment. Enables low-cost manufacturing close to the US market.
CONCEPT
How do supply chain managers use FTZs to reduce landed cost?
ANSWER
By importing materials into an FTZ duty-free, manufacturing or assembling within the FTZ, and paying duty only on the finished product (which may have a lower or different duty rate than the components). Inverted tariff benefit reduces total duty paid.
CONCEPT
What is an inverted tariff and how does FTZ use address it?
ANSWER
When the duty rate on components is higher than the duty rate on the finished product made from those components. By importing components into an FTZ, assembling into finished goods, and entering the market as finished goods, the company pays the lower finished goods duty rate.
DEFINITION
What is the EU Single Market?
ANSWER
A supranational single market among EU members enabling free movement of goods, services, capital, and people without customs duties or non-tariff barriers between EU countries. The largest free trade area in the world by GDP.
DEFINITION
What is CPTPP?
ANSWER
Comprehensive and Progressive Agreement for Trans-Pacific Partnership — a 11-country Pacific Rim trade agreement (Canada, Mexico, Japan, Australia, Vietnam, etc.) reducing tariffs and trade barriers in the Pacific region. US withdrew from predecessor TPP in 2017.
DEFINITION
What is duty drawback?
ANSWER
A refund of duties paid on imported goods when they are subsequently exported — after processing or incorporation into an exported product. Enables cost recovery for manufacturers who use imported materials in export products.
DEFINITION
What is WTO (World Trade Organization)?
ANSWER
The international organization governing global trade rules. WTO agreements provide the framework for trade negotiations, dispute resolution, and MFN principles. 164 member countries (as of 2023) accounting for most world trade.
