The Purchase Order Process
Purchase order (ASCM): The purchaser's authorization used to formalize a purchase transaction with a supplier. When accepted by a supplier, it constitutes a legally binding contract.

The PO process steps:

  • 1. Purchase requisition: Internal request to buy — requires approval from budget holder
  • 2. PO creation: Purchasing creates the PO with all required details (item, quantity, price, delivery date, ship-to address)
  • 3. PO transmission: Sent to supplier (EDI, portal, email)
  • 4. Supplier acknowledgment: Supplier confirms receipt and acceptance (creates the legal contract)
  • 5. Goods receipt: Items received, inspected, and matched to the PO
  • 6. Invoice reconciliation: Supplier invoice matched to PO and goods receipt (3-way match)
  • 7. Payment: Approved invoices paid per agreed payment terms
3-way match: The process of matching the supplier's invoice against the purchase order AND the goods receipt before approving payment. Prevents payment errors and fraud.
A PO is a legally binding contract when accepted by the supplier. Both offer (PO) and acceptance (supplier acknowledgment) must exist for a valid contract.
Types of Purchase Orders
  • Standard PO: One-time purchase — all details specified at the time of ordering. Used for non-recurring or first-time purchases.
  • Blanket PO (BPO): A long-term commitment to a supplier for a total estimated quantity or value, against which individual releases are placed as needed. Locks in pricing, simplifies ordering for recurring items.
  • Contract PO: Establishes terms and conditions for future transactions — pricing and terms agreed upfront, specific quantities ordered via releases later.
  • Planned PO: Created by the planning system (MRP/ERP) based on demand but not yet released to the supplier — used internally for planning visibility.
Blanket purchase order (ASCM): A long-term commitment to a supplier for material against which short-term releases will be generated to satisfy requirements. Often contains an estimated total quantity over a period.
Blanket POs reduce administrative cost for high-frequency, recurring purchases — agreed terms + total quantity, then release individual shipments as needed without creating a new PO each time.
E-Procurement
E-procurement: The use of electronic systems and internet-based tools to automate purchasing processes — from requisition through payment.

E-procurement methods:

  • Electronic catalogs: Supplier product catalogs hosted on-line — buyers select items and quantities, system generates POs automatically
  • Electronic auctions (e-auctions / reverse auctions): Suppliers bid in real time to win business — buyer sees all bids, drives price competition downward
  • Electronic marketplaces: Online platforms where buyers and sellers transact — often used for commodity/MRO (Maintenance, Repair & Operations) purchasing
  • Procurement cards (P-cards): Corporate credit cards for small-value purchases — bypasses full PO process for low-risk, low-value items
Reverse auction: An online auction where multiple suppliers compete by bidding LOWER prices in real time to win the buyer's business. Drives price competition.
E-procurement reduces processing cost, increases speed, improves compliance (only approved suppliers/items), and provides better spend data analytics.

Q1 of 5
A purchase order becomes a legally binding contract when:
A PO is an OFFER from the buyer. It becomes a binding contract only when the SUPPLIER ACCEPTS it (acknowledgment). Both offer and acceptance are required for a valid contract — delivery of goods is performance of the contract, not its formation.
Q2 of 5
A company buys office supplies from the same distributor monthly. Which PO type best reduces administrative cost while locking in pricing?
Blanket PO = one agreement for the year with total estimated quantity + agreed pricing. Individual releases (specific quantities/dates) are placed against the blanket as needed — no new PO or price negotiation each time. Ideal for high-frequency, recurring purchases.
Q3 of 5
The 3-way match in accounts payable involves matching:
3-way match = PO (what was ordered) + Goods Receipt (what was received) + Invoice (what the supplier is charging). All three must agree before payment is approved. Discrepancies trigger investigation before payment.
Q4 of 5
In a reverse auction, suppliers compete by:
Reverse auction = online real-time competition where suppliers bid DOWN (lower prices) to win the contract. Called 'reverse' because unlike a normal auction (buyers bid up), here suppliers bid down. Best for commodities with clear specs where price is the dominant selection criterion.
Q5 of 5
A company issues procurement cards (P-cards) to department managers for small purchases under €500. The main benefit is:
P-cards bypass the full PO process (requisition → PO → receipt → invoice → payment) for small-value, low-risk purchases. This dramatically reduces transaction cost — the admin cost of a full PO process can exceed the value of small purchases.
