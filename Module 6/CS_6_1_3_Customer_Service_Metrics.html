6.1.3 Overview: Customer Service Metrics

Customer service metrics measure what matters to customers — identified through interviews, questionnaires, and benchmarking. Metrics exist at two levels:

  • Strategic metrics: long-term, aggregated assessments (e.g., perfect order fulfillment, customer satisfaction)
  • Operational metrics: detailed measurements driving day-to-day performance
Perfect order fulfillment (SCOR metric): measures every step of a customer’s experience from order placement to delivery — delivered on time, in full, undamaged, with correct documentation.
Key Availability Metrics
Customer service ratio (fill rate): 1) A measure of delivery performance of finished goods, usually expressed as a percentage. In a make-to-stock company, the percentage of orders filled from stock. 2) The ratio of the total orders that can be filled from inventory.
On-time in full (OTIF): a delivery scoring system in which a target delivery goal (usually expressed as a percentage) is set — the deliverer must deliver on the promised date AND in the full quantity ordered.

Fill rate variations:

  • Unit fill rate: % of items delivered vs. ordered
  • Line item fill rate: % of line items delivered vs. ordered
  • Monetary value fill rate: % of monetary value delivered vs. ordered

Stockout frequency measures: % items in stockout; monetary value in stockout; average age of stockout; average time to recover.

Lead Time Monitoring
Cycle time: 1) In industrial engineering, the time between completion of two discrete units of production. 2) In materials management, the length of time from when material is ordered until it is received and ready for use.

Lead time monitoring covers four dimensions:

  • Speed of performance: elapsed time from order placement to delivery and ready for use
  • Consistency (delivery consistency): number of times cycles meet planned time; variance in lead times; % delivered in quoted lead time
  • Flexibility: ability to accommodate unexpected or unusual customer requests
  • Malfunction recovery: contingency plans in place; ability to source out-of-stock items from alternate facilities
Supply chain cycle time: time it would take to fill a customer order if inventory levels were zero — the sum of the longest lead times for each stage in the supply chain.

CRM Performance Management

CRM effectiveness must be measured across four areas:

  • Customer service: response to inquiries (speed + accuracy), order processing (cycle time, % on time, % accurate), level of service (right product/quantity/time/place), product/service quality (defect rate, warranty claims)
  • Customer satisfaction: expectation setting + fulfillment; meeting or exceeding expectations = satisfaction; falling short = dissatisfaction
Customer satisfaction measurement methods: VOC interviews/focus groups, transaction questionnaires, monthly/quarterly questionnaires, performance reviews with key customers, monitoring/responding to online negative feedback.
Communication management plan: a document that describes the communications needs and expectations for the project, how and in what format information will be communicated, when and where each communication will be made, and who is responsible.
Stakeholder relationship management: addressing and managing the competing priorities, needs, and concerns of internal and external stakeholders in a proactive and sustained manner, resulting in successful project delivery.

Question 1
What is the 'perfect order' metric and what does it measure?
A.An order that is placed without errors by the customer
B.The percentage of orders that are delivered on-time, in-full, damage-free, and with accurate documentation — a composite metric capturing all key fulfillment performance dimensions simultaneously
C.An order that is profitable for the supplier
D.An order placed with no minimum order quantity
Perfect Order = % orders that are on-time AND in-full AND damage-free AND accurately documented. It is highly demanding — if each dimension is 97%, Perfect Order = 0.97^4 ≈ 88.5%. It drives a holistic view of supply chain service rather than optimizing one metric at the expense of others.
Question 2
What is 'OTIF' (On-Time In-Full) and why is it a critical supply chain KPI?
A.A shipping notation for on-time international freight
B.The percentage of customer orders delivered both on time (by the promised date) and in full (the complete quantity ordered) — a primary measure of supply chain reliability. Major retailers (Walmart, Amazon) impose financial penalties for OTIF non-compliance
C.An inventory counting method
D.A safety metric for warehouse operations
OTIF captures two critical dimensions: timeliness (did it arrive when promised?) and completeness (did we deliver what was ordered?). Retailer OTIF fines (Walmart's 3% deduction for non-compliance) have elevated it to a CEO-level supply chain metric.
Question 3
What is 'order fill rate' and how does it differ from 'case fill rate'?
A.They are exactly the same metric
B.Order fill rate: % of customer orders shipped complete (all lines, full quantities). Case fill rate: % of ordered units (cases) actually shipped. A 90% case fill rate means 10% of ordered cases were not shipped — creating stockouts at retail
C.Fill rate measures delivery speed; case fill measures accuracy
D.Order fill rate is for B2B; case fill rate is for B2C
Fill rate can be measured at order, line, or unit level. Order fill rate (all-or-nothing) is most stringent; line fill rate measures individual SKUs; case fill rate measures unit volume. Each reveals different patterns of stockout impact.
Question 4
What is 'order cycle time' (OCT) and what are its components?
A.The time it takes to count inventory
B.The elapsed time from when a customer places an order to when they receive it — components: order entry and processing time, credit check, pick/pack, carrier pickup, transit time, and delivery. Shorter OCT is a competitive advantage and enables leaner customer inventory
C.The time between production runs
D.The time needed to fulfill a purchase order from a supplier
OCT (or 'order lead time' from customer's perspective) drives how much safety stock the customer needs to hold. If OCT shortens from 7 days to 2 days, the customer can reduce safety stock significantly — making supplier OCT a key driver of customer competitiveness.
Question 5
What is 'backorder rate' and what does a high rate indicate?
A.The rate at which orders are shipped from a backup warehouse
B.The percentage of orders (or order lines) that cannot be fulfilled immediately due to insufficient inventory — indicating supply-demand imbalance caused by forecast error, supply disruption, or inadequate safety stock
C.The percentage of orders that are returned by customers
D.The rate of orders placed for future delivery
A high backorder rate signals inventory management problems: safety stock too low, forecast accuracy poor, or supply disruptions not mitigated. Backordered items delay customer revenue, require expediting cost, and risk order cancellations if customers find alternatives.
Question 6
What is 'customer satisfaction score' (CSAT) and how is it measured?
A.The score given to customers based on their creditworthiness
B.A measure of how satisfied customers are with a specific interaction or overall experience — typically measured via surveys (rate your satisfaction 1-5 or 1-10) immediately after transactions or periodically. Supply chain performance is a primary driver of CSAT
C.A score used to prioritize customer orders
D.The rating of customer-facing employees
CSAT surveys capture customer perception: even with 99% OTIF, a single bad experience can damage CSAT. Supply chain touchpoints (delivery experience, packaging condition, ease of returns) all contribute. CSAT trends identify where supply chain improvement will have the most customer impact.
Question 7
What is 'days sales outstanding' (DSO) and how is it relevant to customer service?
A.The number of days between a sale and the shipment of goods
B.The average number of days a company takes to collect payment after a sale is made — high DSO indicates billing/invoice problems (errors that cause disputes and delay payment) or customer financial stress. Invoice accuracy (a supply chain metric) directly affects DSO
C.The number of sales made per day
D.The time from order to final delivery
DSO is affected by supply chain: invoice errors (wrong quantity, wrong price, not matching PO) create disputes that delay payment — increasing DSO. Perfect order documentation (accurate invoices on first submission) reduces DSO by enabling straight-through payment processing.
Question 8
What is 'warehouse order accuracy' and why is it measured separately?
A.The accuracy of warehouse location records
B.The percentage of orders picked and packed with the correct items, quantities, and packaging — measured separately from delivery performance because warehouse errors (wrong item, wrong quantity) are controllable at the warehouse, while delivery timing is partly carrier-dependent
C.The accuracy of warehouse inventory counts
D.The percentage of warehouse workers who achieve their productivity targets
Warehouse accuracy is a leading indicator for order accuracy: if picking accuracy is 99.5%, the customer receives a wrong item in 5 of every 1,000 orders — causing returns, re-ships, and customer dissatisfaction. Root causes (mislabeled bins, complex SKUs, manual picking) differ from transportation failures.
Question 9
What is 'inventory availability' or 'in-stock rate' and how is it measured?
A.The percentage of inventory that has been counted in the last audit
B.The percentage of time (or % of customer requests) that a SKU is available when customers want to purchase it — measured at various points: warehouse stock vs. plan, store shelf vs. plan, or as % of orders filled from stock without backorder
C.The ratio of finished goods to work-in-process
D.The accuracy of inventory records vs. physical count
In-stock rate (or service level from inventory perspective) measures supply chain reliability from the customer demand perspective. A 95% in-stock rate means 5% of the time a customer wanted an item, it wasn't available — driving substitution or lost sale.
Question 10
What is 'customer complaint rate' and how should it be used?
A.The percentage of customers who leave negative reviews online
B.The number of formal complaints received per unit of sales — a diagnostic metric that, when analyzed by type (late delivery, wrong item, damage, invoice error), identifies the most impactful supply chain improvement opportunities
C.A measure of how often customers complain to call centers
D.The frequency of product recalls
Complaint analysis reveals improvement priorities: if 60% of complaints are for late delivery and 30% are for invoice errors, investment in transportation reliability and invoice accuracy will have more impact than addressing damage (10%). Pareto analysis of complaint types drives focused improvement.
Question 11
What is 'return rate' as a customer service metric and what does it signal?
A.The rate at which customers return to make repeat purchases
B.The percentage of sold units returned by customers — high rates for specific reasons (wrong size, defective, damaged in transit) signal quality, packaging, or product description problems that should be corrected at the root cause to prevent future returns
C.The percentage of returns that are refunded vs. exchanged
D.The rate of return on investment for customer service operations
Return rate as a service metric signals supply chain quality issues: if 8% of a product is returned for 'defective,' quality control is failing; if 5% is returned for 'damaged in transit,' packaging or carrier handling is inadequate. Root cause corrective action reduces both returns and customer dissatisfaction.
Question 12
What is a 'customer service dashboard' and what metrics should it include?
A.A dashboard displayed in a customer service center
B.A real-time or near-real-time management tool displaying key customer service performance metrics — typically including OTIF, order fill rate, order accuracy, on-hand inventory vs. target, backorder status, complaint volume, and CSAT — enabling rapid identification of issues
C.A customer-facing tracking website
D.A financial reporting tool for customer revenue
A customer service dashboard consolidates metrics that collectively show supply chain health from the customer's perspective. Leading indicators (inventory coverage, scheduled shipments vs. commitment) complement lagging indicators (OTIF, complaint rate) to enable both real-time reaction and trend management.
