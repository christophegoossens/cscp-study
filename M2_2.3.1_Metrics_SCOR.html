Why Metrics Matter
"You get what you measure." Metrics shape behavior — people focus on what is measured. Poorly designed metrics drive wrong behaviors (e.g., measuring units shipped encourages overshipping to hit targets).

Three basic objectives of any measurement system:

  • Track performance: Observe and gather data relevant to business objectives
  • Compare to standards: Identify when performance needs attention or modification
  • Motivate behavior: Understand what incentives drive people to work toward organizational goals

Good metrics are: objective, consistent, quantified, and measure at least two parameters — typically an actual result AND a target or standard for comparison.

KPI (Key Performance Indicator): A metric that tracks the most critical aspects of supply chain performance — tied directly to strategic objectives.
Supply chain metrics should cut ACROSS functions and organizations to promote collaboration — not just measure each silo's local performance.
SCOR DS (Supply Chain Operations Reference — Diagnostic Standard)
SCOR (Supply Chain Operations Reference) model: A standard framework developed by ASCM for describing, evaluating, and benchmarking supply chain performance. Covers six processes: Plan, Source, Make, Deliver, Return, Enable.

SCOR DS is the diagnostic standard version — a simplified, accessible framework for measuring supply chain performance using standard KPIs aligned to SCOR processes.

SCOR metrics are organized into three levels of reliability:
  • Level 1 — Strategic: High-level performance metrics (e.g., Perfect Order Fulfillment, Cash-to-Cash Cycle Time)
  • Level 2 — Tactical: More detailed metrics for specific SCOR processes
  • Level 3 — Operational: Detailed diagnostic metrics

The 5 performance attributes of SCOR:

  • Reliability: Delivering the right product, right place, right time, right condition (Perfect Order Fulfillment)
  • Responsiveness: Speed of supply chain — Order Fulfillment Cycle Time
  • Agility: Ability to respond to market changes
  • Costs: Supply chain management cost as % of revenue
  • Asset management: How effectively assets (inventory, cash) are being used
Key SCOR Level 1 Metrics
Perfect Order Fulfillment: % of orders delivered: complete, on time, damage-free, and correctly documented. The composite reliability metric.
Perfect Order Fulfillment = (Orders meeting ALL criteria / Total orders) × 100%
Order Fulfillment Cycle Time: The average time from customer order receipt to delivery. Measures responsiveness.
Cash-to-Cash Cycle Time: Days inventory outstanding + Days sales outstanding − Days payable outstanding. Measures working capital efficiency.
Cash-to-Cash = DIO + DSO − DPO DIO = Days Inventory Outstanding DSO = Days Sales Outstanding DPO = Days Payable Outstanding Lower is better — less cash tied up in the operating cycle
Supply chain cost as % of revenue: Total supply chain operating costs divided by net revenue. Measures cost efficiency.

ANSWER
Local/functional metrics encourage silo optimization at the expense of overall SC performance. Cross-functional metrics (e.g., perfect order, C2C) align departments toward shared outcomes and encourage collaboration.

Q1 of 5
A supply chain manager measures warehouse 'units picked per hour' but ignores order accuracy. Pickers rush to hit the speed target and error rates rise. This illustrates:
'You get what you measure' — people optimize for whatever is measured. Measuring speed without accuracy creates a perverse incentive. Good metrics must capture the right outcome — not just the easiest-to-measure activity.
Q2 of 5
Perfect Order Fulfillment requires an order to be:
Perfect Order Fulfillment is a composite metric — ALL four criteria must be met: complete quantity, on time, damage-free, and correct documentation/invoicing. Missing any one criterion = imperfect order.
Q3 of 5
A company has DIO = 45 days, DSO = 30 days, DPO = 60 days. What is the Cash-to-Cash Cycle Time?
C2C = DIO + DSO − DPO = 45 + 30 − 60 = 15 days. This means the company has cash tied up in operations for 15 days between paying suppliers and collecting from customers. Negative C2C (e.g., Amazon) means customers pay before suppliers are paid — highly favorable working capital position.
Q4 of 5
Which SCOR performance attribute does Order Fulfillment Cycle Time primarily measure?
Order Fulfillment Cycle Time = the speed from order to delivery. In SCOR, this is the RESPONSIVENESS attribute — how quickly the supply chain can fulfill customer demand.
Q5 of 5
Why is it important for KPIs to include both an actual result AND a target/standard?
A metric without a benchmark is meaningless — '95% on-time delivery' sounds good, but is it? Only by comparing to a target (98% goal) or industry benchmark do you know if action is required. Good metrics always pair actuals with standards.
