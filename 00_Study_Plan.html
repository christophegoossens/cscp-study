Exam Strategy Tips

💡 How to approach CSCP questions

  • Scenario-based thinking: Questions present real-world situations. Always ask "what would a supply chain professional do here?" rather than looking for a memorized fact.
  • ASCM definitions matter: The exam uses very precise ASCM Supply Chain Dictionary language. Learn the official definitions, not paraphrases.
  • Eliminate 2 wrong answers first: The exam is multiple choice. Often 2 answers are clearly wrong — eliminate them, then choose between the remaining 2.
  • Answer every question: No penalty for wrong answers. Never leave a question blank.
  • Time management: 150 questions in 3.5 hours = ~84 seconds per question. Flag hard ones and return later.
  • Focus on high-weight modules: Modules 4 (19%), 3 (17%), and 6 (17%) together represent 53% of the exam. Know these deeply.
  • Understand trade-offs: Supply chain management is about balancing costs, speed, quality, and risk. Many questions test whether you understand these trade-offs.
  • Don't over-think: The correct answer is usually the most supply-chain-professional answer — not the most clever one.
