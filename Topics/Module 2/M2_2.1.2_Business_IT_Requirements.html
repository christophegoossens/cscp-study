Efficient vs. Responsive Supply Chains
Efficient supply chain: Optimized for lowest total cost — minimal inventory, low overhead, stable demand, few product changes. Best for commodities and predictable markets.
Responsive supply chain: Optimized for fast response to customer demand — flexible capacity, higher inventory, more product variety. Best for volatile demand or short product lifecycles.
Efficient and responsive supply chains represent opposite ends of a spectrum. Companies must choose where to position based on their product characteristics and competitive strategy.

The two variables that differentiate efficient vs. responsive: (1) Inventory volume — how much buffer? (2) Demand uncertainty — how predictable is demand?

Efficient SC attributes: stable demand, infrequent product changes, focus on cost reduction, high capacity utilization.

Responsive SC attributes: volatile demand, frequent new products, investment in flexibility and speed, accepts higher costs for agility.

Resilience: A third dimension — the ability to withstand and recover from disruptions. Requires redundant capacity and diversified sourcing, which adds cost but reduces risk.
IT Requirements for Supply Chain Design

IT requirements for supply chain translate from business requirements — the technology must support the chosen supply chain strategy (efficient vs. responsive).

Key IT goals for supply chain: (1) Automate routine tasks (free managers for value-added work), (2) Enable visibility across tiers, (3) Support data-driven decision-making.
  • For efficient SCs: ERP for cost control, automated reorder systems, lean-focused planning tools
  • For responsive SCs: Advanced planning systems (APS), demand sensing, rapid order management, real-time visibility

IT systems must support the DATA requirements: how often data is transferred, how it is cleansed and validated, how it flows between supply chain partners.

Benefit-cost analysis must justify every IT investment — both tangible benefits (cost savings, inventory reduction) and intangible benefits (faster decision-making, customer satisfaction).

TERM / CONCEPT
What is supply chain resilience and why does it cost more?
ANSWER
Resilience = the ability to withstand and recover from disruptions. It requires redundant capacity, diversified sourcing, and safety buffers — all of which add cost. It is a third dimension beyond efficiency and responsiveness.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the key IT goals for supply chain management?
ANSWER
1) Automate routine/repetitive tasks 2) Enable end-to-end visibility across tiers 3) Support data-driven decision making (planning, analytics) 4) Connect supply chain partners with timely, accurate data
TERM / CONCEPT
Why can't most companies be both maximally efficient AND maximally responsive?
ANSWER
Efficiency and responsiveness require opposing design choices: efficiency needs lean inventory and stable demand; responsiveness needs excess capacity and rapid flexibility. Investing in both simultaneously drives up cost. Companies must choose where to position on the spectrum.
TERM / CONCEPT
How do IT requirements differ between efficient and responsive supply chains?
ANSWER
Efficient: ERP for cost control, automated reorder, lean planning tools. Responsive: Advanced Planning Systems (APS), demand sensing, real-time visibility, rapid order management — tools that enable speed and flexibility over cost minimization.

Q1 of 5
A company sells a commodity product with very stable, predictable demand and competes primarily on price. Which supply chain strategy fits best?
Stable, predictable demand + price competition = Efficient supply chain. The design should minimize cost through high utilization, lean inventory, and minimal customization. Responsiveness and resilience add cost that isn't justified when demand is stable.
Q2 of 5
A fashion company launches new clothing collections every 6 weeks with highly unpredictable demand. Which supply chain characteristic is most critical?
Fast fashion = high demand volatility + short product lifecycle → Responsive supply chain. Speed to market, flexible production, and the ability to react to demand signals matter more than cost minimization.
Q3 of 5
The two key variables that differentiate efficient and responsive supply chains are:
Efficient vs. responsive is determined by: (1) how much inventory is needed (inventory volume) and (2) how predictable the demand is (demand uncertainty). High uncertainty + high volume needs → responsive. Low uncertainty + lean inventory → efficient.
Q4 of 5
Adding redundant supplier relationships and safety stock for critical components to protect against supply disruptions is primarily an investment in:
Resilience = ability to absorb and recover from disruptions. Redundant suppliers, safety buffers, and backup capacity all add cost but protect against supply chain failures — the third strategic dimension alongside efficiency and responsiveness.
Q5 of 5
A company's IT system automates routine purchase order generation based on reorder points, freeing buyers to focus on supplier relationships. This IT goal is best described as:
One of the core IT requirements for supply chain is automating routine/repetitive tasks (like PO generation at reorder points) so that supply chain managers can focus on judgment-intensive, higher-value activities like supplier strategy and demand planning.
