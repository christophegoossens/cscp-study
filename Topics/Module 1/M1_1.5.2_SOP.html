What is S&OP?
Sales and Operations Planning (S&OP): A process to develop tactical plans that provide management the ability to strategically direct its businesses to achieve competitive advantage on a continuous basis by integrating customer-focused marketing plans for new and existing products with the management of the supply chain. The process brings together all the plans for the business (sales, marketing, development, manufacturing, sourcing, and financial) into one integrated set of plans.
S&OP is a CROSS-FUNCTIONAL PROCESS — it requires participation from Sales, Marketing, Finance, Operations, and Supply Chain. Its output is a single, agreed, executable plan across the organization.

S&OP operates at the AGGREGATE level — product families, not individual SKUs. The horizon is typically 3–18 months.

The S&OP Process Steps
The standard S&OP process follows a monthly cycle with 5 key steps:
  • Step 1 — Data gathering: Collect actuals vs. last month's plan (sales performance, inventory, production). Update the statistical forecast with latest actuals.
  • Step 2 — Demand review: Sales and marketing review and adjust the statistical forecast. Add intelligence: planned promotions, new customers, lost accounts, market changes. Output: one unconstrained demand plan.
  • Step 3 — Supply review: Operations reviews capacity against the demand plan. Identifies constraints. Output: constrained supply plan and gap analysis.
  • Step 4 — Pre-S&OP / reconciliation meeting: Finance and management review demand vs. supply gaps. Options to close gaps are evaluated (increase capacity, reduce demand, accept the gap). Financial implications are quantified.
  • Step 5 — Executive S&OP: Senior leadership reviews and approves the reconciled plan. Sets the 'one number' that all functions will work to.
The output of S&OP is the MASTER PRODUCTION SCHEDULE (MPS) input — an approved, balanced plan of what will be produced, sourced, and sold over the planning horizon.
S&OP Benefits and Success Factors
Benefits of S&OP: reduced inventory (better alignment), improved service levels, better capacity utilization, one plan across the business (eliminates shadow forecasting), improved financial predictability.
  • Executive sponsorship: S&OP must be driven from the top — it requires cross-functional decision-making authority
  • One number discipline: All functions must commit to the agreed plan — no 'private' forecasts
  • Regular cadence: Monthly cycle with fixed meeting dates and agenda
  • Data quality: Garbage in, garbage out — S&OP is only as good as the underlying data
  • Pre-work: Each functional review must be completed before the executive meeting
S&OP vs. IBP (Integrated Business Planning)
Integrated Business Planning (IBP): An evolution of S&OP that extends the integration to include financial planning, strategic planning, and portfolio management. IBP typically has a longer planning horizon (24–36 months) and involves more senior leadership.
IBP → S&OP → Master Scheduling is the planning hierarchy. IBP sets the strategic direction, S&OP translates it into tactical plans, Master Scheduling makes it operational.

The ASCM exam uses S&OP as the primary term — IBP is a more advanced evolution of the concept.

ASCM definition of Sales and Operations Planning (S&OP)

What are the 5 steps of the S&OP process?
ANSWER
1) Data gathering (actuals vs. plan) 2) Demand review (adjust forecast with intelligence) 3) Supply review (capacity vs. demand, gap analysis) 4) Pre-S&OP / reconciliation (gap options, financial impact) 5) Executive S&OP (approve the one agreed plan)
TERM / CONCEPT
At what level does S&OP operate?
ANSWER
AGGREGATE level — product families (not individual SKUs). Planning horizon: typically 3–18 months. The output feeds into Master Production Scheduling, which operates at the individual item level.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the 'one number' principle in S&OP?
ANSWER
All functions must commit to the same agreed plan — no parallel 'shadow forecasts.' Sales, operations, finance, and supply chain all work from a single number. Eliminates the dysfunction of different departments planning to different numbers.
TERM / CONCEPT
S&OP vs. Integrated Business Planning (IBP)
ANSWER
S&OP: tactical, 3–18 month horizon, product family level, monthly cycle. IBP: extends S&OP to include financial and strategic planning, 24–36 month horizon, senior leadership, includes portfolio management. IBP is the evolved/advanced version.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the output of the S&OP process?
ANSWER
A single, approved, balanced plan for what will be produced, sourced, and sold — used as the primary input to Master Production Scheduling (MPS). Also provides a financial forecast for budget management.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the 5 key success factors for S&OP?
ANSWER
1) Executive sponsorship (cross-functional authority) 2) One number discipline (no shadow forecasts) 3) Regular monthly cadence (fixed agenda) 4) Data quality (accurate inputs) 5) Completed functional pre-work before executive meeting
TERM / CONCEPT
What happens in the Supply Review step of S&OP?
ANSWER
Operations reviews available capacity (labor, machines, materials) against the unconstrained demand plan from the demand review. Identifies capacity gaps and constraints. Output: constrained supply plan + list of gaps to resolve.

Q1 of 5
What is the primary OUTPUT of the S&OP process?
S&OP produces ONE agreed plan across all functions — demand, supply, and financial — at the product family/aggregate level. This plan feeds into Master Production Scheduling, which then translates it to the individual item/SKU level.
Q2 of 5
During the S&OP process, the supply review identifies a capacity constraint — the company cannot produce enough to meet the demand plan. Who is responsible for resolving this gap?
Gaps identified in the supply review are escalated to the pre-S&OP and executive S&OP steps, where senior leadership evaluates options (add capacity, reduce demand via pricing/promotion, accept lower service, etc.) and makes the business decision. S&OP is a decision-making process, not a rubber stamp.
Q3 of 5
A sales manager continues to use his own private demand forecast rather than the agreed S&OP number when placing orders with the supply chain. This violates which S&OP principle?
'One number discipline' — the cornerstone of S&OP effectiveness — means ALL functions commit to the SAME agreed plan. Shadow forecasts create the very demand/supply misalignment S&OP is designed to eliminate.
Q4 of 5
S&OP planning typically operates at which level of aggregation?
S&OP operates at the AGGREGATE / product family level (not individual SKUs). This makes the planning more accurate (aggregation reduces error) and manageable in the meeting format. Individual SKU-level planning happens in Master Scheduling.
Q5 of 5
How does Integrated Business Planning (IBP) differ from traditional S&OP?
IBP is an evolution of S&OP that extends the scope to include financial planning, strategic portfolio management, and a longer time horizon (24–36 months). S&OP = tactical (3–18 months). IBP = strategic + tactical, broader business integration.
