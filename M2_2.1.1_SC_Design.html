What is Supply Chain Design?
Supply chain design (ASCM): The determination of how to structure a supply chain. Design decisions include the selection of partners, the location and capacity of warehouses and production facilities, the products, the suppliers, and the information systems.

Supply chain design translates organizational strategy into supply chain structure. It must reflect customer needs AND trading partners' capacities.

Design decisions are long-term and capital-intensive — they set the framework within which tactical and operational decisions are made. Getting design right is foundational.

After business strategy is set, the organization develops: (1) organizational strategy (how to structure the business), then (2) supply chain strategy (how the SC will support the business strategy).

Key Supply Chain Design Decisions
The 5 key supply chain design decisions:
  • Partner selection: Which suppliers, logistics providers, and distributors to work with
  • Facility location and capacity: Where to locate plants, warehouses, and DCs — and how large they should be
  • Product allocation: Which products are made/stored at which facilities
  • Information systems: What technology architecture will connect the network
  • Data governance: How frequently data is transferred, cleansed, and analyzed

Supply chain design should balance: cost efficiency, responsiveness to customers, resilience to disruption.

Technology is now a critical design input — it extends visibility across multiple tiers, captures data, and enables real-time decision-making.

Strategy Alignment
Supply chain strategy: A plan that shapes the long-term structure of the supply chain to support the organization's business strategy and competitive position.
There is no universally correct supply chain design — the right design depends on the competitive environment, customer requirements, product characteristics, and the organization's capabilities.

Common strategic tradeoffs in supply chain design: efficiency vs. responsiveness, centralization vs. decentralization of inventory, in-house vs. outsourced capabilities.

ASCM definition: Supply chain design
ANSWER
The determination of how to structure a supply chain. Decisions include: partner selection, facility location & capacity, product allocation, information systems, and data governance.
TERM / CONCEPT
What 5 decisions does supply chain design address?
ANSWER
1) Partner selection (suppliers, 3PLs, distributors) 2) Facility location and capacity 3) Product allocation across facilities 4) Information systems architecture 5) Data governance (transfer frequency, cleansing, use)
TERM / CONCEPT
Why is supply chain design considered long-term and strategic?
ANSWER
Design decisions (facility locations, partner contracts, technology platforms) require major capital investment and take years to change. They set the framework within which all tactical and operational decisions are made.
TERM / CONCEPT
How does supply chain strategy relate to business strategy?
ANSWER
Business strategy sets how the company competes. Organizational strategy structures the business. Supply chain strategy translates both into a supply chain structure that delivers the required cost, service, and flexibility.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the 3 key tradeoffs in supply chain design?
ANSWER
1) Efficiency vs. responsiveness 2) Centralization vs. decentralization of inventory/facilities 3) In-house vs. outsourced capabilities
TERM / CONCEPT
Why is there no universally correct supply chain design?
ANSWER
Because the right design depends on: competitive environment, customer service requirements, product characteristics (volume, variability, perishability), and the company's own capabilities and cost structure.

Q4 of 5
Why must supply chain design reflect customer needs?
The purpose of a supply chain is to deliver value to customers. If the design doesn't match customer service requirements (e.g., delivery speed, product availability), the chain cannot compete regardless of its internal efficiency.
Q5 of 5
What is the relationship between business strategy and supply chain strategy?
Strategy cascade: business strategy (how to compete) → organizational strategy (how to structure) → supply chain strategy (how to configure the SC). Misalignment between business and SC strategy creates a chain that cannot deliver on the company's competitive promise.
