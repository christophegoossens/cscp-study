SC Technology Road Map

A comprehensive SC technology system covers three areas: process value chain (translates demand into production/fulfilment), value delivery chain (delivers the service component to end customers), and demand channel (collects, analyzes, and disseminates market information).

Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA): a system architecture design in which related tasks are grouped in self-contained packages. Each package can be used by other software applications.

ERP Systems
Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP): a framework for organizing, defining, and standardizing the business processes necessary to effectively plan and control an organization so the organization can use its internal knowledge to seek external advantage.
  • Modularized suite of integrated business applications sharing a single central database.
  • Key database files: customer, product/price, supplier, open orders, purchase orders (POs), BOM, inventory, order/PO history.
  • ERP vs. Best-of-Breed: single vendor = simpler integration, easier upgrades; best-of-breed = best functionality per module but complex integration.
  • Advanced ERP extensions: e-procurement, CRM, APS (Advanced Planning and Scheduling), WMS (Warehouse Management System).
Exhibit 2-8: ERP System Functionality
Advanced Planning & Scheduling (APS)
Advanced Planning and Scheduling (APS): techniques that deal with analysis and planning of logistics and manufacturing over the short, intermediate, and long-term time periods. APS describes computer programs that use advanced mathematical algorithms or logic to optimize finite capacity scheduling, sourcing, capital planning, resource planning, forecasting, demand management, and others.
  • APS goes beyond ERP — while ERP tracks what happened, APS optimizes what should happen using constraints and objectives.
  • Covers: demand planning, supply planning, production scheduling, distribution planning, transportation planning.

TERM / CONCEPT
What is the key feature of an ERP system?
ANSWER
A shared central database. All modules (sales, finance, production, procurement, inventory) use the same data store, eliminating data silos and manual re-keying.
TERM / CONCEPT
What master data files does an ERP database contain?
ANSWER
Customer files, product/price files, supplier files, open order files, purchase order files, BOM files, inventory files, and order/PO history files.
TERM / CONCEPT
ERP single-vendor vs. best-of-breed: key trade-offs?
ANSWER
Single-vendor: simpler integration, easier upgrades, one support contact. Best-of-breed: best functionality in each category but complex, costly integration between modules from different vendors.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the difference between ERP and APS?
ANSWER
ERP records and manages transactions (what happened). APS uses optimization algorithms to plan what SHOULD happen — factoring in constraints like capacity, materials, and lead times.
TERM / CONCEPT
What does APS cover?
ANSWER
Demand planning, supply planning, production/finite capacity scheduling, distribution planning, and transportation planning. Goes beyond ERP in using optimization logic.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the 3 parts of a comprehensive SC technology system?
ANSWER
1. Process value chain (translates demand into production/fulfillment). 2. Value delivery chain (delivers service component to customers). 3. Demand channel (collects, analyzes, and disseminates market information).
TERM / CONCEPT
What does an ERP transactional module do?
ANSWER
Handles all user interactions with the system: placing orders, receiving goods, recording payments, managing inventory transactions. Each module supports one business function (sales, finance, production, etc.).
TERM / CONCEPT
What does an ERP planning module do?
ANSWER
Gathers input data and analysis to support tactical and operational planning — e.g., production planning, procurement planning, capacity planning. Feeds decisions back into transactional modules.
TERM / CONCEPT
Why do even older ERP versions add value?
ANSWER
They automate processes (reducing manual errors), increase efficiency, and provide a shared data foundation. Even basic ERP eliminates duplicate data entry and improves information flow.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are advanced ERP extensions?
ANSWER
Add-on capabilities that go beyond core ERP: CRM (customer relationship management), e-procurement, APS (planning optimization), WMS (warehouse management), TMS (transportation management).
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the demand channel in a SC technology system?
ANSWER
The IT layer that collects market/customer demand information, analyzes it, and disseminates it to the process value chain so production and supply plans can be aligned to actual market demand.
TERM / CONCEPT
How does SOA benefit SC technology architecture?
ANSWER
By packaging tasks into self-contained, reusable modules, SOA allows different applications to call on the same functionality without rebuilding it. This makes integration between systems easier and more flexible.
TERM / CONCEPT
What capability do advanced ERP versions enable?
ANSWER
Build-to-order/direct-to-customer models, lean manufacturing, collaborative planning with partners, global data access for all SC partners, linking management pay to SC performance metrics.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is a WMS (Warehouse Management System)?
ANSWER
Software that optimizes and manages warehouse operations — receiving, putaway, picking, packing, and shipping. Often integrated with ERP and TMS.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is e-procurement?
ANSWER
Using internet/electronic means to purchase goods and services. Automates the procurement process (RFQs, purchase orders, supplier catalogs) to reduce cost and cycle time.
TERM / CONCEPT
Why is ERP integration with APS important?
ANSWER
ERP provides the master data (BOM, inventory, demand) that APS needs to optimize plans. APS outputs feed back into ERP for execution. Without integration, planning and execution are disconnected.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is finite capacity scheduling?
ANSWER
A scheduling approach that respects actual resource constraints (machine hours, labor) when planning production — unlike infinite capacity scheduling which ignores constraints. APS typically uses finite capacity scheduling.

Question 2
What is a Warehouse Management System (WMS)?
A.A system that manages transportation contracts
B.Software that controls and optimizes warehouse operations — receiving, put-away, picking, packing, and shipping — using real-time inventory location data
C.An ERP module for tracking fixed assets
D.A system for managing supplier relationships
A WMS directs all warehouse activities, tracks inventory by location, optimizes pick paths, manages labor, and provides real-time inventory visibility — significantly improving warehouse accuracy and productivity.
Question 3
What is a Transportation Management System (TMS)?
A.A system for managing supply chain risk
B.Software that plans, executes, and optimizes the physical movement of goods — including carrier selection, route optimization, load planning, freight bill audit, and shipment tracking
C.A system for managing import/export documentation
D.A WMS with transportation capabilities added
A TMS optimizes transportation planning and execution — carrier selection, load building, route optimization, carrier tendering, shipment tracking, and freight audit. It reduces transportation cost and improves service.
Question 4
What is EDI (Electronic Data Interchange)?
A.A type of warehouse management technology
B.The electronic exchange of structured business documents (orders, invoices, ASNs) between trading partners in standardized formats (e.g., ANSI X12, EDIFACT)
C.An inventory counting method
D.A type of demand forecasting algorithm
EDI is the computer-to-computer exchange of business documents in standardized electronic formats, replacing paper documents (purchase orders, invoices, advance ship notices) and enabling automated processing.
Question 5
What does RFID stand for, and what is its supply chain application?
A.Real-time Freight Identification — a carrier tracking system
B.Radio-Frequency Identification — wireless technology using tags and readers to automatically identify and track goods without line-of-sight, enabling real-time inventory visibility
C.Remote Freight Inspection Device — used at customs checkpoints
D.Real-time Financial Integration Data — a finance system
RFID uses radio waves to read tags attached to items, cases, or pallets — enabling automatic, real-time inventory tracking without manual scanning. It improves receiving speed, inventory accuracy, and loss prevention.
Question 6
What is a Supplier Portal and what are its benefits?
A.A website where customers place orders
B.A web-based platform where suppliers can view purchase orders, update delivery status, submit invoices, and access performance data — improving communication and reducing manual processing
C.A system for managing customs and import documentation
D.A portal where supply chain managers post job openings
Supplier portals give suppliers self-service access to purchase orders, delivery requirements, and performance data. They reduce phone and email communication, accelerate invoice processing, and improve supplier compliance.
Question 7
What is VMI (Vendor-Managed Inventory)?
A.A system where customers set their own inventory levels
B.An arrangement where the supplier monitors the customer's inventory and replenishes it when it falls below a specified level — transferring inventory management responsibility to the supplier
C.A financial arrangement where vendors provide inventory on consignment
D.A system where all inventory decisions are made by the warehouse manager
In VMI, the supplier monitors customer inventory (via POS or EDI feeds) and takes responsibility for maintaining agreed service levels — typically reducing stock-outs and inventory for both parties.
Question 8
What is the role of a Supply Chain Control Tower?
A.Physical observation tower installed in distribution centers
B.A centralized platform that provides end-to-end supply chain visibility, alerts exceptions, and enables rapid response — integrating data from multiple systems (ERP, TMS, WMS, suppliers)
C.A management reporting tool for quarterly financial reviews
D.A software system for route optimization only
A supply chain control tower aggregates data from multiple systems into a single visibility platform, detects exceptions (delays, stock-outs, capacity issues), and enables faster, better-informed decisions.
Question 9
What is the key business benefit of real-time track-and-trace technology?
A.It eliminates the need for safety stock
B.It provides customers and supply chain managers with up-to-the-minute shipment location data — enabling proactive exception management and improved customer communication
C.It automates customs clearance at all borders
D.It replaces the need for a TMS
Real-time track-and-trace enables proactive exception management — detecting delays before they impact customers and enabling rerouting or early notification. It also improves customer experience through accurate delivery visibility.
Question 10
Which supply chain technology enables automatic replenishment without manual order creation?
A.ERP financial accounting module
B.VMI (Vendor Managed Inventory) or automatic replenishment systems that trigger orders when inventory drops to the reorder point without human intervention
C.Manual phone orders to suppliers
D.Spreadsheet-based inventory tracking
Automatic replenishment (via VMI, EDI order triggers, or CPFR) removes the need for manual order creation — reducing order processing cost, minimizing stock-outs, and enabling smoother demand signals to suppliers.
Question 11
What is the purpose of an Advance Ship Notice (ASN)?
A.To notify the customer of a price increase before shipment
B.An electronic document sent by the supplier before shipment arrives, containing detailed shipment contents, quantities, and expected delivery time — enabling the receiver to prepare and automate receiving
C.A customs document for international shipments
D.A notification to the carrier about a pickup request
The ASN (or ASN/856 in EDI) allows the receiver to pre-create receiving records, allocate resources, and update inventory in advance — dramatically reducing receiving dock time and improving inventory accuracy.
Question 12
What does 'cloud-based' supply chain technology mean for implementation?
A.The system is hosted on servers in the sky — literally
B.The software is hosted and maintained by the vendor on shared or dedicated cloud infrastructure, accessed via internet — typically faster to deploy, lower upfront cost, but with ongoing subscription fees
C.The system can only be accessed when internet is available, making it unsuitable for manufacturing environments
D.All data is stored on the user's local computer
Cloud-based (SaaS) supply chain systems are vendor-hosted, subscription-based, and accessible via internet. They eliminate on-premise server costs, speed deployment, and shift maintenance to the vendor.
