Master Data & MDM

Master data are static reference files (customer, supplier, product/SKU, BOM, inventory) that support all transactions. Master Data Management (MDM) is the discipline that coordinates the consistent use of master data across all systems and partners.

  • Types of SC master data: customer/supplier files, product/SKU files, BOM and standard costs, PO data, order history, inventory data, logistics/shipment data, engineering data.
  • Master data life cycle: Plan → Create & Process → Analyze/Use/Transfer → Maintain & Store → Archive → Destroy or Repurpose.
  • Effectivity date: the date on which a new engineering specification replaces an old one. Critical for BOM accuracy.

Data Capture Methods & Challenges
Automatic Identification System (AIS): a system that can encode product data and enter it automatically into a computer system. Used for tracking shipments, inventory, and WIP.
  • Capture at source: best practice — avoids transcription errors from manual re-entry.
  • Passive/automatic capture (RFID, barcode scanners) is more productive than manual entry.
  • Real-time vs. batch: real-time is 10x+ more expensive; use batch where real-time isn’t required.
  • Big data risk: too much data slows systems and reduces focus — collect what adds value.
  • Challenges: fast-paced environments (best solution: passive/automated capture), hostile environments (special scanning equipment), language/literacy barriers (picture-based interfaces).

Barcode, RFID & Decision Support
  • 1D barcode (UPC): encodes product data in parallel lines. Requires line-of-sight scan. UPC-A is the standard 12-digit US barcode.
  • 2D barcode (QR code): stores more data than 1D. Can encode URLs, text, serial numbers. Scanned by camera.
  • RFID (Radio Frequency Identification): uses radio waves to identify tagged items. Does NOT require line of sight — can scan multiple items simultaneously. More expensive than barcodes but enables bulk scanning and tracking in motion.
  • Decision Support System (DSS): uses master data and analytics to help managers make decisions (e.g., inventory reorder, network optimization, demand sensing).

What is Master Data Management (MDM)?
ANSWER
A discipline that works alongside IT to coordinate the consistent definition, governance, and use of master data across all systems, departments, and supply chain partners.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the 6 stages of the master data life cycle?
ANSWER
1. Plan. 2. Create & Process. 3. Analyze / Use / Transfer. 4. Maintain & Store. 5. Archive. 6. Destroy or Repurpose.
TERM / CONCEPT
What types of master data are important in SC management?
ANSWER
Customer/supplier files, product/SKU files, BOMs and standard costs, PO data for spend analysis, order history for demand analysis, inventory data, logistics/shipment data, engineering data.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is an effectivity date?
ANSWER
The date on which a new engineering specification (e.g., revised BOM or component) replaces the old one. Critical for ensuring the correct version of a BOM is used in production.
TERM / CONCEPT
ASCM def: Automatic Identification System (AIS)
ANSWER
A system that can encode product data and enter it automatically into a computer system. Used for tracking shipments, inventory, and work-in-process.
TERM / CONCEPT
What are the two key features of AIDC devices?
ANSWER
1. Automatic classification (categorizing the item). 2. Automatic identification (identifying the specific item/batch). Together they eliminate manual scanning and data entry errors.
TERM / CONCEPT
Why is capturing data at the source best practice?
ANSWER
It avoids transcription errors from manual re-entry. Data captured at origin is more accurate and timely than data entered later from paper records or memory.
TERM / CONCEPT
Real-time vs. batch data capture: key trade-off?
ANSWER
Real-time is many times more expensive to engineer. Use real-time only when the business truly needs it (e.g., live stock visibility). Batch is cheaper and sufficient for many non-time-critical processes.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the big data risk?
ANSWER
Organizations can be overwhelmed by too much data — more data = slower systems, harder analysis, and less focus. Collect only data that adds value to decisions.
TERM / CONCEPT
How to handle fast-paced data capture environments?
ANSWER
Use passive/automatic capture (barcodes, RFID) rather than manual entry. Management is often resistant to steps that slow the process — automated capture adds speed AND accuracy.
TERM / CONCEPT
How to handle language/literacy barriers in data capture?
ANSWER
Use picture-based interfaces, color coding, or voice-guided systems. Reduces dependence on literacy or language proficiency for data input.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is a 1D barcode (UPC)?
ANSWER
A barcode that encodes data in parallel lines of varying widths. Requires direct line-of-sight scanning. UPC-A is the standard 12-digit US consumer product barcode.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is a 2D barcode (QR code)?
ANSWER
A barcode that encodes data in a grid pattern. Can store much more data than 1D (URLs, text, serial numbers). Scanned by camera — no line-of-sight laser required.
TERM / CONCEPT
RFID vs. barcode: key differences?
ANSWER
RFID: uses radio waves, no line-of-sight needed, multiple items scanned simultaneously, items can move at speed, higher cost. Barcode: line-of-sight required, one item at a time, cheaper.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the global identification requirement for AIDC?
ANSWER
Identifiers for objects must be unique globally so the internet will yield only one result. This is the basis for GS1 standards (GTIN, EPC) used in global trade.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is a Decision Support System (DSS)?
ANSWER
An information system that uses master data and analytics to help managers make decisions. Provides analysis, modeling, and scenario tools — not just data reporting.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the maintain and store phase of the master data life cycle?
ANSWER
Ensuring data security in transit and at rest, cleansing data (removing duplicates, correcting errors), and keeping master data current and accurate for ongoing transactions.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the archive phase of the master data life cycle?
ANSWER
Moving data that is no longer needed for current transactions to long-term storage (archive). Retained for legal, audit, or historical analysis purposes.
TERM / CONCEPT
What is the destroy/repurpose phase of master data life cycle?
ANSWER
Deleting data no longer needed (for legal or system performance reasons) or repurposing it for new uses. Must comply with data privacy laws (e.g., GDPR).
TERM / CONCEPT
Why do barcodes and RFID reduce stockouts?
ANSWER
Automated, accurate real-time inventory tracking eliminates the errors (missed scans, wrong counts) that lead to phantom inventory and stockouts — especially critical for perishables and high-velocity items.

Question 1
What is 'master data' in a supply chain context?
A.Historical transaction records such as past invoices
B.The core reference data shared across systems and processes — including item master, customer master, supplier master, and BOM data — that remains relatively stable over time
C.Daily operational data like order quantities and shipment dates
D.Employee payroll data
Master data is the stable, core reference data that multiple systems and processes rely on — items, customers, suppliers, pricing, BOMs, locations. Incorrect master data corrupts every transaction that uses it.
Question 2
What is the cost of poor master data quality in a supply chain?
A.No significant cost — data quality issues are minor operational problems
B.Poor master data leads to incorrect orders, inventory errors, billing errors, compliance failures, and poor decision-making — creating significant operational cost and customer service failures
C.Only IT systems are affected by data quality issues
D.Poor data quality only affects financial reporting
Bad master data (wrong lead times, incorrect units of measure, stale supplier information) corrupts every planning decision and transaction that uses it — causing ordering errors, inventory mismatches, billing disputes, and poor forecasts.
Question 3
What is a 'single source of truth' for master data?
A.Storing all data on a single server
B.Having one authoritative, validated version of each master data record that all systems and users reference — eliminating conflicting or duplicated data across systems
C.Using only one ERP system in the company
D.A data backup system that stores one copy of all records
A single source of truth means all systems read master data from one authoritative source (e.g., a Master Data Management system or the ERP) — eliminating the problems caused by different systems having different versions of the same data.
Question 4
What is an 'item master' and what data does it typically contain?
A.A list of all purchase orders placed in a year
B.The central record for each product that defines it — including description, unit of measure, weight, dimensions, lead times, ABC classification, and other attributes used by planning, purchasing, and logistics systems
C.A record of all items returned by customers
D.The inventory count for each item in the warehouse
The item master is the foundation of supply chain planning — it contains all attributes of each product (description, UoM, lead time, weight, classification) that are referenced by every transaction and planning process.
Question 5
What is the 'bill of materials' (BOM) and why must it be accurate?
A.A list of all purchase orders for a supplier
B.A structured list of all components, quantities, and assemblies required to produce one unit of a finished product — inaccurate BOMs cause incorrect material requirements calculations and production failures
C.A financial document listing all product costs
D.A shipping manifest listing items in a shipment
The BOM defines exactly what goes into a product. MRP uses the BOM to calculate component requirements — an error in the BOM (wrong quantity, missing component) directly causes incorrect purchasing, production failures, and quality issues.
Question 6
What does 'data governance' mean?
A.Governing access to company buildings
B.The framework of policies, processes, roles, and accountabilities for managing data quality, security, and lifecycle — determining who can create, modify, and use data, and what standards apply
C.The government's regulation of data storage requirements
D.A system for managing customer data for marketing
Data governance establishes ownership (who is responsible for each data element), standards (what format and quality is required), and processes (how data is created, maintained, and retired) — the organizational foundation for data quality.
Question 7
Which of the following is a common symptom of poor master data management?
A.Supply chain systems processing orders too quickly
B.Multiple different descriptions for the same product across different systems, leading to inability to aggregate spend or inventory across the enterprise
C.Too much automation in the ordering process
D.Supplier invoices being paid too early
Duplicate or inconsistent item records across systems prevent spend consolidation (same item = multiple codes = can't compare supplier prices), accurate inventory reporting, and demand aggregation.
Question 8
What is an MDM (Master Data Management) system?
A.A type of warehouse management system
B.A platform that centralizes, validates, and governs master data across the enterprise — providing a single authoritative source of data that all other systems synchronize with
C.A manufacturing data monitoring system
D.A marketing data management platform for customer analytics
An MDM system serves as the golden record for key reference data (items, customers, suppliers), with workflows for data creation and approval, quality validation, and synchronization to all consuming systems.
Question 9
Why is master data management particularly challenging in a post-merger or multi-site environment?
A.Different sites have different weather, making data entry more difficult
B.Different legacy systems, local conventions, and item numbering schemes create many duplicate and conflicting records that must be rationalized and harmonized into a single consistent dataset
C.IT systems from different companies are automatically compatible
D.Master data issues don't arise in multi-site environments
Mergers and multi-site operations typically bring incompatible item numbers, customer codes, and supplier records from different legacy systems — rationalization requires significant effort to identify and merge duplicates.
Question 10
What is a 'global data synchronization network' (GDSN)?
A.A satellite network used for tracking shipments globally
B.A standardized global network (using GS1 standards) for sharing product master data between trading partners — enabling consistent, synchronized product information across retailers and suppliers
C.A global internet network for supply chain visibility
D.A currency exchange network for international trade
GDSN (e.g., 1WorldSync) enables suppliers and retailers to synchronize product master data using GS1 standards — ensuring both parties have consistent product attributes (dimensions, descriptions, hazmat info) without manual re-entry.
Question 11
What role do data stewards play in master data management?
A.Physical security of data servers
B.Business subject matter experts responsible for the quality, accuracy, and maintenance of specific master data domains — they review, approve, and correct data as part of the governance process
C.IT engineers who maintain the database software
D.Auditors who review financial data
Data stewards are business process owners for specific data domains (e.g., item master steward owns product data quality). They validate new records, resolve data quality issues, and maintain standards — not IT, who maintain the systems.
Question 12
What is 'data cleansing' in a master data context?
A.Physically cleaning computer hardware
B.The process of identifying and correcting inaccurate, incomplete, duplicate, or outdated master data records — typically done before a new system implementation or periodically as part of data quality management
C.Deleting all data older than one year
D.Encrypting sensitive data for security
Data cleansing identifies and corrects problems in master data — duplicates, wrong values, missing required fields, outdated information — ensuring the data is fit for use in supply chain systems and planning.
